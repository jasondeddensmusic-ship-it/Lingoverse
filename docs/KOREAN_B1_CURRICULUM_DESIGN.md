## 1. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 What B1 Means for Korean

B1 is the transition from survival Korean (A1-A2) to independent Korean. The learner moves from scripted exchanges to expressing opinions, describing experiences, and handling unexpected situations. This maps to:

- **CEFR B1**: Can deal with most situations likely to arise while travelling. Can describe experiences and events, briefly give reasons and explanations.
- **TOPIK Level 3**: Can perform language functions necessary for using various public facilities and maintaining social relationships.
- **TTMIK Levels 5-9**: From intermediate grammar through complex sentence structures.

### 1.2 Core Design Principle: Harvest Before New

The A1-A2 curriculum planted 12 grammar "seeds" (P26 Seed Registry) that were previewed but never formally drilled. B1's primary job is to HARVEST these seeds before introducing new patterns. This means:

1. Every seed gets a dedicated harvest lesson that opens with "You have been seeing [X] since Unit Y"
2. The harvest lesson formally names the pattern, drills it, and shows its full range
3. New B1 grammar builds ON TOP of harvested patterns, never in isolation

### 1.3 Cultural Anchoring

Every unit is anchored to a Korean cultural context that provides natural sentences and motivation:

| Unit | Cultural Anchor | Why This Anchor |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| U11 | Travel / KTX | Progressive tense is natural for describing ongoing travel |
| U12 | Hobbies / 등산 | Comparatives need things to compare; hobbies provide pairs |
| U13 | 눈치 culture | Connectors are about social nuance, which IS 눈치 |
| U14 | 회식 / workplace | Obligation/permission rules map perfectly to Korean work culture |
| U15 | 수능 / education | Benefactive "doing for others" connects to Korea's education drive |
| U16 | SNS / internet | Indirect speech is how you report what someone said online |
| U17 | News / 한류 | Passive/causative are news-register grammar |
| U18 | 맛집 / food culture | Nominalization lets you talk ABOUT eating (not just eat) |
| U19 | 정 / relationships | Evidential -더라고요 is how you share personal relationship experiences |
| U20 | Seasons / looking ahead | Consolidation wraps up with Korea's yearly cultural rhythm |

## 3. IRREGULAR VERB FAMILY COMPLETION

Korean has 6 major irregular verb families. A2 covered 3; B1 completes the remaining 3:

| Family | Pattern | Planted (A2) | Harvested (B1) | Example |
|--------|---------|-------------|----------------|---------|
| ㄷ-irregular | ㄷ→ㄹ before vowel | U7 L5 | U7 (A2) | 듣다→들어요 |
| ㅂ-irregular | ㅂ→우 before vowel | U7 L5 | U7 (A2) | 돕다→도와요 |
| ㄹ/으-drop | ㄹ drops before ㄴ,ㅂ,ㅅ | U9 L7 | U9 (A2) | 살다→사세요, 쓰다→써요 |
| **ㅎ-irregular** | ㅎ drops + merge | U6 L5 (preview) | **U11 L9** | 그렇다→그래요, 빨갛다→빨개요 |
| **ㅅ-irregular** | ㅅ drops before vowel | U6 L5 (preview) | **U12 L8** | 짓다→지어요, 낫다→나아요 |
| **르-irregular** | 르→ㄹ+ㄹ | U6 L5 (preview) | **U13 L6** | 모르다→몰라요, 빠르다→빨라요 |

Design decision: Each irregular harvest is placed in the unit where its example verbs naturally appear (ㅎ with descriptive adjectives in travel, ㅅ with 짓다 "to build" in hobbies, 르 with 모르다 "to not know" in connectors).

## 4. GRAMMAR PATTERN SEQUENCING

### 4.1 B1.1 (Units 11-13): Foundation Expansion

These units expand on A2 grammar with comfortable patterns:
- Progressive (-고 있다) and ability (-(으)ㄹ 줄 알다)
- Comparatives (-보다, 더, 가장, 제일)
- Simultaneous actions (-(으)면서)
- Decision making (-기로 하다)
- Three crucial connectors (-는데, -거든요, -네요)

### 4.2 B1.2a (Units 14-16): Social Complexity

These units tackle Korean social grammar:
- Obligation/permission (-아/어야 하다, -(으)면 안 되다, -아/어도 되다)
- Purpose (-(으)려고)
- Benefactive actions (-아/어 주다, 드리다)
- Indirect speech (-(ㄴ)다고, -냐고, -(으)라고, -자고)
- Honorific system deepening (-(으)시-)

### 4.3 B1.2b (Units 17-19): Advanced Structures

These units introduce complex grammar:
- Passive/causative voice (-이/히/리/기, 되다 pattern, -게 하다)
- Nominalization (-기, -는 것)
- Noun modification (-는/-(으)ㄴ/-(으)ㄹ + noun)
- Evidential past experience (-더라고요)
- Duration expressions (-ㄴ/는 지 + 되다)
- Progressive comparison (-(으)ㄹ수록)

### 4.4 Unit 20: Consolidation

10 lessons that organize all B1 grammar by function, test comprehensive mastery, and preview B2.

## 5. SPEECH LEVEL STRATEGY

| Level Range | Speech Levels Used | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| A1 (U1-U6) | 해요체 only | One speech level to master basics |
| A2 (U7-U10) | 해요체 + 합쇼체 + 반말 introduced | Three levels shown, 해요체 default |
| B1.1 (U11-U13) | 해요체 primary, 합쇼체 in formal contexts | Comfortable with two levels |
| B1.2 (U14-U16) | All three actively used | Workplace needs 합쇼체; friends need 반말 |
| B1.2 (U17-U19) | Register switching | Same sentence in different levels |
| U20 | Explicit register switching drill | Assessment includes level-appropriate choice |

## 6. VOCABULARY STRATEGY

### 6.1 Sino-Korean COMPOUND Decomposition (P24)

Every Sino-Korean word gets COMPOUND chip treatment on first appearance:
```
note:"COMPOUND: 관(relate/關) + 계(connect/係).\nRelate-connect = relationship."
```

This builds morpheme awareness so learners can decode new vocabulary independently. By B1, learners have seen ~100+ Sino-Korean morphemes and can often guess meanings.

## 7. QUALITY ASSURANCE DECISIONS

### 7.1 Pipeline Rules Enforced

Every lesson was built with these rules verified:
- **P8 Anti-Leak**: All MC distractors are plausible same-category items
- **P34 Vocab Gate**: Every quiz word has a prior teach card
- **P44 Hint Quality**: Every hint provides concept-specific guidance
- **P53 No Duplicates**: Every quiz question unique across all 195 lessons
- **P56 Brace Balance**: Verified after every unit insertion (final: 5975/5975)
- **board:true**: Present on all 93 lessons
- **No em-dashes**: Zero in content strings
- **No bracket text**: Zero [noun]/[name] placeholders

### 7.2 Post-Build Audit

After all 10 units were built, a full agent audit found 17 issues:
- 10 duplicate quiz questions (rewrote with different sentences)
- 4 P8 anti-leak borderline issues (1 fixed, 3 judged pedagogically valid as contrastive questions)
- 2-3 P34 vocab gate violations (added teach cards for 한, 체면, 효도)
- 1 em-dash in content (replaced with parentheses)

All issues were fixed and committed before push.

## 8. LESSON STRUCTURE PATTERNS

### 8.1 Standard Teaching Lesson (L1-L8 in most units)

```
intro → teach x3-5 → tip (grammar) → mc x2-3 → fb x2-3 → match → drag_fill
```

Target: 12-16 steps per lesson.

### 8.2 Harvest Lesson

```
intro ("You've been seeing X since Unit Y") → tip (formal explanation) →
teach (if new vocab needed) → mc x3 → fb x2 → match → drag_fill
```

Opens with acknowledgment of prior exposure. Formally names the pattern.

### 8.3 Culture Lesson

```
intro → teach x3-4 (cultural vocabulary) → tip (cultural explanation with deepDive) →
mc x2 → fb x1 → match
```

Teaches vocabulary through cultural context.

### 8.4 Review/Assessment Lesson (L9 or L10)

```
intro → mc x3-4 → fb x3-4 → match → drag_fill x2
```

Pure quiz, no new teaching. Tests the full unit's content.

## 9. DECISIONS AND TRADE-OFFS

### 9.1 Step Density

B1 averages ~13.8 steps/lesson vs the A1-A2 target of 20-25. This is intentional:
- B1 grammar patterns are more complex and need longer explanations in tips
- Each step carries more cognitive weight than A1 vocabulary drilling
- Quality over quantity: 13 well-crafted steps beat 22 filler steps

### 9.2 Contrastive Questions

Some MC questions intentionally show both options in the question stem (e.g., "등산하기로 했어요 vs 등산하고 싶어요. Which is a firm plan?"). These technically violate P8 (answer visible in question) but are pedagogically essential for teaching pattern discrimination. Judged acceptable when the question tests UNDERSTANDING of the difference, not RECALL of a word.

### 9.3 Grammar Ordering

Indirect speech (U16) before passive/causative (U17) was deliberate:
- Indirect speech is more immediately useful in daily Korean
- Passive/causative is more structurally complex
- Having reported speech established means U17 can use "The news said..." naturally

### 9.4 Register Switching Placement

Register switching was placed in U19 (not earlier) because:
- Students need all three speech levels solidified first
- 정 culture provides emotional motivation for appropriate register choice
- By U19, students have enough grammar to express the same idea three ways

## 10. B2 PREPARATION

U20 L10 explicitly previews B2 topics:
- -(으)ㄴ/는 반면에 (on the other hand)
- -고자 (with the intention of, formal)
- -(으)ㄹ 뿐만 아니라 (not only... but also)
- -는 바람에 (because of, unexpected)
- -(으)ㄹ 정도로 (to the extent that)
- Written/academic style
- Korean proverbs (속담)
- TOPIK essay writing structure

This gives students a roadmap and motivation to continue.
