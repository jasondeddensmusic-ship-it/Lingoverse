## Notes

- Korean has the highest quiz count (6,074) and among the highest tip counts (332) of all audited languages. This reflects the grammatical complexity of Korean (agglutinative morphology requiring more construct-level instruction).
- The 69 units × 524 lessons architecture exceeds German (36 units, 558 lessons) in unit count, providing substantial recycling opportunities.
- Because Korean does not use inflection for tense/gender the way Romance languages do, many constructs appear as verb-ending suffixes. Teaching these requires dedicated tip explanations — and Korean delivers on this.
- Particle constructs (은/는, 이/가, 을/를, 에/에서) are covered primarily via teach cards rather than dedicated tips, which is appropriate since these are vocabulary-level items.
