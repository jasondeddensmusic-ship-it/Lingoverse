## Framework Notes

- Russian has 36 units (added 2026-04-17), mirroring German's structure. It is the newest language alongside Chinese and Portuguese.
- Russian's unique grammar architecture (6 cases, aspect pairs, verbs of motion) means that many communicative functions are embedded in grammar units rather than standalone scenario units. This is pedagogically defensible but creates PP58 mapping ambiguity.
- Past tense and future are formally at B1.1 (U13-15) for Russian, reflecting the aspect-based teaching approach. This defers A2 narrative functions to B1, which is acceptable for Russian's complexity level.
- The comparative adjectives gap (F4 at A2) is unique to Russian among the 9 languages — all other languages have a dedicated comparatives unit, Russian does not. Comparatives appear incidentally via adjective declension but never as a communicative function unit.
- B2 shows relatively strong coverage (77%) for a new language thanks to well-structured thematic units (society, science, business, arts, history, formal language).
- The Cyrillic script is handled via Foundations (not lesson units), consistent with Japanese and Chinese approach.
- Russian B1 case units (U16-21: prepositional, accusative, genitive, dative, instrumental) are grammar-mechanics units. They enable B1 communicative functions but are not themselves communicative function units per PP58 definition.
