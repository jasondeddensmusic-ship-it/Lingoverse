## Framework Notes

- Portuguese has exactly 36 units, mirroring German. Being one of the newest languages (added 2026-04-17), structural coverage is thinner at A1 compared to French and Spanish.
- Portuguese is the only language with a dedicated unit for the personal infinitive (U20), a grammatical structure unique to Portuguese with communicative implications for instruction-giving and reporting.
- Past tense (preterite) and future are deferred to B1.1 (U13-15), which means A2 functions F1 and F2 are technically covered only at B1 level. This is a structural concern: CEFR A2 expects basic past narration.
- The imperative is at B1.2 (U22), making instruction-giving (F3) very late by CEFR standards.
- Tell-time function at A1 (F11) is partially covered: U5 has time adverbs but no clock-reading lesson. This is a practical gap for CEFR A1 exams (PT certificates like CELPE-Bras).
- The pattern of missing complaints, admin, and personal-significance units is consistent with all 9 non-German languages.
