## Framework Notes

- Chinese has 45 units: U1-36 are the core grid (HSK 1-4 concept-driven); U37-45 are additional units added 2026-04-17 (A2.2 relationships/home life + B1.2/B2 professional/international).
- Chinese is the newest language (added 2026-04-17) and shows the most structural gaps. The ability expression (能/可以/会) is deferred to B1.2 (U18) — this is significantly late by CEFR standards where A2 should cover basic ability.
- Past tense concepts exist at A2 (via U14 at B1.1 label) — there is a sub-level alignment issue where some A2-functional content is labeled B1.
- The absence of a complaint unit (F8) and an administrative unit (F22) are consistent with the pattern seen across all non-German languages.
- Hanzi+pinyin notation (analogous to Japanese PP65) is fully implemented; this is architectural, not a PP58 issue.
- Chinese has good coverage of abstract/intellectual functions at B2 due to chengyu (U32) and philosophy (U30) units, outperforming some European languages at that level.
