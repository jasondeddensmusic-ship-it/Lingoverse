## Framework Notes

- Italian has exactly 36 units, directly mirroring the German template. This gives the best structural alignment with the reference mapping.
- Italian A1 is notably thin (only 6 units: U1-6) compared to German (6 units) and French (8 units at A1). The 6 Italian A1 units cover greetings, numbers/time, family, bar/cafe, home, city — leaving out daily routine and ability (can/cannot).
- Weather is absent across Italian A1-A2. This is the most consistent gap vs. German.
- B1 is the strongest level (95% coverage). The subjunctive mood (U13) and conditional (U21) give strong functional scaffolding for B1 communicative goals.
- U11-12 handle past tense at A2.2, U13+ handle subjunctive at B1.1 — this Italian tense progression closely tracks German's A2-B1 progression.
- Italian's 252 batch density files (`_batch*_u*_L*`) contain teach cards for individual lessons but do not add new communicative units — PP58 maps only to the 36 unit-level files.
