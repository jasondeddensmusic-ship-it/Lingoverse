## Methodology Notes

- Vocabulary extracted via `grep -hE 'trg:"[^"]+"' src/data/<lang>-v2/*.js`
- Chinese: hanzi stripped from `漢字(pinyin)` notation before matching
- Japanese: kanji stripped from `漢字(ふりがな)` notation before matching
- Matching: exact string match, then fuzzy match allowing ±3 characters for morphological endings
- Sample sizes per language: 110–308 words drawn from official CEFR-aligned thematic categories
- Sources: publicly available official exam frameworks (DELF, PCIC, CILS, NT2, TOPIK, JLPT, HSK, CELPE-Bras, ТРКИ)

## Key Findings

### 1. Mature Languages (5,000+ teach cards): Targeted B1/B2 Gaps

French, Spanish, and Dutch have large vocabulary bases (4,643–4,713 teach cards) but show a consistent pattern: **strong A1/A2 coverage drops sharply at B1/B2 for civic and abstract vocabulary**.

Missing B1/B2 categories across all three languages:
- Social/civic nouns: `société/sociedad/samenleving` (society), `politique/política/politiek` (politics), `économie/economía/economie` (economy), `éducation/educación/onderwijs` (education)
- Civic discourse verbs: `comparer/comparar/vergelijken` (compare), `critiquer/criticar/bekritiseren` (criticize)
- Abstract B2 concepts: `mondialisation/globalización/globalisering`, `démocratie/democracia/democratie`, `immigration/inmigración/immigratie`

These are directly tested in DELF B1/B2, DELE B1/B2, and NT2 B1/B2 reading comprehension and writing tasks.

### 2. Italian: Best-Performing Romance Language

Italian has the best coverage profile among Romance languages:
- A1: 83.5% | A2: 75.7% | B1: 60.0% | B2: 76.9%
- The B1 gap is largely 8 abstract nouns (`società`, `politica`, `economia`, `istruzione`, `sviluppo`, `vantaggio`, `svantaggio`, `confrontare`)
- B2 only 3 gaps in the sample (`argomentare`, `analizzare`, `criticare`)

### 3. Dutch: Strongest A1/A2 Coverage

Dutch achieves 92.1% A1/A2 coverage — the highest of all languages. The 11 missing A1/A2 words (`slapen`, `restaurant`, `vliegveld`, `vlees`, `slecht`, `makkelijk`, etc.) are remediable with targeted additions.

### 4. Korean: Systemic Gaps in Common Vocabulary

Despite 69 units and 3,930 teach cards, Korean shows a 62.5% TOPIK I coverage. Root causes:
- Many common verbs (`말하다`, `읽다`, `알다`, `모르다`, `만나다`) appear missing as standalone teach cards
- Native Korean numeral system (`하나/둘/셋...`) appears incomplete
- Discourse connectors (`그래서`, `하지만`, `그런데`) absent — these appear in every TOPIK reading passage

### 5. Japanese: PP65 Script Form Mismatch Inflates Gap Estimates

Some Japanese N5 gaps (e.g., `今日` today, `明日` tomorrow) may be taught in hiragana (`きょう`, `あした`) in older batch files predating PP65. A full PP65-compliant audit would need to:
- Check both kanji and hiragana representations
- Verify batch files have been fully converted to kanji+furigana

True gaps beyond script issues include personal pronouns (`私`, `彼`, `彼女`), common verbs (`来る`, `見る`, `聞く`), and basic adjectives.

### 6. New Languages (Chinese, Portuguese, Russian): Expected Low Coverage

These three languages (new as of 2026-04-17) have 574–728 teach cards, far below the 3,000–5,000 needed for B2 completeness. PP55 violations are expected at the current stage. Priority should be completing A1/A2 coverage first.

## Recommended Remediation Priority

### Priority 1 — Immediate (mature languages, A1/A2 gaps)

These words should exist but don't in languages that are otherwise complete:

**French A1**: `famille`, `restaurant`, `légume`, `fruit`, `sœur`, `enfant`, `ville`, `école`, `dormir`, `boire`, `savoir`
**Spanish A1**: `familia`, `restaurante`, `universidad`, `escuela`, `usted`, `hacer`, `querer`, `saber`, `fruta`
**Dutch A1**: `slapen`, `restaurant`, `vliegveld`, `vlees`, `slecht`, `makkelijk`
**Korean TOPIK I**: `말하다`, `알다`, `모르다`, `만나다`, `이것/그것/저것`, `병원`, `은행`, `식당`, `재미있다`, `어렵다`

### Priority 2 — High (B1 civic vocabulary, all mature Romance/Germanic)

A 30–60 word "civic vocabulary" lesson at B1 in French, Spanish, and Dutch covering:
- Society/politics/economy: `société/sociedad/samenleving`, `politique/política/politiek`, `économie/economía/economie`
- Education/development: `éducation/educación/onderwijs`, `développement/desarrollo/ontwikkeling`
- Discourse verbs: `proposer/proponer/voorstellen`, `refuser/rechazar/weigeren`, `comparer/comparar/vergelijken`

### Priority 3 — Medium (B2 civic discourse, mature languages)

- French/Spanish/Dutch B2: `mondialisation/globalización/globalisering`, `démocratie/democracia/democratie`, `immigration/inmigración/immigratie`, `discrimination/discriminación/discriminatie`
- Korean TOPIK II: `그래서`, `그러나`, `하지만`, `그런데` (discourse connectors)
- Italian B1: `società`, `politica`, `economia`, `istruzione`

### Priority 4 — Long-term (new language builds)

Chinese, Portuguese, and Russian need systematic A1/A2 completion before PP55 can be assessed meaningfully. Target: complete all CEFR A1/A2 thematic categories first, then A2-full sweep.

## Source Limitations

The following official wordlists are proprietary and could not be accessed in full:
- DELF/DALF (Alliance Française / CIEP): full lists require institutional access
- PCIC (Instituto Cervantes): full digital corpus not freely redistributable
- CILS/CELI: full lists proprietary to Siena/Perugia universities
- NT2 (CvTE): full 4,000-word list not publicly available
- ТРКИ (Pushkin Institute): official word list proprietary

For these, the audit used: (1) publicly available thematic category descriptors, (2) official sample test materials, and (3) well-known exam preparation reference lists. TOPIK, JLPT, and HSK official word lists are freely available and were used directly.

A fully exhaustive PP55 audit would require licensed access to the complete official word lists for DELF, DELE/PCIC, CILS, and NT2.
