## Framework Notes

- Japanese has exactly 36 units, mirroring the German template.
- Japanese A1 is notably compressed (U1-10 at A1.1/A1.2/A1.3), with 10 units at A1. However, the script-learning gap is structural: hiragana/katakana/kanji are handled via Foundations, so spelling/forms (F15, F19) don't map to lesson units.
- Weather is covered at A1.3 (U10) — one of the few languages with good weather coverage.
- Ability (F17) is appropriately deferred to A2.1 (U12) for Japanese, where the potential form is first introduced.
- B2 shows the weakest coverage (77%) due to the thematic vocabulary focus at B2.3-B2.4 (philosophy, law, medicine, literature, international relations). These units provide vocabulary but do not include dedicated communicative practice (role-plays, formal writing tasks, dispute scenarios).
- The lack of an administrative situations unit (F22) is consistent across all 9 non-German languages.
- PP65 (kanji+furigana) is fully implemented per prior audit; this does not affect PP58 scope.
