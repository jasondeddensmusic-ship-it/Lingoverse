# Korean Curriculum: TOPIK + TTMIK Standards Audit

> Date: 2026-03-14
> Scope: LingoVerse Korean A1-B1 (20 units, 195 lessons, 3,353 steps)
> Benchmarks: TOPIK I (Levels 1-2), TTMIK Levels 1-6

---

## Executive Summary

LingoVerse Korean covers approximately **60-65% of TTMIK Levels 1-6 grammar** with significantly more practice depth per covered point. The architecture (seed-harvest, cultural anchoring, Sino-Korean compound system) is pedagogically superior to TTMIK's linear approach. However, approximately **40 grammar patterns** that both TOPIK and TTMIK consider standard for A1-B1 are missing entirely.

**The problem is breadth, not depth.** 195 lessons and 3,353 steps is adequate volume. Each covered grammar point gets extensive drill. But there are real holes that would hurt learners in conversation and on TOPIK.

## What's Strong (Both Audits Agree)

1. **Seed-harvest system**: All 12 B1 seeds verified complete. No TTMIK equivalent exists. Pedagogically superior.
2. **Practice ratio**: 65.3% exercises (well above 50% target). Each grammar point drilled extensively.
3. **Cultural anchoring**: Every unit tied to real Korean life (눈치, 정, 회식, 수능). Stronger than TTMIK's culture-as-supplement.
4. **Irregular verb systematization**: All 7 families methodically planted and harvested across A1-B1.
5. **Sino-Korean compounds**: 130+ morpheme decompositions. Excellent LEGO principle execution.
6. **Stem+ending system**: Taught in U1 L2b, far earlier and more explicitly than TTMIK.
7. **Vowel harmony**: Previewed U1, formally taught U7. TTMIK never names the principle.
8. **Foundations**: All Hangul components covered. Gate quiz with diagnostic routing. Learners read before U1.

## Teaching Order Observations

**Strengths vs TTMIK:**
- Stem+ending system taught U1 (TTMIK waits until conjugation)
- Vowel harmony named and taught (TTMIK never names it)
- Seed-harvest system for irregulars (TTMIK scatters randomly)
- Cultural anchoring per unit (TTMIK treats culture as supplement)

**Concerns:**
- Past tense at U7 (lesson ~55). TTMIK: lesson 17. Significant delay for survival grammar.
- Future tense at U9. TTMIK: lesson 26. Similar delay.
- -(으)ㄹ게요 never taught. One of the most important Korean distinctions.
- Question words (누구/왜/어떻게) used in examples but never formally taught.

## Recommendation

The curriculum needs **~15-20 new lessons** distributed across existing and potentially new units to fill the Tier 1 and Tier 2 gaps. The existing architecture (seed-harvest, cultural anchoring, step types) easily supports this expansion. See `docs/KOREAN_CURRICULUM_GAP_PLAN.md` for the implementation plan.
