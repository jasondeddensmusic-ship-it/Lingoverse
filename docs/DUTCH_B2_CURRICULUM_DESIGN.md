## 1. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 What B2 Means for Dutch

B2 is the transition from independent Dutch (B1) to professional/academic Dutch. The learner moves from handling most everyday situations to engaging with complex texts, arguing positions, and functioning in Dutch professional environments. This maps to:

- **CEFR B2**: Can interact with native speakers without strain. Can produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects. Can explain viewpoints giving advantages and disadvantages.
- **NT2 Staatsexamen II / Programma II**: The official Dutch B2 exam covering reading, writing, listening, and speaking in professional/academic contexts.
- **Taalportaal B2 standards**: Complex sentence structures, formal register, and abstract vocabulary.

### 1.2 Core Design Principle: The Slot Deepens

Dutch B1 introduced "The Slot" metaphor (main verb position 2, participle/infinitive at end). B2 deepens this by teaching what happens INSIDE the slot with complex multi-clause sentences:
1. Extended verb clusters (three or more verbs stacking at the end)
2. Participial constructions replacing relative clauses
3. Left dislocation and topic fronting for emphasis
4. Written vs spoken register differences in slot usage

### 1.3 Cultural Anchoring

Every unit is anchored to a Dutch cultural context that provides natural sentences and motivation:

| Unit | Cultural Anchor | Why This Anchor |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| U21 | Op het werk (Workplace) | Formal emails, meetings, vergadercultuur — Dutch workplace has specific communication norms |
| U22 | Actueel (Current affairs) | News reading, NOS/RTL — passive voice and reported speech are news-register grammar |
| U23 | Mening geven (Debate culture) | Dutch directness is legendary — B2 gives tools to argue politely yet firmly |
| U24 | Onderwijs (Education system) | HBO/WO distinction, inschrijven, studiepunten — academic register for Dutch higher education |
| U25 | Gezondheid & Zorg (Healthcare) | Dutch healthcare system (huisarts, verwijsbrief) — formal vocabulary for real-life essential situations |
| U26 | Huisvesting & Recht (Housing & Law) | Huurcontract, woningnood, gemeente — legal/bureaucratic Dutch for essential adult life |

## 2. WHAT B1 GRADUATES KNOW

### Grammar Covered in Units 1-20:
- **Tenses**: Present, perfect (voltooid tegenwoordige tijd), imperfect (onvoltooid verleden tijd), past perfect (voltooid verleden tijd), future (zullen + infinitive)
- **Word order**: V2 main clause, verb-final subordinate clause, question inversion
- **Subordinate clauses**: dat, omdat, als, wanneer, toen, voordat, nadat, terwijl, hoewel, zodat
- **Relative clauses**: die, dat, wie, waar, wat
- **Passive voice**: worden + voltooid deelwoord (present), zijn + voltooid deelwoord (past)
- **Conditional**: zou/zouden + infinitive
- **Er**: 5 uses (existential, locative, prepositional, quantitative, passive)
- **Indirect speech**: Hij zei dat..., Ze vroeg of...
- **Infinitive constructions**: om...te, zonder...te, in plaats van...te
- **Verb clusters**: modal + infinitive, hebben/zijn + participle
- **Participles**: as adjectives (de gesloten deur)
- **Diminutives**: -je, -tje, -pje, -etje, -kje
- **Compounds**: productive compound formation
- **Prefixes**: ver-, be-, ont-, her-, ge-
- **Register**: informal (jij/je) vs formal (u)

### Vocabulary Domains Covered:
- Greetings, identity, numbers, time, food, family, home, transport, health, hobbies, work, money, opinions, education, housing, news, environment, arts

## 4. UNIT-BY-UNIT DESIGN

### Unit 21: Op het Werk — Workplace Communication
**Grammar**: Double infinitive (IPP), extended relative clauses, formal email register
**Vocabulary**: vergadering, notulen, overleggen, deadline, functioneringsgesprek, aanvragen
**Cultural anchor**: Dutch vergadercultuur — meetings have structure, everyone speaks, decisions are made by consensus (polderen)
**Lessons** (10):
1. Intro + workplace vocabulary (teach x5)
2. Double infinitive: Ik heb het niet kunnen doen (teach + drill)
3. IPP in past: Had willen komen, was gaan werken
4. Extended relative clauses in workplace context
5. Formal email: opening, body, closing conventions
6. Vergadercultuur: running a Dutch meeting
7. Functioneringsgesprek vocabulary and register
8. Combining IPP + relative clauses in professional text
9. Practice: write a formal email (guided)
10. Unit review + assessment

### Unit 22: Actueel — News & Current Affairs
**Grammar**: Passive with modals, participial constructions, impersonal constructions
**Vocabulary**: bericht, verklaring, maatregelen, onderzoek, commissie, aankondigen
**Cultural anchor**: NOS Journaal, RTL Nieuws — Dutch news language is neutral, factual, and heavily uses passive voice
**Lessons** (10):
1. Intro + news vocabulary (teach x5)
2. Passive with modals: Er moet ingegrepen worden
3. Passive with krijgen: Ik kreeg te horen dat...
4. Participial constructions: het door de storm beschadigde dak
5. Extended noun phrases in news Dutch
6. Impersonal constructions: Men verwacht dat..., Er wordt beweerd dat...
7. Reading Dutch news: NOS-style article structure
8. Agent marking with door + prepositional passive
9. News writing practice: convert active to passive
10. Unit review + assessment

### Unit 23: Mening Geven — Opinions & Dutch Debate
**Grammar**: Left dislocation, cleft sentences, discourse markers for argumentation
**Vocabulary**: standpunt, beweren, beargumenteren, nuanceren, weerleggen, concluderen
**Cultural anchor**: Dutch directness — the "doe maar normaal" culture values honest opinions, but B2 teaches how to disagree politely
**Lessons** (10):
1. Intro + opinion vocabulary (teach x5)
2. Left dislocation: emphasis through topic fronting
3. Cleft sentences: Het is X die/dat...
4. Discourse markers: enerzijds/anderzijds, daarentegen
5. Argumentation structure: stelling, argument, conclusie
6. Polite disagreement: Ik begrijp je punt, maar...
7. Nuanceren: hedging and softening in Dutch
8. Met andere woorden / kortom: summarizing arguments
9. Debate practice: for and against structured exercises
10. Unit review + assessment

### Unit 24: Onderwijs — Education & Academic Dutch
**Grammar**: Correlative conjunctions, extended verb clusters, nominalization
**Vocabulary**: inschrijven, collegegeld, studiepunten, propedeuse, afstuderen, scriptie
**Cultural anchor**: Dutch HBO/WO system — binary higher education, OV-chipkaart, studiefinanciering
**Lessons** (10):
1. Intro + education vocabulary (teach x5)
2. Correlative conjunctions: zowel...als, noch...noch
3. Hetzij...hetzij, niet alleen...maar ook
4. Extended verb clusters (3+ verbs)
5. Nominalization: van werkwoord naar zelfstandig naamwoord
6. Academic writing: essay structure (inleiding, kern, conclusie)
7. Hedging: Het lijkt erop dat..., Er kan worden gesteld...
8. Combining correlatives with subordination
9. Academic text reading practice
10. Unit review + assessment

### Unit 25: Gezondheid & Zorg — Healthcare System
**Grammar**: Advanced subordination (naarmate, doordat, vandaar dat)
**Vocabulary**: huisarts, verwijsbrief, specialist, behandeling, verzekering, eigen risico

### Unit 26: Huisvesting & Recht — Housing & Legal Dutch

### Unit 29: Reizen & Vergelijken — Travel & Advanced Comparison
**Grammar**: Auxiliary selection depth, advanced comparison (hoe...hoe, des te...des te)

### 5.1 Compound Decomposition (LEGO Principle)
Every Dutch compound gets decomposed on first appearance:
```
note: "COMPOUND: vergadering = vergader(discuss) + ing(noun suffix).\nDiscussion-event = meeting."
note: "COMPOUND: woningnood = woning(dwelling) + nood(need/distress).\nDwelling-distress = housing crisis."
note: "COMPOUND: waterbeheer = water(water) + beheer(management).\nWater-management."

### 7.1 Standard Teaching Lesson
```
intro → teach x4-5 → tip (grammar) → mc x3 → fb x2-3 → match → drag_fill
```
Target: 20-25 steps per lesson (P43 density, enforced per lesson).

### 7.2 Harvest Lesson
```

```

```
intro → tip (structure guide) → mc x2 → fb x3 → drag_fill x3 → match
```
Focuses on production skills (formal writing, argumentation).

### 7.4 Assessment Lesson
```
intro → mc x4 → fb x4 → drag_fill x2 → match

Pure quiz. Tests entire unit's grammar and vocabulary.
