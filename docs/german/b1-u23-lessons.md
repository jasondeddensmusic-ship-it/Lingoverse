## Lesson 1: "Lernmaschine" (Learning Machine)

**Type:** Story intro
**Mode:** Cinematic. Verumius's apartment transformed into a study bunker. Flashcards on walls, grammar charts, empty coffee cups, Post-its with Konjunktiv II forms. Dawn light through the window: he has been up all night.
**Characters:** Verumius (protagonist, deep in exam prep), Narrator
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r23l1, board:true
**New vocabulary (18):** Wissen(das), Ubung(die), Wiederholung(die), Methode(die), Regel(die), Kapitel(das), Inhalt(der), Bedeutung(die), Abschnitt(der), Reihenfolge(die), Zusammenhang(der), konzentrieren, verbessern, behalten, erkennen, regelmassig, grundlich, ziemlich
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Irreale Bedingungssatze (#6) in narrator text ("Wenn er mehr geschlafen hatte, ware er weniger mude"). RECYCLED: Konjunktiv II (wurde-form from A2), Nebensatz word order (weil/dass from A2)
**Communicative functions:** Describe study habits (exposure), express determination (exposure), talk about learning methods (exposure)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,983**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Lernmaschine." Goals: Learn vocabulary for studying and learning. See Verumius in full exam preparation mode. Understand the structure of the Goethe B1 exam.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:determined. "Es ist funf Uhr morgens. Verumius sitzt an seinem Schreibtisch. Uberall liegen Karteikarten, Grammatiktabellen und leere Kaffeetassen. Die Prufung ist in drei Tagen." / "It is five in the morning. Verumius sits at his desk. Everywhere lie flashcards, grammar charts, and empty coffee cups. The exam is in three days."
3. **teach:** Wissen(das). Fun info: "Knowledge." The noun form of "wissen" (to know). Das Wissen is neuter, like most nominalized infinitives. "Wissen ist Macht" (Knowledge is power) is a phrase Germans love, attributed to Francis Bacon.
4. **teach:** Ubung(die). Fun info: From "uben" (to practice). "Ubung macht den Meister" (Practice makes the master) is the German version of "Practice makes perfect." Note the upgrade: Germans aim for mastery, not mere perfection.
5. **teach:** Wiederholung(die). Fun info: Wieder (again) + holen (to fetch) + -ung. Literally "fetching again." Repetition, review, or a TV rerun. "Wiederholung ist die Mutter des Lernens" (Repetition is the mother of learning). Germans and Russians agree on this one.
6. **teach:** Methode(die). Fun info: From Greek "methodos" (pursuit of knowledge). Die Methode. "Eine gute Methode" (a good method). International word, same in dozens of languages. Verumius has tried every Methode: flashcards, apps, sticky notes on the mirror.
7. **teach:** Regel(die). Fun info: "Rule." From Latin "regula." "Die goldene Regel" (the golden rule). "In der Regel" (as a rule / usually). Grammar rules are "Grammatikregeln." German has many Regeln, but also many Ausnahmen (exceptions).
8. **teach:** Kapitel(das). Fun info: "Chapter." From Latin "capitulum" (little head). Das Kapitel. "Ein neues Kapitel aufschlagen" (to open a new chapter) works literally and metaphorically. Verumius is on the final Kapitel of his B1 textbook.
9. **teach:** Inhalt(der). Fun info: "Content/contents." In (in) + Halt (hold). What a container holds. "Inhaltsverzeichnis" (table of contents). The -halt root appears everywhere: Gehalt (salary = what a position holds), Haushalt (household = house-hold).
10. **teach:** Bedeutung(die). Fun info: "Meaning/significance." From "bedeuten" (to mean). "Von grosser Bedeutung" (of great significance). The Goethe exam tests whether you understand Bedeutungen in context, not just isolated words.
11. **teach:** Abschnitt(der). Fun info: "Section/paragraph." Ab (off) + Schnitt (cut). A piece cut off from a whole. "Textabschnitt" (text section). The Lesen (reading) part of the exam is divided into Abschnitte.
12. **teach:** Reihenfolge(die). Fun info: "Sequence/order." Reihe (row) + Folge (succession). The order in which things follow in a row. "In der richtigen Reihenfolge" (in the correct order). A word that is itself perfectly ordered: row-succession.
13. **teach:** Zusammenhang(der). Fun info: "Context/connection." Zusammen (together) + Hang (hanging). Things that hang together. "Im Zusammenhang mit" (in connection with). The exam tests whether you understand words im Zusammenhang, not in isolation.
14. **teach:** konzentrieren. Fun info: "To concentrate." From Latin "concentrare" (to bring to a center). Reflexive: "Sich konzentrieren" (to concentrate oneself). "Ich kann mich nicht konzentrieren" is what Verumius mutters at 5 AM.
15. **teach:** verbessern. Fun info: "To improve/correct." Ver- + besser (better). To make something better. "Fehler verbessern" (to correct mistakes). "Sich verbessern" (to improve oneself). The whole point of exam prep.
16. **teach:** behalten. Fun info: "To keep/retain/remember." Be- + halten (to hold). "Vokabeln behalten" (to retain vocabulary). "Das kann ich mir nicht behalten" (I cannot remember that). The verb every language learner wishes worked better.
17. **teach:** erkennen. Fun info: "To recognize/realize." Er- + kennen (to know). "Ich erkenne das Muster" (I recognize the pattern). Different from "kennen" (to be acquainted with). Erkennen is the flash of recognition.
18. **teach:** regelmassig. Fun info: "Regular/regularly." Regel (rule) + massig (measured). Following rules in a measured way. "Regelmassig uben" (to practice regularly). Verumius has been regelmassig studying for months. It shows.
19. **teach:** grundlich. Fun info: "Thorough/thoroughly." From Grund (ground/reason). Getting to the bottom of things. "Grundlich lernen" (to study thoroughly). Germans value Grundlichkeit (thoroughness) as a cultural trait.
20. **teach:** ziemlich. Fun info: "Quite/fairly." From "ziemen" (to be proper/fitting). Originally meant "fittingly." Now softens statements: "ziemlich gut" (quite good), "ziemlich mude" (fairly tired). The most diplomatic German intensifier.
21. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:reflective. "Wenn Verumius mehr geschlafen hatte, ware er jetzt weniger mude. Aber er braucht jede Minute. Die Prufung wartet nicht." / "If Verumius had slept more, he would be less tired now. But he needs every minute. The exam does not wait." [Exposes irreale Bedingungssatze naturally]
22. **mc:** Study vocabulary. q: "Verumius reviews his flashcards every evening. Which word describes this regular activity?" opts: [Wiederholung, Bedeutung, Zusammenhang, Abschnitt] ans: Wiederholung. hint: "The word means doing something again, from wieder (again) and holen (to fetch)."
23. **fb:** Learning context. s: "Um Vokabeln zu {1}, muss man regelmaessig uben." a: ["behalten"] opts: [behalten, erkennen, konzentrieren, verbessern] hint: "Which verb means to keep something in your memory?"
24. **match:** Study pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Methode",src:"method"},{trg:"Regel",src:"rule"},{trg:"Kapitel",src:"chapter"},{trg:"Reihenfolge",src:"sequence"}]

## Lesson 2: "Werkzeuge des Lernens" (Tools of Learning)

**Type:** Vocab teach 1
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Pure vocabulary: academic and language-learning terms
**Characters:** None (pure vocabulary)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r23l2, board:true
**New vocabulary (20):** Worterbuch(das), Lexikon(das), Uberschrift(die), Zeile(die), Grafik(die), Abbildung(die), Darstellung(die), Broschure(die), Detail(das), Punkt(der), Aussprache(die), Alphabet(das), Ausdruck(der), Beschreibung(die), Erklarung(die), Einzelheit(die), Hinweis(der), aufschreiben, nachschlagen, markieren
**Grammar:** RECYCLED: Nebensatz word order (weil/dass), Perfekt. EXPOSED: brauchen + zu (#29) in example sentences ("Du brauchst nur die Uberschriften zu lesen")
**Communicative functions:** Describe text structure (taught), use reference materials (taught), discuss language features (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,003**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Werkzeuge des Lernens." Goals: Learn vocabulary for books, texts, and reference materials. Master words for describing written content. Build your academic German toolkit.
2. **teach:** Worterbuch(das). Fun info: Worter (words) + Buch (book). A book of words. Das Worterbuch. The Duden is Germany's most famous Worterbuch, first published in 1880. Verumius has worn his Deutsch-Englisch Worterbuch thin.
3. **teach:** Lexikon(das). Fun info: From Greek "lexikon" (of words). An encyclopedia or comprehensive reference. Broader than a Worterbuch: a Lexikon explains concepts, not just definitions. "Das grosse Lexikon" sits on every German bookshelf.
4. **teach:** Uberschrift(die). Fun info: Uber (over/above) + Schrift (writing). The writing above: a headline or title. "Die Uberschrift lesen" (to read the headline). Exam tip: the Uberschrift often reveals the topic before you read the text.
5. **teach:** Zeile(die). Fun info: "Line (of text)." From Latin "titulus" via Old High German. "Zwischen den Zeilen lesen" (to read between the lines). The Goethe exam often asks "In welcher Zeile steht...?" (In which line does it say...?).
6. **teach:** Grafik(die). Fun info: "Graphic/chart." From Greek "graphike" (the art of drawing). "Eine Grafik beschreiben" (to describe a chart) is a standard B1 writing task. Pie charts, bar graphs, line charts: all Grafiken.
7. **teach:** Abbildung(die). Fun info: Ab (from) + Bild (picture) + -ung. A depiction, illustration, or figure. "Siehe Abbildung 3" (See Figure 3). Abbreviated "Abb." in textbooks. Every German academic paper is full of Abbildungen.
8. **teach:** Darstellung(die). Fun info: Dar (there/forth) + stellen (to place) + -ung. A representation or presentation. "Grafische Darstellung" (graphical representation). Broader than Abbildung: can be visual, verbal, or mathematical.
9. **teach:** Broschure(die). Fun info: From French "brochure" (stitched). A pamphlet or brochure. "Informationsbroschure" (information brochure). Verumius has collected Broschuren from every language school in Berlin.
10. **teach:** Detail(das). Fun info: From French "detail" (a cutting). Das Detail. "Im Detail" (in detail). "Auf Details achten" (to pay attention to details). The Goethe exam rewards those who read the Details carefully.
11. **teach:** Punkt(der). Fun info: "Point/period/dot." From Latin "punctum." "Punkt fur Punkt" (point by point). Also the period at the end of a sentence. "Auf den Punkt kommen" (to get to the point). Multi-purpose.
12. **teach:** Aussprache(die). Fun info: Aus (out) + Sprache (speech). How words come out of your mouth: pronunciation. "Die deutsche Aussprache" (German pronunciation). The Sprechen section of the Goethe exam evaluates your Aussprache.
13. **teach:** Alphabet(das). Fun info: From Greek alpha + beta (first two letters). Das Alphabet. The German alphabet has 30 letters: 26 standard + a, o, u, ss. "Das deutsche Alphabet aufsagen" (to recite the German alphabet).
14. **teach:** Ausdruck(der). Fun info: Aus (out) + Druck (press). Expression. "Ein idiomatischer Ausdruck" (an idiomatic expression). Also means "printout" (something pressed out of a printer). Context decides. The Goethe exam loves testing Ausdrucke.
15. **teach:** Beschreibung(die). Fun info: From "beschreiben" (to describe). Be- + schreiben (to write). Writing about something. "Bildbeschreibung" (picture description) is a classic exam task. Describe what you see, systematically.
16. **teach:** Erklarung(die). Fun info: From "erklaren" (to explain). "Eine einfache Erklarung" (a simple explanation). Also means "declaration" (Liebeserklarung = declaration of love). Explaining and declaring: both make something clear.
17. **teach:** Einzelheit(die). Fun info: Einzel (single/individual) + -heit. A single detail, a particular. "Alle Einzelheiten" (all the details). More formal than Detail. Official documents prefer Einzelheit. Exams use both.
18. **teach:** Hinweis(der). Fun info: Hin (toward) + Weis (pointing). A hint, note, or reference. "Wichtiger Hinweis" (important notice). Exam papers always begin with Hinweise (instructions). "Hinweis: Sie haben 60 Minuten."
19. **teach:** aufschreiben. Fun info: Auf (up/on) + schreiben (to write). To write down. "Schreib das auf!" (Write that down!). Separable verb: "Ich schreibe es auf." Verumius has aufgeschrieben every new word for a year.
20. **teach:** nachschlagen. Fun info: Nach (after/to look up) + schlagen (to hit/strike). To look up (in a reference). "Ein Wort nachschlagen" (to look up a word). Separable: "Ich schlage es nach." Originally meant to strike the pages of a book open.
21. **teach:** markieren. Fun info: "To mark/highlight." From French "marquer." "Wichtige Stellen markieren" (to highlight important passages). Verumius's textbook is more yellow (from highlighting) than white.
22. **mc:** Reference materials. q: "Verumius needs to find the meaning of an unknown word quickly. Which tool should he use?" opts: [Worterbuch, Broschure, Grafik, Uberschrift] ans: Worterbuch. hint: "A book of words, where meanings and translations live."
23. **fb:** Text structure. s: "Lesen Sie zuerst die {1}, bevor Sie den ganzen Text lesen." a: ["Uberschrift"] opts: [Uberschrift, Zeile, Einzelheit, Punkt] hint: "Exam strategy: read the writing above the text first."
24. **match:** Academic pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Hinweis",src:"hint/note"},{trg:"Ausdruck",src:"expression"},{trg:"Beschreibung",src:"description"},{trg:"Erklarung",src:"explanation"}]

## Lesson 3: "Vor der Prufung" (Before the Exam)

**Type:** Vocab teach 2
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Education, exam, and formal writing vocabulary
**Characters:** None (pure vocabulary)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r23l3, board:true
**New vocabulary (20):** Institut(das), Intensivkurs(der), Semester(das), Seminar(das), Klassenarbeit(die), Schularbeit(die), Nachhilfe(die), Referat(das), Prasentation(die), Studie(die), Studierende(die), Teilnahme(die), Teilnehmer(der), Teilnehmerin(die), Professor(der), Professorin(die), Kursleiter(der), Kursleiterin(die), unterrichten, testen
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: brauchen + zu (#29) in example sentences. RECYCLED: Konjunktiv II (wurde-forms), Nebensatz word order
**Communicative functions:** Talk about education and courses (taught), describe academic roles (taught), discuss exam preparation (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,023**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Vor der Prufung." Goals: Learn vocabulary for education, courses, and academic life. Know the people in a classroom. Prepare for the Goethe exam format.
2. **teach:** Institut(das). Fun info: From Latin "institutum" (establishment). Das Institut. "Goethe-Institut" is Germany's worldwide cultural institute, present in 98 countries. Where Verumius will sit his exam.
3. **teach:** Intensivkurs(der). Fun info: Intensiv (intensive) + Kurs (course). A course with more hours per week. "Deutsch-Intensivkurs" (intensive German course). Typically 20-25 hours per week. Verumius took one in his first month.
4. **teach:** Semester(das). Fun info: From Latin "semestris" (six-monthly). Das Semester. German universities run on Wintersemester (October-March) and Sommersemester (April-September). Borrowed into English for the same concept.
5. **teach:** Seminar(das). Fun info: From Latin "seminarium" (seed bed). A smaller, discussion-based class. "Im Seminar diskutieren" (to discuss in the seminar). More interactive than a Vorlesung (lecture). Ideas grow like seeds.
6. **teach:** Klassenarbeit(die). Fun info: Klasse (class) + Arbeit (work). A written test in school. "Morgen schreiben wir eine Klassenarbeit" makes every German student nervous. More formal than a Quiz, less than an Examen.
7. **teach:** Schularbeit(die). Fun info: Schule (school) + Arbeit (work). Homework or a school test (varies by region). In Austria, Schularbeit means a formal written exam. In Germany, it often means homework. Regional German at work.
8. **teach:** Nachhilfe(die). Fun info: Nach (after) + Hilfe (help). Extra tutoring. "Nachhilfe geben" (to give tutoring), "Nachhilfe nehmen" (to take tutoring). A massive industry in Germany: one in four students gets Nachhilfe.
9. **teach:** Referat(das). Fun info: From Latin "referre" (to report). A presentation or oral report. "Ein Referat halten" (to give a presentation). Standard in German schools and universities. You research, write, and present.
10. **teach:** Prasentation(die). Fun info: From Latin "praesentare." A presentation. More modern than Referat. "Eine PowerPoint-Prasentation" is what German students prepare today. The Goethe B1 Sprechen section is essentially a mini-Prasentation.
11. **teach:** Studie(die). Fun info: "Study (scientific)." From Latin "studium." "Laut einer Studie" (according to a study). Different from "Studium" (university studies). A Studie is a research project, a Studium is what you do at university.
12. **teach:** Studierende(die). Fun info: "Student(s)." The gender-neutral modern form. From "studieren." Replacing "Student/Studentin" in official German. Universities now write "Liebe Studierende" (Dear students) to be inclusive.
13. **teach:** Teilnahme(die). Fun info: Teil (part) + Nahme (taking). Participation. "Teilnahme am Kurs" (participation in the course). "Teilnahmebescheinigung" (certificate of attendance). You need a Teilnahmebescheinigung to sit the Goethe exam.
14. **teach:** Teilnehmer(der). Fun info: "Participant (male)." Teil + nehmen + -er. One who takes part. "Kursteilnehmer" (course participant). The Goethe exam room has about 20 Teilnehmer per session.
15. **teach:** Teilnehmerin(die). Fun info: The feminine form. Teilnehmer + -in. "Alle Teilnehmerinnen und Teilnehmer" (all participants, fem. and masc.). Modern German often uses "Teilnehmende" as a gender-neutral alternative.
16. **teach:** Professor(der). Fun info: From Latin "professor" (public teacher). In Germany, Professor is an earned title, not just a job description. "Herr Professor Muller" is correct. Used on business cards, nameplates, and doorbells.
17. **teach:** Professorin(die). Fun info: The feminine form. "Frau Professorin Schmidt." Only about 27% of German professors are women (as of 2024). The gap is closing, but slowly.
18. **teach:** Kursleiter(der). Fun info: Kurs (course) + Leiter (leader). Course instructor. "Der Kursleiter erklärt die Grammatik" (The course instructor explains the grammar). Verumius's Kursleiter at the Goethe-Institut is strict but fair.
19. **teach:** Kursleiterin(die). Fun info: The feminine form. "Die Kursleiterin gibt die Prufungstermine bekannt" (The course instructor announces the exam dates). -in suffix, as always for feminine person nouns.
20. **teach:** unterrichten. Fun info: Unter (under/among) + richten (to direct). To teach/instruct. "Deutsch unterrichten" (to teach German). "Unterricht" (instruction/class) is the noun form. Originally meant to direct knowledge into someone.
21. **teach:** testen. Fun info: "To test." From English "test," which came from Latin "testum" (clay pot used for assaying metals). Full circle: Latin to English to German. "Wissen testen" (to test knowledge). Simple, regular, international.
22. **mc:** Education vocabulary. q: "Verumius needs extra help with grammar before the exam. What should he look for?" opts: [Nachhilfe, Referat, Studie, Semester] ans: Nachhilfe. hint: "The word for extra tutoring. Nach (after) + Hilfe (help)."
23. **fb:** Academic context. s: "Am Goethe-{1} kann man die B1-Prufung ablegen." a: ["Institut"] opts: [Institut, Seminar, Studio, Semester] hint: "The worldwide German cultural organization."
24. **match:** Education roles. pairs: [{trg:"Kursleiter",src:"course instructor"},{trg:"Teilnehmer",src:"participant"},{trg:"Professor",src:"professor"},{trg:"Studierende",src:"students"}]

## Lesson 4: "Hypothesen und Wunsche" (Hypotheses and Wishes)

**Type:** Story development + grammar introduction
**Mode:** Bubble. Dr. Yilmaz's office at the Goethe-Institut. Bookshelves, diplomas, warm lamp, two cups of tea. Verumius across the desk, practice exam in hand.
**Characters:** Dr. Yilmaz (mentor, calm reassurance), Verumius (nervous, seeking advice)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r23l4, board:true
**New vocabulary (18):** Ausnahme(die), Folge(die), Gegensatz(der), Grund(der), Ursache(die), Begrudung(die), Reaktion(die), Versuch(der), Vorteil(der), Nachteil(der), feststellen, ergänzen, folgen, gelten, stimmen, gelingen, allgemein, kompliziert
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Irreale Bedingungssatze (#6). Structure: Wenn + subject + Konjunktiv II, main clause with wurde + infinitive. Also hatte/ware in wenn-clause. TAUGHT: brauchen + zu (#29). Structure: brauchen + nicht/nur/kein + zu + infinitive. EXPOSED: Double infinitive (#32) in Yilmaz's speech
**Communicative functions:** Express hypothetical wishes (taught), give reasons and explanations (taught), discuss advantages and disadvantages (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,041**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Hypothesen und Wunsche." Goals: Master irreale Bedingungssatze (unreal conditional sentences). Learn brauchen + zu for necessity. Discuss exam strategies with Dr. Yilmaz.
2. **story:** speaker:yilmaz, mood:calm-mentor. "Wenn du mehr Zeit hattest, wurdest du dich sicherer fuhlen. Aber du brauchst dir keine Sorgen zu machen. Du bist gut vorbereitet." / "If you had more time, you would feel more confident. But you do not need to worry. You are well prepared." [Models both #6 and #29]
3. **teach:** Ausnahme(die). Fun info: Aus (out) + Nahme (taking). Something taken out of the rule. An exception. "Die Ausnahme bestatigt die Regel" (The exception confirms the rule). Grammar is full of Regeln with Ausnahmen.
4. **teach:** Folge(die). Fun info: "Consequence/episode." From "folgen" (to follow). "Als Folge" (as a consequence). Also: a TV episode. "Die nachste Folge" (the next episode). Consequence and sequel: what follows.
5. **teach:** Gegensatz(der). Fun info: Gegen (against) + Satz (sentence/setting). The opposite. "Im Gegensatz zu" (in contrast to). "Gegensatze ziehen sich an" (Opposites attract). A word that contains opposition in its bones.
6. **teach:** Grund(der). Fun info: "Reason/ground." "Aus diesem Grund" (for this reason). Same word for physical ground and abstract reason. "Im Grunde" (basically). "Grundlos" (without reason). The foundation of arguments.
7. **teach:** Ursache(die). Fun info: Ur- (original/primal) + Sache (thing/matter). The original cause. "Die Ursache des Problems" (the cause of the problem). Deeper than Grund: Ursache is the root cause, Grund is the stated reason.
8. **teach:** Begrudung(die). Fun info: From "begrunden" (to give reasons). The justification. "Eine schriftliche Begrudung" (a written justification). On the Goethe exam, "Begrunden Sie Ihre Meinung" (Justify your opinion) is a standard prompt.
9. **teach:** Reaktion(die). Fun info: From Latin "reactio." "Meine Reaktion" (my reaction). "Allergische Reaktion" (allergic reaction). International word. The Goethe exam's Sprechen section tests your Reaktion to discussion prompts.
10. **teach:** Versuch(der). Fun info: From "versuchen" (to try/attempt). An attempt or experiment. "Beim ersten Versuch" (on the first try). "Versuch und Irrtum" (trial and error). Scientists say "Versuch" where English says "experiment."
11. **teach:** Vorteil(der). Fun info: Vor (before/in front) + Teil (part). The part in front, the advantage. "Der grosse Vorteil" (the big advantage). "Vor- und Nachteile abwagen" (to weigh pros and cons). The Goethe exam loves this task.
12. **teach:** Nachteil(der). Fun info: Nach (after/behind) + Teil (part). The part behind, the disadvantage. "Ein grosser Nachteil" (a big disadvantage). "Vorteile und Nachteile" is one of the most common B1 writing tasks.
13. **teach:** feststellen. Fun info: Fest (firm) + stellen (to place). To establish, determine, or notice. "Ich stelle fest, dass..." (I notice/find that...). "Feststellung" (finding/observation). To place something firmly into knowledge.
14. **teach:** erganzen. Fun info: "To complete/supplement." From "ganz" (whole). To make whole again. "Den Satz erganzen" (to complete the sentence). Exam instruction: "Erganzen Sie den Text" (Complete the text).
15. **teach:** folgen. Fun info: "To follow." "Daraus folgt..." (It follows that...). "Dem Rat folgen" (to follow the advice). Takes dative. "Konnen Sie mir folgen?" (Can you follow me?) means "Do you understand?"
16. **teach:** gelten. Fun info: "To be valid/to apply." Irregular: gilt, galt, gegolten. "Diese Regel gilt immer" (This rule always applies). "Als Experte gelten" (to be considered an expert). A word about what counts.
17. **teach:** stimmen. Fun info: "To be correct/to vote." "Das stimmt" (That is correct). "Abstimmen" (to vote). From "Stimme" (voice). Your voice agrees: it is correct. One of the most useful B1 words.
18. **teach:** gelingen. Fun info: "To succeed." Impersonal: "Es gelingt mir" (I succeed, literally: it succeeds for me). Irregular: gelang, gelungen. "Wenn es mir gelingt, die Prufung zu bestehen..." (If I succeed in passing the exam...). Dative subject.
19. **teach:** allgemein. Fun info: "General/generally." "Im Allgemeinen" (in general). "Allgemeinbildung" (general education). From "alle" (all) + "gemein" (common). What is common to all. Formal register, common in academic German.
20. **teach:** kompliziert. Fun info: From Latin "complicare" (to fold together). "Die Grammatik ist kompliziert" (The grammar is complicated). Verumius's most-used adjective when talking about German. But: "Kompliziert ist nicht unmöglich."
21. **tip:** "Irreale Bedingungssatze: The 'What if' of German" -- Structure: Wenn + subject + Konjunktiv II verb (hatte/ware/konnte...), subject + wurde + infinitive. Example: "Wenn ich mehr Zeit hatte, wurde ich mehr lernen" (If I had more time, I would study more). The wenn-clause uses hatte/ware, the main clause uses wurde + infinitive. Without wenn: "Hatte ich mehr Zeit, wurde ich mehr lernen" (verb jumps to position 1). This is ONLY for unreal/impossible conditions. For real conditions, use normal Wenn + present.
22. **tip:** "brauchen + zu: The polite refusal" -- Structure: brauchen + nicht/nur/kein + zu + infinitive. "Du brauchst nicht zu kommen" (You do not need to come). "Man braucht nur zu fragen" (One only needs to ask). NEVER without nicht/nur/kein: saying "Ich brauche zu lernen" is wrong. Colloquial speech often drops "zu": "Du brauchst nicht kommen." The Goethe exam expects the full form with "zu."

2. **teach:** bevor. Fun info: "Before." Be- + vor (before). "Bevor du die Prufung schreibst, atme tief ein" (Before you write the exam, take a deep breath). Nebensatz conjunction: verb goes to the end. Different from "vor" (preposition).

4. **teach:** damit. Fun info: "So that/in order that." "Er ubt, damit er die Prufung besteht" (He practices so that he passes the exam). Nebensatz: verb at end. Different from "um...zu" (which requires same subject). Damit allows different subjects.
5. **teach:** nachdem. Fun info: "After." Nach (after) + dem. "Nachdem er die Prufung bestanden hat, feiert er" (After he has passed the exam, he celebrates). Requires a tense shift: the nachdem-clause uses a "more past" tense than the main clause.
6. **teach:** obwohl. Fun info: "Although." Ob (whether) + wohl (well). "Obwohl er mude ist, lernt er weiter" (Although he is tired, he keeps studying). Nebensatz conjunction. The concession word. "I know, but still."
7. **teach:** falls. Fun info: "In case/if." Fall (case) + -s. "Falls du Fragen hast, frag mich" (In case you have questions, ask me). More conditional than "wenn" (which also means "when"). Falls is purely hypothetical.
8. **teach:** sobald. Fun info: "As soon as." So (so) + bald (soon). "Sobald die Prufung beginnt, bin ich konzentriert" (As soon as the exam starts, I am focused). The trigger-word: the moment X happens, Y begins.
9. **teach:** solange. Fun info: "As long as." So (so) + lange (long). "Solange ich lebe, lerne ich" (As long as I live, I learn). Duration with a condition. "Solange du ubst, wirst du besser." Time-bound conditions.
10. **teach:** sodass. Fun info: "So that (result)." So + dass. "Er hat grundlich gelernt, sodass er gut vorbereitet ist" (He studied thoroughly, so that he is well prepared). Introduces a RESULT, not a purpose. Compare: damit (purpose) vs. sodass (result).
11. **teach:** indem. Fun info: "By (doing)." In + dem. "Man lernt Sprachen, indem man sie spricht" (One learns languages by speaking them). The method conjunction. How you achieve something. Unique to German; English uses "by + gerund."
12. **teach:** ob. Fun info: "Whether." "Ich frage mich, ob ich bestehe" (I wonder whether I will pass). Introduces indirect questions. "Ob er kommt oder nicht" (Whether he comes or not). Short, common, essential.
13. **teach:** entweder. Fun info: "Either." Always paired with "oder" (or). "Entweder du bestehst oder du versuchst es noch mal" (Either you pass or you try again). Entweder...oder is the binary choice structure.
14. **teach:** weder. Fun info: "Neither." Always paired with "noch" (nor). "Weder mude noch nervos" (Neither tired nor nervous). Weder...noch negates both options. Double negation without being wrong.
15. **teach:** sowohl. Fun info: "Both." Always paired with "als auch" (as well as). "Sowohl mundlich als auch schriftlich" (Both orally and in writing). Inclusive: not one or the other, but both.
16. **teach:** sondern. Fun info: "But rather." Stronger than "aber." Used ONLY after a negation. "Nicht schwer, sondern anspruchsvoll" (Not hard, but rather demanding). Corrects a misconception. The "actually" of German conjunctions.
17. **teach:** trotzdem. Fun info: "Nevertheless." Trotz (despite) + dem. "Die Prufung ist schwer. Trotzdem gibt er nicht auf" (The exam is hard. Nevertheless he does not give up). Adverbial connector: main clause word order. Different from obwohl (Nebensatz).
18. **teach:** jedoch. Fun info: "However." More formal than "aber." "Die Aufgabe ist lang, jedoch machbar" (The task is long, however doable). Written German, formal register. Goethe Schreiben section loves jedoch.
19. **teach:** nämlich. Fun info: "Namely/because." "Er ist mude, er hat nämlich nicht geschlafen" (He is tired; he has not slept, you see). Post-verbal position. Explains the reason AFTER the statement. Unique German word order.
20. **teach:** zwar. Fun info: "Indeed/admittedly." "Zwar ist die Prufung schwer, aber machbar" (Admittedly the exam is hard, but doable). Always paired with "aber" or "jedoch." The concession before the counter-argument. Sophisticated B1 writing.
21. **teach:** ebenfalls. Fun info: "Likewise/also." Eben (even/just) + falls (case). "Ich wunsche Ihnen ebenfalls viel Erfolg" (I wish you likewise much success). Formal. More elegant than "auch." Written register.
22. **mc:** Conjunction usage. q: "Er lernt viel, _____ er die Prufung besteht." opts: [damit, sodass, obwohl, bevor] ans: damit. hint: "He studies so that he WILL pass. Which conjunction expresses purpose?"
23. **mc:** Contrast words. q: "Die Prufung ist nicht einfach, _____ anspruchsvoll." opts: [sondern, aber, trotzdem, jedoch] ans: sondern. hint: "After a negation (nicht), which word means 'but rather'?"
24. **fb:** Complex connector. s: "{1} die Prufung beginnt, liest Verumius die Hinweise." a: ["Bevor"] opts: [Bevor, Nachdem, Sobald, Solange] hint: "Which conjunction means 'before'?"
25. **match:** Conjunction pairs. pairs: [{trg:"obwohl",src:"although"},{trg:"nachdem",src:"after"},{trg:"sobald",src:"as soon as"},{trg:"indem",src:"by (doing)"}]
26. **drag_fill:** Two-part conjunction. s: "{1} er gut vorbereitet ist, {2} er trotzdem nervos." blanks: {1:"Obwohl",2:"ist"} pool: ["Obwohl","Weil","ist","hat","bleibt","Nachdem"] hint: "Although he is prepared, he IS still nervous. Obwohl = Nebensatz (verb at end), main clause = normal V2 word order."

## Lesson 9: "BESTANDEN!" (PASSED!)

**Type:** Story resolution + quiz
**Mode:** Cinematic opening (Goethe-Institut hallway, results board, BESTANDEN). Transition to bubble (Biergarten celebration, full cast, Hildi's cake).
**Characters:** Verumius (triumphant), Dr. Yilmaz (proud), Hildi (cake), Lukas (beer), Mia (gift), Heinrich (bread and wisdom)
**Step count:** 26
**id:** deu_r23l9, board:true
**New vocabulary (20):** deswegen, ebenso, genauso, weshalb, wieso, daher, seitdem, teilweise, eher, eventuell, offenbar, vermutlich, ungefahr, unterschiedlich, interessant, interessiert, neugierig, begrenzt, sichtbar, speziell
**Grammar:** PRACTICED: All three constructs (#6, #29, #32) in celebration context. RECYCLED: All B1 grammar integrated into story and quiz. Focus: natural usage in emotional context.
**Communicative functions:** Express relief and joy (taught), give congratulations (practiced), reflect on achievement (taught), express gratitude (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,139**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "BESTANDEN!" Goals: Learn adverbs of reason and degree. Experience the moment of exam results. Celebrate with the full cast at the Biergarten.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:triumph. "Verumius steht im Flur des Goethe-Instituts. Seine Augen suchen die Liste. Sein Finger gleitet uber die Namen. Da: sein Name. Daneben ein einziges Wort: BESTANDEN. Er ballt die Faust." / "Verumius stands in the hallway of the Goethe-Institut. His eyes scan the list. His finger slides across the names. There: his name. Beside it, a single word: PASSED. He clenches his fist."
3. **teach:** deswegen. Fun info: "Therefore/that is why." Des (of that) + wegen (because of). "Er hat viel gelernt, deswegen hat er bestanden" (He studied a lot, that is why he passed). Causal connector. Main clause word order after deswegen.
4. **teach:** ebenso. Fun info: "Equally/just as." Eben (even/just) + so (so). "Ebenso wichtig wie Grammatik ist Vokabelarbeit" (Equally important as grammar is vocabulary work). Formal register. Comparison without "wie...wie."
5. **teach:** genauso. Fun info: "Just as/exactly as." Genau (exact) + so (so). "Genauso nervos wie beim ersten Mal" (Just as nervous as the first time). Less formal than ebenso. "Genauso gut" (just as good). Precise comparison.
6. **teach:** weshalb. Fun info: "For which reason/why." Wes (whose) + halb (on behalf of). More formal than "warum." "Weshalb hast du so viel gelernt?" (Why did you study so much?). Written and formal register. Goethe exam level.
7. **teach:** wieso. Fun info: "Why/how come." From "wie so" (how so). More casual than "warum," which is more neutral than "weshalb." Three words for "why," three registers. "Wieso nicht?" (Why not?). Conversational.
8. **teach:** daher. Fun info: "Therefore/from there." Da (there) + her (from here). "Daher kommt sein Erfolg" (That is where his success comes from). Also: "Daher hat er bestanden" (Therefore he passed). Direction and causality in one word.
9. **teach:** seitdem. Fun info: "Since then." Seit (since) + dem (that). "Seitdem er in Deutschland ist, lernt er Deutsch" (Since he has been in Germany, he has been learning German). Conjunction or adverb. Marks the starting point.
10. **teach:** teilweise. Fun info: "Partially/partly." Teil (part) + -weise (way/manner). "Teilweise richtig" (partially correct). "Teilweise bewolkt" (partly cloudy). Not all, not none: somewhere in between.
11. **teach:** eher. Fun info: "Rather/sooner." Comparative of "eh" (anyway). "Eher schwierig als einfach" (Rather difficult than easy). "Je eher, desto besser" (The sooner, the better). Subtle preference.
12. **teach:** eventuell. Fun info: "Possibly/perhaps." FALSE FRIEND: does NOT mean "eventually" (that is "schliesslich"). "Eventuell komme ich spater" (I might come later). One of the most dangerous false friends between English and German.
13. **teach:** offenbar. Fun info: "Apparently/evidently." Offen (open) + -bar (able to be). Open to being seen: apparent. "Offenbar hat er bestanden" (Apparently he passed). "Offenbar" suggests evidence without certainty.
14. **teach:** vermutlich. Fun info: "Presumably/probably." From "vermuten" (to presume). "Vermutlich ist er glucklich" (Presumably he is happy). Stronger than "vielleicht," weaker than "sicher." Educated guess.
15. **teach:** ungefahr. Fun info: "Approximately." Un- (not) + Gefahr (danger). Originally "without danger/harmless," shifted to "rough/approximate." "Ungefahr drei Stunden" (approximately three hours). The meaning journey: harmless to approximate.
16. **teach:** unterschiedlich. Fun info: "Different/varying." From "unterscheiden" (to distinguish). "Unterschiedliche Meinungen" (differing opinions). "Die Ergebnisse sind unterschiedlich" (The results vary). Academic vocabulary.
17. **teach:** interessant. Fun info: "Interesting." From French "interessant." "Ein interessantes Buch" (an interesting book). "Interessanterweise" (interestingly). Every German teacher's favorite compliment: "Interessant!"
18. **teach:** interessiert. Fun info: "Interested." Past participle as adjective. "Ich bin interessiert" (I am interested). "Interessiert an" + dative. "Interessiert" is the person; "interessant" is the thing. Common confusion.
19. **teach:** neugierig. Fun info: "Curious." Neu (new) + gierig (greedy). Greedy for the new. "Neugierig auf die Ergebnisse" (Curious about the results). The German word for curiosity reveals its nature: hunger for novelty.
20. **teach:** begrenzt. Fun info: "Limited." From "begrenzen" (to limit). Be- + Grenze (border). "Begrenzte Zeit" (limited time). "Meine Geduld ist begrenzt" (My patience is limited). The past participle as adjective.
21. **story:** speaker:heinrich, mood:warm-wise. "Heinrich kommt spat mit einem Laib Sauerteigbrot, in Stoff gewickelt. Er klopft Verumius auf die Schulter: 'Prufungen kommen und gehen. Aber Brot bleibt.' Alle lachen." / "Heinrich arrives late with a loaf of sourdough bread wrapped in cloth. He claps Verumius on the shoulder: 'Exams come and go. But bread stays.' Everyone laughs."
22. **story:** speaker:heinrich, mood:reflective. "Heinrich erzahlt, wie er vor sechzig Jahren seine eigene Meisterprufung nicht bestanden hat. 'Beim zweiten Mal war das Brot besser.' Verumius lachelt." / "Heinrich tells how he failed his own master baker's exam sixty years ago. 'The second time, the bread was better.' Verumius smiles."
23. **mc:** Cause and effect. q: "Verumius hat monatelang gelernt. _____ hat er die Prufung bestanden." opts: [Deswegen, Trotzdem, Obwohl, Bevor] ans: Deswegen. hint: "Months of study caused the result. Which connector shows causality?"
24. **fb:** False friend. s: "{1} kommt Lukas zur Feier. Er ist sich noch nicht sicher." a: ["Eventuell"] opts: [Eventuell, Schliesslich, Seitdem, Offenbar] hint: "Which word means 'possibly,' not 'eventually'?"
25. **mc:** Irrealis in reflection. q: "Wenn Verumius nicht nach Deutschland gekommen ware, hatte er diese Freunde nie kennengelernt. This sentence expresses..." opts: ["Gratitude for a choice that could have gone differently.", "A plan to leave Germany.", "Regret about coming to Germany.", "A prediction about the future."] ans: "Gratitude for a choice that could have gone differently." hint: "The unreal past condition (nicht gekommen ware) shows the road not taken. The reality is the opposite: he DID come, and he DID meet them."
26. **drag_fill:** Celebration language. s: "Verumius ist {1} stolz {2} glucklich." blanks: {1:"sowohl",2:"als auch"} pool: ["sowohl","als auch","weder","noch","entweder","oder"] hint: "He is BOTH proud AND happy. Which two-part conjunction means both...and?"

## Lesson 10: "Bildung und Kultur" (Education and Culture)

**Type:** Culture + final quiz
**Mode:** Bubble. Cultural deep dive + comprehensive assessment
**Characters:** None (culture + assessment)
**Step count:** 28
**id:** deu_r23l10, board:true
**New vocabulary (36):** dasselbe, derselbe, dieselbe, dies, diese, dieser, dieses, irgendein, mehrere, sämtlich, selb, selber, solch, so viel, desto, gleichfalls, je, umso, regeln, fassen, mitteilen, ausrichten, hinweisen, ereignen, genügen, gelten, stimmen, Aufnahme(die), mittler, ofter, relativ, folgend, spezial-, Biologie(die), Grafik(die), sichtbar
**Grammar:** ASSESSED: All three constructs (#6, #29, #32) in comprehensive quiz. RECYCLED: All B1 grammar. Focus: demonstrative pronouns, indefinite determiners, and remaining vocabulary integration.
**Communicative functions:** Discuss education systems (taught), express opinions on learning (practiced), use formal written register (assessed), express comparison and identity (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,175**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Bildung und Kultur." Goals: Learn demonstrative pronouns and remaining academic vocabulary. Explore German education culture. Prove mastery of all Unit 23 grammar in a final assessment.
2. **teach:** dasselbe. Fun info: Das (the, neuter) + selbe (same). "The same thing." "Das ist dasselbe" (That is the same thing). Different from "das Gleiche" (the same kind). Dasselbe = identical object. Das Gleiche = same type, different object. Germans argue about this distinction.
3. **teach:** derselbe. Fun info: "The same (masculine)." "Derselbe Mann" (the same man). "Am selben Tag" (on the same day). The article part (der/die/das) declines, but "selbe" stays. A hybrid word.
4. **teach:** dieselbe. Fun info: "The same (feminine)." "Dieselbe Frage" (the same question). "Immer dieselbe Geschichte" (Always the same story). Three genders, three forms, one meaning: identical.
5. **teach:** dies. Fun info: "This." Short form of "dieses." "Dies ist mein Ergebnis" (This is my result). "Dies" is formal/written. Spoken German prefers "das" or "dieses." Academic and exam register.
6. **teach:** diese. Fun info: "This/these (feminine/plural)." "Diese Prufung" (this exam). "Diese Worter" (these words). The feminine/plural demonstrative. Declines through all cases.
7. **teach:** dieser. Fun info: "This (masculine)." "Dieser Tag" (this day). "Dieser Moment" (this moment). Points to something specific. "Eben dieser Moment" (this very moment). Emphatic when combined with "eben."
8. **teach:** dieses. Fun info: "This (neuter)." "Dieses Buch" (this book). "Dieses Mal" (this time). The full neuter form. In spoken German, often shortened to "dies Buch" or even "dis."
9. **teach:** irgendein. Fun info: "Some/any." Irgend- (some/any) + ein. "Irgendein Fehler" (some mistake or other). The vagueness prefix. "Irgendwann" (sometime), "irgendwo" (somewhere), "irgendwie" (somehow). Irgend- makes everything indefinite.
10. **teach:** mehrere. Fun info: "Several." From "mehr" (more). "Mehrere Versuche" (several attempts). Always plural. "Mehrere hundert Teilnehmer" (several hundred participants). More than two, fewer than many.
11. **teach:** sämtlich. Fun info: "All/entire." "Samtliche Aufgaben" (all tasks). Stronger than "alle." "Samtliche Teilnehmer haben bestanden" (Every single participant passed). Emphatic completeness. Written register.
12. **teach:** selb. Fun info: The root of derselbe/dieselbe/dasselbe. "Zur selben Zeit" (at the same time). Only appears combined with articles. The bare form "selb" is archaic. Modern German needs the article: derselbe, dieselbe.
13. **teach:** selber. Fun info: "Oneself (colloquial)." "Ich habe es selber gemacht" (I did it myself). Informal version of "selbst." Northern German prefers "selber," southern German prefers "selbst." Both are correct.
14. **teach:** solch. Fun info: "Such." "Solch ein Erfolg" (such a success). "Ein solcher Tag" (such a day). Flexible word order: "solch ein" or "ein solcher." Same meaning as "so ein" but more formal.
15. **teach:** so viel. Fun info: "So much." "So viel gelernt" (learned so much). Written as two words. "So viel wie moglich" (as much as possible). Can also be a conjunction: "Soviel ich weiss" (As far as I know, written as one word).
16. **teach:** desto. Fun info: "The (more)...the." Always paired with "je." "Je mehr man ubt, desto besser wird man" (The more one practices, the better one becomes). The German comparative correlation. Unique structure.
17. **teach:** gleichfalls. Fun info: "Likewise." Gleich (same) + Falls (case). "Danke, gleichfalls!" (Thanks, likewise!). The mirror response. "Schonen Tag!" -- "Gleichfalls!" Polite and automatic.
18. **teach:** je. Fun info: "Ever/each/the (in comparisons)." "Je nachdem" (depending on). "Je langer, desto besser" (the longer, the better). "Je zwei Personen" (two persons each). Tiny word, huge range.
19. **teach:** umso. Fun info: "All the more." Synonym of "desto." "Je mehr, umso besser" (the more, the better). "Umso besser!" (All the better!). Interchangeable with desto in je...desto/umso structures.
20. **tip:** "The Goethe B1 exam: what it proves" -- The Goethe-Zertifikat B1 has four parts: Lesen (65 min), Horen (40 min), Schreiben (60 min), Sprechen (15 min with partner). You need 60% overall to pass. B1 proves you can: understand the main points of clear standard speech, deal with most travel situations, write simple connected text on familiar topics, describe experiences and give opinions with reasons. It is accepted for German citizenship applications and by many employers. Verumius's BESTANDEN means he has proven all of this.
21. **teach:** regeln. Fun info: "To regulate/settle." From Regel (rule). "Das Problem regeln" (to settle the problem). "Geregelt" (regulated). "Das ist geregelt" (That is taken care of). From rule to resolution.
22. **teach:** fassen. Fun info: "To grasp/compose." "Zusammenfassen" (to summarize). "Sich fassen" (to compose oneself). "Einen Entschluss fassen" (to make a decision). To grasp physically and mentally.
23. **teach:** mitteilen. Fun info: Mit (with) + teilen (to share). "To communicate/inform." "Ich teile Ihnen mit, dass..." (I inform you that...). "Mitteilung" (notification). Sharing information formally.
24. **mc:** Demonstrative. q: "Verumius hat letztes Jahr dieselbe Prufung versucht und nicht bestanden. Dieses Mal hat er bestanden. What do 'dieselbe' and 'dieses' specify?" opts: ["The identical exam and this particular time.", "A different exam and an unknown time.", "Any exam and every time.", "The same kind and the last time."] ans: "The identical exam and this particular time." hint: "Dieselbe = identical (not just similar). Dieses = this specific one."
25. **fb:** Comparative correlation. s: "{1} mehr man ubt, desto besser wird man." a: ["Je"] opts: [Je, Wenn, Weil, Da] hint: "The word that starts the German 'the more...the more' structure."
26. **mc:** Unit 23 grammar review. q: "Wenn er hatte mehr uben konnen, hatte er nicht so nervos zu sein brauchen. This sentence combines..." opts: ["Irrealer Bedingungssatz + double infinitive + brauchen + zu", "Perfekt + Prasens + Futur", "Passiv + Konjunktiv I + Imperativ", "Nebensatz + Hauptsatz + Infinitivsatz"] ans: "Irrealer Bedingungssatz + double infinitive + brauchen + zu" hint: "All three Unit 23 grammar constructs in one sentence. Wenn + hatte...konnen (double infinitive in irrealis), hatte + brauchen (brauchen + zu in Konjunktiv II)."
27. **match:** Final pairs. pairs: [{trg:"dasselbe",src:"the same (neuter)"},{trg:"mehrere",src:"several"},{trg:"gleichfalls",src:"likewise"},{trg:"sämtlich",src:"all/entire"}]
28. **drag_fill:** Final comprehensive. s: "{1} er die Prufung bestanden hat, {2} er nun offiziell B1." blanks: {1:"Nachdem",2:"ist"} pool: ["Nachdem","Bevor","ist","hat","wird","Obwohl"] hint: "After he has passed the exam, he IS now officially B1. Nachdem-clause first, then main clause with V2 word order."
