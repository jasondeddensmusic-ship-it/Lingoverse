## Lesson 1: "Nachhaltigkeit im Buro" (Sustainability in the Office)

**Type:** Story intro
**Mode:** Cinematic. Office conference room. Presentation screen showing sustainability charts. Bright daylight from windows. Verumius at the front presenting in German for the first time. Mia in the audience, subtly coaching.
**Characters:** Verumius (presenter, nervous but determined), Mia (audience, proud), Narrator, Colleagues (background)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r22l1
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (18):** Umwelt(die), Umweltschutz(der), Energie(die), Klima(das), Strom(der), Luft(die), Boden(der), Erde(die), Temperatur(die), Hitze(die), Kontrolle(die), System(das), Statistik(die), Durchschnitt(der), steigen, sinken, reduzieren, extrem
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Partizip I as adjective (#26) in narrator sentences ("die steigende Temperatur"). RECYCLED: Konjunktiv II (from U19), Nebensatz word order (A2), Infinitiv mit zu (U19), obwohl/damit/falls (U19)
**Communicative functions:** Present information formally (exposure), describe environmental trends (exposure), express concern about climate issues (exposure)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,839**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Nachhaltigkeit im Buro." Goals: Learn core environmental vocabulary. Witness Verumius presenting in German at work. Understand climate and sustainability topics.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:proud-nervous. "Ein Jahr in Berlin. Verumius steht vor seinen Kollegen und halt eine Prasentation. Auf Deutsch. Mia sitzt in der zweiten Reihe und nickt ermutigend. Sein Thema: Nachhaltigkeit im Buro." / "One year in Berlin. Verumius stands before his colleagues and gives a presentation. In German. Mia sits in the second row, nodding encouragingly. His topic: sustainability in the office."
3. **teach:** Umwelt(die). Fun info: Um (around) + Welt (world). The world around you. Your environment. Germans rank environmental protection as a top political priority. The Umwelt is not just nature. It is everything that surrounds you.
4. **teach:** Umweltschutz(der). Fun info: Umwelt + Schutz (protection). Environmental protection. Germany pioneered the Green Party (Die Grunen) in 1980. Umweltschutz is embedded in Article 20a of the German constitution.
5. **teach:** Energie(die). Fun info: From Greek "energeia" (activity). "Erneuerbare Energie" (renewable energy) is Germany's obsession. The Energiewende (energy transition) aims to phase out nuclear and fossil fuels. Die Energie drives policy.
6. **teach:** Klima(das). Fun info: From Greek "klima" (inclination/region). Das Klima is neuter. "Klimawandel" (climate change) and "Klimaschutz" (climate protection) dominate German news. "Klimaneutral" is the target.
7. **teach:** Strom(der). Fun info: "Electricity" AND "stream/current." From the flowing motion of electric current. "Okostrom" (green electricity) is a real product you can buy. "Der Strom fliesst" works for rivers and wires.
8. **teach:** Luft(die). Fun info: "Air." Cognate with English "loft" (high air). "Frische Luft" (fresh air) is a German prescription for everything. Feeling tired? "Geh an die frische Luft!" Air quality is a major environmental metric.
9. **teach:** Boden(der). Fun info: "Ground/soil/floor." Triple meaning. "Fruchtbarer Boden" (fertile soil), "Erdboden" (ground), "Fussboden" (floor). Related to English "bottom." The foundation of everything.
10. **teach:** Erde(die). Fun info: "Earth/soil." Both the planet and the dirt. Cognate with English "earth." "Die Erde dreht sich" (the earth turns). "In der Erde" (in the soil). Like English, one word for planet and ground.
11. **teach:** Temperatur(die). Fun info: From Latin "temperatura." Germany uses Celsius. 0 degrees is freezing, 20 is comfortable, 30+ is a heatwave. Germans panic at 35 degrees because most homes have no air conditioning.
12. **teach:** Hitze(die). Fun info: "Heat." From "heiss" (hot). "Hitzewelle" (heatwave). Germany's record: 42.6 degrees Celsius in 2019. "Vor Hitze umkommen" (to perish from heat) is how Germans describe summer.
13. **teach:** Kontrolle(die). Fun info: "Control/check/inspection." From French "controle." "Qualitatskontrolle" (quality control), "Passkontrolle" (passport check). Germans love Kontrolle in all forms. Ordnung muss sein.
14. **teach:** System(das). Fun info: From Greek "systema." Das System is neuter. "Das Pfandsystem" (deposit system) is world-famous. Germany recycles 70%+ of packaging. The system works because it is a System.
15. **teach:** Statistik(die). Fun info: From Latin "statisticum" (of the state). Originally meant state data. "Laut Statistik" (according to statistics) is how German presentations begin. Germans trust numbers.
16. **teach:** Durchschnitt(der). Fun info: Durch (through) + Schnitt (cut). A cut through the middle. The average. "Im Durchschnitt" (on average). Literally slicing through data to find the middle. German compound logic at its finest.
17. **teach:** steigen. Fun info: "To rise/climb." Irregular: stieg, gestiegen. "Die Temperaturen steigen" (temperatures are rising). "Einsteigen" (to board), "aussteigen" (to exit). A verb of upward motion in every context.
18. **teach:** sinken. Fun info: "To sink/fall/decrease." Irregular: sank, gesunken. "Die Kosten sinken" (costs are falling). Cognate with English "sink." The opposite of steigen. These two verbs describe every graph.
19. **teach:** reduzieren. Fun info: "To reduce." From Latin "reducere." The -ieren ending marks a borrowed verb. "Emissionen reduzieren" (to reduce emissions). Regular conjugation. International and professional.
20. **teach:** extrem. Fun info: "Extreme." From Latin "extremus." "Extreme Hitze" (extreme heat), "extrem wichtig" (extremely important). Functions as both adjective and intensifying adverb. Germans use it like English "extremely."
21. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:nervous-proud. "Die steigenden Temperaturen sind ein Problem fur alle. Im Durchschnitt verbraucht unser Buro 30 Prozent mehr Strom als notig. Das konnen wir reduzieren." / "The rising temperatures are a problem for everyone. On average our office uses 30 percent more electricity than necessary. We can reduce that." [Exposes Partizip I: "steigenden"]
22. **mc:** Environmental vocabulary. q: "Verumius talks about reducing office electricity. Which German word means electricity?" opts: [Strom, Sturm, Stern, Stein] ans: Strom. hint: "This word also means stream or current. It flows through wires."
23. **fb:** Trend vocabulary. s: "Die Temperaturen {1} jedes Jahr." a: ["steigen"] opts: [steigen, sinken, reduzieren, kontrollieren] hint: "Which verb means to rise? Temperatures go up, not down."
24. **match:** Environment pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Umwelt",src:"environment"},{trg:"Klima",src:"climate"},{trg:"Energie",src:"energy"},{trg:"Durchschnitt",src:"average"}]

## Lesson 2: "Grun denken" (Thinking Green)

**Type:** Vocab teach
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Pure vocabulary across environment, weather, industry, and sustainability domains
**Characters:** None (pure vocabulary)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r22l2
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (20):** Abfall(der), Abfalleimer(der), Mullabfuhr(die), Mulltonne(die), Plastik(das), Kunststoff(der), Holz(das), Metall(das), Material(das), Stoff(der), Ware(die), Handel(der), Handlers(der), Handlerin(die), Export(der), Import(der), Industrie(die), Produktion(die), produzieren, alternativ
**Grammar:** RECYCLED: Accusative/Dative case (A2), adjective declension (A2). EXPOSED: Partizip I (#26) in example sentences ("der wachsende Handel"), Partizip II (#27) in example sentences ("das recycelte Material")
**Communicative functions:** Describe waste and materials (taught), discuss trade and industry (taught), compare environmental options (exposure)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,859**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Grun denken." Goals: Learn waste disposal and recycling vocabulary. Master material and trade words. Build your environmental German toolkit.
2. **teach:** Abfall(der). Fun info: Ab (off/away) + Fall (fall). Something that falls away, waste. "Abfall trennen" (to separate waste) is a German commandment. Every German household sorts into at least four bins.
3. **teach:** Abfalleimer(der). Fun info: Abfall + Eimer (bucket). Waste bucket. The trash can. "Werfen Sie es in den Abfalleimer" (Throw it in the trash). Germans have color-coded Abfalleimer: yellow for packaging, blue for paper, brown for organic.
4. **teach:** Mullabfuhr(die). Fun info: Mull (trash) + Abfuhr (removal). Garbage collection. "Die Mullabfuhr kommt montags" (The garbage truck comes on Mondays). In Germany, you pay per kilogram of trash. Less waste = lower bills.
5. **teach:** Mulltonne(die). Fun info: Mull + Tonne (barrel/bin). The wheelie bin. Every German address has Mulltonnen in four colors. Putting the wrong item in the wrong Tonne is a social crime that neighbors will correct.
6. **teach:** Plastik(das). Fun info: From Greek "plastikos" (moldable). Das Plastik is neuter. Germany banned single-use plastic bags in 2022. "Plastikfrei" (plastic-free) is a lifestyle movement. The Pfand system tackles plastic bottles.
7. **teach:** Kunststoff(der). Fun info: Kunst (art/artificial) + Stoff (material). Literally "artificial material." The formal German word for plastic. "Kunststoffverpackung" (plastic packaging). More technical than Plastik, preferred in industry.
8. **teach:** Holz(das). Fun info: "Wood." Cognate with English "holt" (a small wood). "Aus Holz" (made of wood). Germany's Black Forest (Schwarzwald) is 60% of Baden-Wurttemberg's area. "Holz hacken" (to chop wood) is a German weekend activity.
9. **teach:** Metall(das). Fun info: From Greek "metallon" (mine/quarry). Das Metall is neuter. "Altmetall" (scrap metal). Germany recycles 93% of metal packaging. "Schwermetall" means both heavy metal (chemistry) and heavy metal (music).
10. **teach:** Material(das). Fun info: From Latin "materialis." Das Material is neuter. "Baumaterial" (building material), "Rohmaterial" (raw material). International word, same in dozens of languages. Plural: Materialien.
11. **teach:** Stoff(der). Fun info: "Material/fabric/substance." Triple meaning. "Rohstoff" (raw material), "Werkstoff" (working material), "Baumwollstoff" (cotton fabric). Also: "Unterrichtsstoff" (lesson material). Anything that constitutes something.
12. **teach:** Ware(die). Fun info: "Goods/merchandise." "Waren" (goods, plural). "Hardware" and "Software" were borrowed back from English. "Warenhaus" (department store). Related to English "ware" (as in hardware, silverware).
13. **teach:** Handel(der). Fun info: "Trade/commerce." From "handeln" (to trade/act). "Einzelhandel" (retail), "Grosshandel" (wholesale), "Aussenhandel" (foreign trade). "Fair Handel" (fair trade) is a growing movement.
14. **teach:** Handler(der). Fun info: "Dealer/trader." From Handel. "Autohandler" (car dealer), "Buchhandler" (bookseller). The -er suffix creates the person who does the action. A Handler handles Handel.
15. **teach:** Handlerin(die). Fun info: The feminine form. Handler + -in. "Die Handlerin auf dem Markt" (the female trader at the market). German consistently creates feminine forms with -in for person nouns.
16. **teach:** Export(der). Fun info: From Latin "exportare" (to carry out). "Made in Germany" is the world's most trusted export label. Germany is the third-largest exporter globally. Cars, machines, chemicals. Der Export drives the economy.
17. **teach:** Import(der). Fun info: From Latin "importare" (to carry in). The opposite of Export. "Importware" (imported goods). Germany imports energy, raw materials, and coffee. Germans are Europe's biggest coffee drinkers.
18. **teach:** Industrie(die). Fun info: From Latin "industria" (diligence). "Schwerindustrie" (heavy industry). Germany's Ruhrgebiet was Europe's industrial heart. "Industrie 4.0" is Germany's digital manufacturing strategy.
19. **teach:** Produktion(die). Fun info: From Latin "productio." "Massenproduktion" (mass production). "Die Produktion einstellen" (to stop production). The -tion ending is always feminine in German and always stressed on the last syllable.
20. **teach:** produzieren. Fun info: "To produce." The -ieren verb from Produktion. Regular conjugation. "In Deutschland produziert" (produced in Germany). "Uberproduzieren" (to overproduce). Reliable borrowed verb.
21. **teach:** alternativ. Fun info: "Alternative." From Latin "alternare." "Alternative Energien" (alternative energies), "alternative Medizin" (alternative medicine). "Die Alternative" (the noun) means the alternative option.
22. **mc:** Waste vocabulary. q: "Your German neighbor scolds you for putting a yogurt cup in the wrong bin. Which bin should packaging go in?" opts: [Gelbe Tonne, Blaue Tonne, Braune Tonne, Schwarze Tonne] ans: Gelbe Tonne. hint: "Packaging and Kunststoff go in the yellow bin. Paper is blue, organic is brown."
23. **fb:** Material identification. s: "Dieser Tisch ist aus {1} gemacht." a: ["Holz"] opts: [Holz, Metall, Luft, Handel] hint: "Which material is a table typically made from? Think of trees."
24. **match:** Trade and industry pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Handel",src:"trade"},{trg:"Export",src:"export"},{trg:"Industrie",src:"industry"},{trg:"Produktion",src:"production"}]

## Lesson 3: "Der Pfand-Meister" (The Deposit Master)

**Type:** Story development + grammar introduction
**Mode:** Bubble. REWE Pfand machine. Callback to Unit 4 Scene 1. Verumius feeds bottles smoothly, collects receipt, efficient. Contrast with his U4 confusion. Practiced, confident.
**Characters:** Verumius (confident, practiced), Narrator
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r22l3
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (18):** Abgas(das), Gefahr(die), Katastrophe(die), Krise(die), Schutz(der), Sicherheit(die), Forschung(die), Forderung(die), Umfrage(die), schutzen, warnen, fordern, vermeiden, verursachen, verschmutzen, giftig, okologisch, sparsam
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Partizip I as adjective (#26). Formation: Infinitiv + -d = Partizip I (lachend, steigend, wachsend). Then declines like a normal adjective (der steigende Preis, die wachsende Gefahr, ein sinkendes Schiff). Meaning: ongoing action, equivalent to English -ing. EXPOSED: Partizip II as adjective (#27) in narrator text, lassen causative (#28) in Verumius's dialogue
**Communicative functions:** Describe environmental dangers (taught), express warnings (taught), discuss research and sustainability (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,877**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Der Pfand-Meister." Goals: Learn environmental danger and protection vocabulary. Understand Partizip I as adjective (the rising temperature, the growing danger). See how far Verumius has come since Unit 4.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:proud-comic. "Erinnern Sie sich an Unit 4? Verumius stand ratlos vor dem Pfandautomaten. Heute schiebt er die Flaschen hinein wie ein Profi. Der lachende Verumius sammelt seinen Bon und geht zur Kasse." / "Remember Unit 4? Verumius stood helplessly in front of the deposit machine. Today he feeds the bottles in like a pro. The laughing Verumius collects his receipt and walks to the checkout." [Models Partizip I: "lachende"]
3. **teach:** Abgas(das). Fun info: Ab (away) + Gas. Exhaust gas. "Autoabgase" (car exhaust fumes). The word says what it is: gas that goes away from the engine. "Abgasnormen" (emission standards) are strict in Germany.
4. **teach:** Gefahr(die). Fun info: "Danger." "In Gefahr" (in danger). "Lebensgefahr" (mortal danger). "Gefahrlich" (dangerous). From Old High German "fara" (ambush). Danger originally meant an unexpected attack.
5. **teach:** Katastrophe(die). Fun info: From Greek "katastrophe" (overturning). "Naturkatastrophe" (natural disaster), "Umweltkatastrophe" (environmental disaster). Germans say "Das ist eine Katastrophe!" for any major problem, from floods to late trains.
6. **teach:** Krise(die). Fun info: From Greek "krisis" (decision/turning point). "Klimakrise" (climate crisis), "Wirtschaftskrise" (economic crisis). A Krise is a turning point, not necessarily the end. It demands a decision.
7. **teach:** Schutz(der). Fun info: "Protection." From "schutzen" (to protect). "Umweltschutz" (environmental protection), "Datenschutz" (data protection), "Mutterschutz" (maternity protection). Germans protect everything with a compound.
8. **teach:** Sicherheit(die). Fun info: "Safety/security." From "sicher" (safe/certain). "Verkehrssicherheit" (traffic safety), "Lebensmittelsicherheit" (food safety). The -heit suffix creates abstract nouns. Sicher + heit = the state of being safe.
9. **teach:** Forschung(die). Fun info: "Research." From "forschen" (to research). "Klimaforschung" (climate research), "Grundlagenforschung" (basic research). Germany spends 3%+ of GDP on Forschung. The Fraunhofer institutes are world-famous.
10. **teach:** Forderung(die). Fun info: "Demand/requirement." From "fordern" (to demand). Not to be confused with "fordern" (to promote/support). One Umlaut changes demand into support. "Die Forderung nach Klimaschutz" (the demand for climate protection).
11. **teach:** Umfrage(die). Fun info: Um (around) + Frage (question). Asking around. A survey or poll. "Laut einer Umfrage" (according to a survey). Germans love Umfragen. Every news broadcast cites one.
12. **teach:** schutzen. Fun info: "To protect." "Die Umwelt schutzen" (to protect the environment). "Sich schutzen" (to protect oneself). Regular verb. The u-umlaut gives it its distinctive German sound.
13. **teach:** warnen. Fun info: "To warn." Cognate with English "warn." "Vor Gefahr warnen" (to warn of danger). "Unwetterwarnung" (severe weather warning). Regular verb. The Wetter-app sends Warnungen constantly.
14. **teach:** fordern. Fun info: "To demand." Different from "fordern" (to support). No Umlaut here. "Wir fordern Klimaschutz" (We demand climate protection). Regular verb. The missing dots change the meaning completely.
15. **teach:** vermeiden. Fun info: "To avoid." Irregular: vermied, vermieden. "Plastik vermeiden" (to avoid plastic). "Fehler vermeiden" (to avoid mistakes). The ver- prefix intensifies "meiden" (to shun).
16. **teach:** verursachen. Fun info: "To cause." Ver- + Ursache (cause). "Abgase verursachen Luftverschmutzung" (Exhaust fumes cause air pollution). Regular verb. The compound logic: to cause = to make into a cause.
17. **teach:** verschmutzen. Fun info: "To pollute/dirty." Ver- + Schmutz (dirt). "Die Luft verschmutzen" (to pollute the air). "Umweltverschmutzung" (environmental pollution). Ver- often means "to make into" something.
18. **teach:** giftig. Fun info: "Toxic/poisonous." From "Gift" (poison). "Giftige Chemikalien" (toxic chemicals). Fun false friend: English "gift" = present. German "Gift" = poison. "Giftmull" (toxic waste) is nobody's present.
19. **teach:** okologisch. Fun info: "Ecological." From Greek "oikos" (house) + "logos" (study). "Okologischer Landbau" (organic farming). Often shortened to "oko-" as a prefix: "Okostrom" (green electricity).
20. **teach:** sparsam. Fun info: "Thrifty/economical." From "sparen" (to save). "Sparsam mit Wasser umgehen" (to use water sparingly). Germans consider Sparsamkeit (thriftiness) a virtue. Being sparsam is a compliment.
21. **tip:** "Partizip I as adjective: the -ing form" -- German can turn any verb into an adjective that means "currently doing." Formation: Infinitiv + d.\n\nsteigen (to rise) becomes steigend (rising)\nlachen (to laugh) becomes lachend (laughing)\nwachsen (to grow) becomes wachsend (growing)\n\nThen it declines like any adjective:\n- der steigende Preis (the rising price)\n- die wachsende Gefahr (the growing danger)\n- ein sinkendes Schiff (a sinking ship)\n\nThink of English: "the laughing man" = "der lachende Mann." Same logic, same word order. The Partizip I always describes an ongoing action.
22. **mc:** Partizip I recognition. q: "Die steigenden Temperaturen sind gefahrlich. What does 'steigenden' mean here?" opts: [rising, fallen, extreme, average] ans: rising. hint: "Steigen means to rise. The -d ending plus adjective ending makes it describe an ongoing action."
23. **fb:** Environmental danger. s: "Die wachsende {1} durch Abgase ist ein ernstes Problem." a: ["Gefahr"] opts: [Gefahr, Sicherheit, Forschung, Umfrage] hint: "Which noun means danger? Something that grows and threatens."
24. **mc:** Partizip I formation. q: "How do you form the Partizip I of 'schlafen' (to sleep)?" opts: [schlafend, geschlafen, schlaft, schlafen] ans: schlafend. hint: "Take the infinitive and add -d. The formation is always the same."

## Lesson 4: "Grammatik der Natur" (Grammar of Nature)

**Type:** Grammar
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Grammar instruction with environment and sustainability examples
**Characters:** None (grammar instruction)
**Step count:** 26
**id:** deu_r22l4
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (18):** Fabrik(die), Kraftwerk(das), Anlage(die), Werbung(die), Reklame(die), Marke(die), Modell(das), Faktor(der), Stufe(die), Netz(das), Netzwerk(das), Technik(die), Technologie(die), erhohern, fordern, protestieren, statistisch, durchschnittlich
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Partizip II as adjective (#27) with 4+ quiz steps. Formation: Partizip II used as adjective, declines normally. Meaning: completed action. Contrast with Partizip I (ongoing). TAUGHT: lassen causative (#28) with 4+ quiz steps. Three meanings: have something done, let/allow, leave. sich lassen = passive alternative. PRACTICED: all three constructs interleaved (#26, #27, #28)
**Communicative functions:** Describe completed states (taught), express having something done (taught), contrast ongoing and completed actions (taught), discuss technology and infrastructure (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,895**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Grammatik der Natur." Goals: Master Partizip II as adjective (the recycled paper, the polluted air). Learn lassen causative (I have my bike repaired). Combine all three new constructs.
2. **teach:** Fabrik(die). Fun info: "Factory." From Latin "fabrica" (workshop). "Chemiefabrik" (chemical factory), "Autofabrik" (car factory). The Ruhrgebiet had thousands. Many are now museums or cultural centers.
3. **teach:** Kraftwerk(das). Fun info: Kraft (power) + Werk (work/plant). Power plant. "Atomkraftwerk" (nuclear power plant), "Kohlekraftwerk" (coal power plant), "Windkraftwerk" (wind power plant). Germany is shutting down nuclear ones.
4. **teach:** Anlage(die). Fun info: "Facility/plant/investment." From "anlegen" (to lay out). "Solaranlage" (solar installation), "Klaranlage" (water treatment plant), "Geldanlage" (financial investment). Context determines which meaning.
5. **teach:** Werbung(die). Fun info: "Advertising." From "werben" (to advertise/court). "Werbung machen" (to advertise). "Keine Werbung!" on German mailboxes means no junk mail. Werbung is both the industry and the ads.
6. **teach:** Reklame(die). Fun info: From French "reclame." Advertising, but slightly old-fashioned or pejorative. "Reklame machen" sounds less professional than "Werbung machen." Used for flashy or aggressive ads.
7. **teach:** Marke(die). Fun info: "Brand/stamp." "Briefmarke" (postage stamp), "Automarke" (car brand). "Deutsche Marken" are globally trusted. The word comes from "markieren" (to mark). A brand marks its products.
8. **teach:** Modell(das). Fun info: "Model." From Italian "modello." Das Modell is neuter. "Geschaftsmodell" (business model), "Automodell" (car model). "Ein Modell fur Nachhaltigkeit" (a model for sustainability).
9. **teach:** Faktor(der). Fun info: "Factor." From Latin "factor" (maker). "Ein wichtiger Faktor" (an important factor). "Risikofaktor" (risk factor). International word, same in most European languages.
10. **teach:** Stufe(die). Fun info: "Step/level/stage." "Vorstufe" (preliminary stage), "Entwicklungsstufe" (development stage). Also literally a stair step. "Vorsicht, Stufe!" (Watch out, step!) is on signs everywhere.
11. **teach:** Netz(das). Fun info: "Net/network." Cognate with English "net." "Internet" (internet), "Stromnetz" (power grid), "Netzwerk" (network). "Ins Netz gehen" (to go into the net) means to go online. Also: to fall into a trap.
12. **teach:** Netzwerk(das). Fun info: Netz + Werk (work). "Soziales Netzwerk" (social network). "Ein Netzwerk aufbauen" (to build a network). Professional networking is "Netzwerken." Germans turned it into a verb.
13. **teach:** Technik(die). Fun info: "Technology/technique." From Greek "techne" (art/skill). "Spitzentechnik" (cutting-edge technology). "Informationstechnik" (IT). "Technik" emphasizes practical application, "Technologie" the science.
14. **teach:** Technologie(die). Fun info: Technik + -logie (study of). "Umwelttechnologie" (environmental technology), "Biotechnologie" (biotechnology). More academic and theoretical than Technik. The -ie ending is always stressed.
15. **teach:** erhohen. Fun info: "To increase/raise." Er- + hoh (high). "Die Temperatur erhohen" (to increase the temperature). "Die Preise erhohen" (to raise prices). Transitive: someone raises something. Compare: steigen (intransitive, something rises on its own).
16. **teach:** fordern. Fun info: "To promote/support." WITH Umlaut, unlike fordern (to demand). "Erneuerbare Energien fordern" (to promote renewable energies). "Forderung" (funding/support). One dot changes demand to support.
17. **teach:** protestieren. Fun info: "To protest." From Latin "protestari." Regular -ieren verb. "Gegen den Klimawandel protestieren" (to protest against climate change). "Fridays for Future" started protests worldwide, including Germany.
18. **teach:** statistisch. Fun info: "Statistical." From Statistik. "Statistisch gesehen" (statistically speaking). The -isch ending creates adjectives from nouns. "Statistisch signifikant" (statistically significant).

2. **teach:** Protest(der). Fun info: From Latin "protestari" (to declare publicly). "Klimaprotest" (climate protest). "Gegen etwas protestieren" (to protest against something). "Fridays for Future" made Berlin a global protest capital.
3. **teach:** Alternative(die). Fun info: From Latin "alternare." "Eine Alternative finden" (to find an alternative). "Es gibt keine Alternative" (there is no alternative). "Alternativlos" was Germany's un-word of the year 2010.
4. **teach:** Klimaanlage(die). Fun info: Klima + Anlage (system/facility). Air conditioning. Rare in German homes. "Brauchen wir eine Klimaanlage?" is the summer debate. Most Germans say no. Open the window. Fresh air beats everything.
5. **teach:** Hersteller(der). Fun info: Her (here/toward) + stellen (to place). Manufacturer. "Automobilhersteller" (car manufacturer). "Vom Hersteller empfohlen" (recommended by the manufacturer). The one who places something into existence.
6. **teach:** Feuerwehr(die). Fun info: Feuer (fire) + Wehr (defense). Fire defense = fire department. "Die Feuerwehr rufen" (to call the fire department). Call 112. German volunteer fire departments have 1 million members. Heroes in every village.
7. **teach:** Umweltverschmutzung(die). Fun info: Umwelt + Verschmutzung (pollution). Environmental pollution. 22 letters. A compound noun that carries the weight of the problem. "Umweltverschmutzung reduzieren" (to reduce environmental pollution).
8. **teach:** Zunder(der). Fun info: "Tinder/igniter." From "zunden" (to ignite). "Zundstoff" (explosive material, also figuratively: a hot topic). The dating app is named after this concept: something that ignites.
9. **teach:** Zundholz(das). Fun info: Zund (ignite) + Holz (wood). A match. Synonym of Streichholz. Southern German prefers Zundholz, northern prefers Streichholz. Same object, regional word choice.
10. **teach:** Form(die). Fun info: "Form/shape." From Latin "forma." "In Form" (in shape/fit), "Formular" (form to fill out, from U5). "Die Form wahren" (to keep up appearances). A word that spans fitness, bureaucracy, and etiquette.
11. **teach:** entsorgen. Fun info: Ent- (away from) + sorgen (to care). To dispose of. "Mull entsorgen" (to dispose of trash). "Sondermull entsorgen" (to dispose of hazardous waste). The ent- prefix removes the care: you send it away.
12. **teach:** schaden. Fun info: "To damage/harm." "Der Umwelt schaden" (to harm the environment). Takes dative. "Rauchen schadet der Gesundheit" (Smoking damages health). Also a noun: der Schaden (the damage).
13. **teach:** vergrossern. Fun info: "To enlarge/increase." Ver- + gross (big). "Das Bild vergrossern" (to enlarge the image). "Den Abstand vergrossern" (to increase the distance). The opposite: verkleinern (to make smaller).
14. **teach:** verbrennen. Fun info: "To burn (up)." Ver- + brennen. Irregular: verbrannte, verbrannt. "Mull verbrennen" (to incinerate trash). "Sich verbrennen" (to burn oneself). The ver- prefix means to completion: burned up entirely.
15. **teach:** elektr-/elektro-(affix). Fun info: From Greek "elektron" (amber). "Elektrisch" (electric), "Elektroauto" (electric car), "Elektronik" (electronics). Germany's Elektromobilitat (e-mobility) push aims for 15 million e-cars by 2030.
16. **teach:** oko-(affix). Fun info: From Greek "oikos" (house/household). "Okologie" (ecology), "Okostrom" (green electricity), "Okosystem" (ecosystem). The prefix that turns everything green. "Oko" by itself is slang for an environmentalist.
17. **teach:** uber-(affix). Fun info: "Over/above/beyond." "Uberdurchschnittlich" (above average), "uberall" (everywhere), "ubertreiben" (to exaggerate). The most productive German prefix. It intensifies everything it touches.
18. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:reflective. "Ein Jahr in Berlin. Der verlorene Tourist vom Flughafen ist jetzt ein Berliner, der seinen Mull trennt, sein Fahrrad reparieren lasst und jeden Pfandautomaten in Kreuzberg kennt. Die nachste Stufe wartet: die Prufung." / "One year in Berlin. The lost tourist from the airport is now a Berliner who separates his trash, has his bike repaired, and knows every deposit machine in Kreuzberg. The next level awaits: the exam." [Partizip II "verlorene," lassen causative "reparieren lasst," hooks U23]
19. **mc:** Full-unit vocabulary. q: "Umweltverschmutzung, Klimaanlage, Hersteller. What domain do these words all belong to?" opts: [Environment and industry, Food and cooking, Travel and transport, Health and medicine] ans: Environment and industry. hint: "All three relate to nature, production, or environmental impact."
20. **fb:** Partizip II in story context. s: "Der {1} Tourist vom Flughafen ist jetzt ein richtiger Berliner." a: ["verlorene"] opts: [verlorene, verloren, verlierender, Verlierer] hint: "The tourist WAS lost. Completed action as adjective. Partizip II + adjective ending."
21. **mc:** lassen causative review. q: "Verumius lasst sein Fahrrad reparieren. Who does the actual repair?" opts: [A mechanic or bike shop, Verumius himself, Mia, Nobody] ans: A mechanic or bike shop. hint: "Causative lassen means having someone ELSE do the action. Verumius arranges it."
22. **tip:** "German environmental culture" -- Germany's environmental consciousness runs deep.\n\nPfand: Deposit system for bottles. 25 cents per plastic, 8 cents per glass. Return rate: 98%.\nMulltrennung: Waste separation. Four bins minimum. Neighbors will correct you.\nEnergiewende: The shift to renewable energy. Germany aims for 80% renewable electricity by 2030.\nBio: Organic products have a large market share. The Bio-Siegel (organic seal) is trusted.\n\nWhen Mia says "Du bist ein richtiger Berliner geworden," she means more than geography. Separating trash, saving energy, returning bottles: these are acts of belonging.
