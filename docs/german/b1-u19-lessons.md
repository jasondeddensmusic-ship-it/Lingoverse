## Lesson 1: "Wartenummer B089" (Queue Number B089)

**Type:** Story intro + vocabulary
**Mode:** Cinematic -- Auslanderbehorde waiting room, fluorescent lights, queue ticket machine, rows of plastic chairs
**Characters:** Verumius (nervous but prepared), unnamed waiting room occupants (background)
**Step count:** 22
**id:** deu_r19l1
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (16):** Behorde(die), Amt(das), Dokument(das), Antrag(der), Visum(das), Aufenthalt(der), Unterlage(die), Frist(die), nötig, rechtzeitig, zunächst, vorläufig, sicher, danach, Bestätigung(die), Meldung(die)
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Passive with modals (#9 -- heard in narrator/signage), subordinating conjunctions (#18 -- bevor/nachdem in story narration). RECYCLED: Perfekt, indirect questions (from U16), Nebensatz word order
**Communicative functions:** Navigating bureaucratic settings (exposure), describing document requirements (exposure), expressing readiness/nervousness
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,450**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Wartenummer B089" -- Goals: Learn key bureaucratic vocabulary. Understand how German immigration offices work. Follow Verumius through the waiting room of the Auslanderbehorde.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:tense -- Verumius sits in the Auslanderbehorde waiting room. Queue number B089. The digital display shows A247. His folder is perfectly organized. Hildi helped.
3. **teach:** Behorde(die) -- Fun info: From "beherrschen" (to govern). A Behorde is any government authority. The Auslanderbehorde is the foreigners' authority. Germans have a Behorde for everything.
4. **teach:** Amt(das) -- Fun info: From Gothic "andbahti" (service). An Amt is an office or bureau. Burgeramt, Finanzamt, Standesamt. German bureaucracy runs on Amter. Das Amt is neuter, like most institutions.
5. **teach:** Dokument(das) -- Fun info: From Latin "documentum" (lesson/proof). Nearly identical in English. In Germany, you need Dokumente for everything: renting, banking, working, even getting a library card.
6. **teach:** Antrag(der) -- Fun info: An- (toward) + tragen (to carry). Literally "to carry toward" the authorities. An Antrag is a formal application. "Einen Antrag stellen" (to file an application) is survival vocabulary in Germany.
7. **teach:** Visum(das) -- Fun info: From Latin "visum" (something seen/approved). Das Visum, plural: Visa. Every non-EU resident in Germany needs one. The Aufenthaltstitel (residence permit) replaces it long-term.
8. **teach:** Aufenthalt(der) -- Fun info: Auf- (up/upon) + enthalten (to hold). A stay or residence. Aufenthaltserlaubnis = residence permit. Aufenthaltstitel = residence title. German compound words build meaning layer by layer.
9. **teach:** Unterlage(die) -- Fun info: Unter (under) + Lage (position). Something that lies underneath, supporting your case. Die Unterlagen (plural) means "documents/paperwork." Every Behorde asks: "Haben Sie alle Unterlagen?"
10. **teach:** Frist(die) -- Fun info: From Old High German "frist" (time period). A deadline. "Die Frist lauft ab" (the deadline expires). Missing a Frist in German bureaucracy has real consequences. Always ask: "Wann ist die Frist?"
11. **teach:** nötig -- Fun info: From "Not" (need/necessity). Something that is needed. "Es ist nötig, alle Dokumente mitzubringen." Stronger than "brauchen" (to need) because it implies urgency or obligation.
12. **teach:** rechtzeitig -- Fun info: Recht (right) + zeitig (timely). On time, punctual. Germans value Pünktlichkeit (punctuality). Arriving rechtzeitig at the Behorde means arriving early. Late = your appointment is gone.
13. **teach:** zunächst -- Fun info: Zu (to) + nachst (nearest). "First of all" or "initially." A formal connector. "Zunachst mussen Sie das Formular ausfullen." You hear this word constantly in official instructions.
14. **teach:** vorläufig -- Fun info: Vor (before) + laufig (running). Provisional, temporary. A vorlaufige Genehmigung is a temporary approval. Many German permits start vorlaufig before becoming endgultig (final).
15. **teach:** sicher -- Fun info: From Latin "securus" (without care). Means both "safe" and "certain." "Ich bin sicher" = "I am sure." "Das ist sicher" = "That is certain/safe." Context determines which.
16. **teach:** danach -- Fun info: Da (there) + nach (after). "After that." A pronominal adverb. "Zunachst das Formular, danach die Gebuhr bezahlen." Germans love sequencing with zunachst...danach...schliesslich.
17. **teach:** Bestätigung(die) -- Fun info: From "bestatigen" (to confirm). A confirmation. "Konnen Sie mir eine Bestatigung geben?" Every official action in Germany produces a Bestatigung. Paper proof is king.
18. **teach:** Meldung(die) -- Fun info: From "melden" (to report/register). A registration or report. The Anmeldung (residential registration) is the first Meldung every resident needs. No Meldung = no bank account, no insurance, no phone contract.
19. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:reflective -- Verumius checks his folder one more time. Geburtsurkunde, Meldebescheinigung, Arbeitsvertrag, Krankenversicherung, Passfotos. Everything is there. Hildi's checklist system works.
20. **mc:** Vocabulary -- q: "Verumius hat eine Wartenummer. Wo ist er?" opts: [In der Behorde, Im Restaurant, In der Schule, Im Krankenhaus] ans: In der Behorde. hint: "A place where you take a number and wait for government services."
21. **fb:** Sequencing -- s: "Verumius muss {1} alle Unterlagen sammeln." a: ["zunachst"] opts: [zunachst, danach, trotzdem, wahrend] hint: "Which word means 'first of all' when listing steps?"
22. **mc:** Context -- q: "Verumius hat eine vorlaufige Genehmigung. Was bedeutet das?" opts: [Die Genehmigung ist temporar., Die Genehmigung ist endgultig., Die Genehmigung ist abgelaufen., Die Genehmigung ist falsch.] ans: Die Genehmigung ist temporar.. hint: "Vorlaufig means something is not yet permanent."

## Lesson 2: "Amtsdeutsch" (Official German)

**Type:** Vocab teach (bureaucracy + legal + family)
**Mode:** Bubble -- no scene, pure vocabulary
**Characters:** None (vocabulary instruction)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r19l2
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (18):** Gebühr(die), Urkunde(die), Gesetz(das), Recht(das), Pflicht(die), Herkunft(die), Integration(die), Migration(die), Migrant(der), Migrantin(die), Verwaltung(die), Wohnort(der), Wohnsitz(der), Daten, rechtlich, endgültig, verantwortlich, zuständig
**Grammar:** RECYCLED: Genitive (des Gesetzes, der Behorde), compound nouns, Nebensatz word order. EXPOSED: Passive with modals (#9 -- "Das muss beantragt werden" in examples)
**Communicative functions:** Understanding official terminology, describing legal requirements, discussing immigration status
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,468**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Amtsdeutsch" -- Goals: Master bureaucratic vocabulary. Understand legal terms used at the Auslanderbehorde. Learn words for citizenship, duties, and rights.
2. **teach:** Gebühr(die) -- Fun info: From "gebuhren" (to be due). A fee charged by the government. Die Bearbeitungsgebuhr (processing fee) is attached to every Antrag. Germany never does paperwork for free.
3. **teach:** Urkunde(die) -- Fun info: From Old High German "urchundi" (testimony). An official certificate. Geburtsurkunde (birth certificate), Heiratsurkunde (marriage certificate). Original documents only. Copies will not do.
4. **teach:** Gesetz(das) -- Fun info: From "setzen" (to set/place). A law. Something that is "set" in place. "Gesetz ist Gesetz" (the law is the law) is a very German phrase. Das Grundgesetz is the German constitution.
5. **teach:** Recht(das) -- Fun info: Same root as English "right." Both a right (das Recht auf...) and the legal system (das Recht). "Sie haben das Recht auf einen Aufenthaltstitel" means you are legally entitled to one.
6. **teach:** Pflicht(die) -- Fun info: From Old High German "pflihti" (care/duty). An obligation. Die Schulpflicht (compulsory education), die Versicherungspflicht (mandatory insurance). Germany runs on Pflichten.
7. **teach:** Herkunft(die) -- Fun info: Her- (from) + Kunft (coming). Origin, where you come from. "Herkunftsland" = country of origin. The Behorde always asks about Herkunft. Not the same as Heimat (which is emotional).
8. **teach:** Integration(die) -- Fun info: Latin "integratio" (renewal). The process of becoming part of society. Germany has Integrationskurse (integration courses): 600 hours of German + 100 hours of civics. Mandatory for many immigrants.
9. **teach:** Migration(die) -- Fun info: Latin "migratio" (moving). The broader concept of population movement. Immigration (Einwanderung) and emigration (Auswanderung) are both types of Migration.
10. **teach:** Migrant(der) / Migrantin(die) -- Fun info: A person who migrates. German always has masculine and feminine forms: der Migrant, die Migrantin. The plural "Migranten" covers everyone. Gender-specific titles are standard in official German.
11. **teach:** Verwaltung(die) -- Fun info: From "verwalten" (to administer). Administration. Die Stadtverwaltung = city administration. Germany's Verwaltung is legendarily thorough. Every process has a Verwaltungsakt (administrative act).
12. **teach:** Wohnort(der) -- Fun info: Wohn- (dwelling) + Ort (place). Your place of residence. On every form. Different from Wohnsitz (official registered address). You can have a Wohnort without a Wohnsitz if you have not registered yet.
13. **teach:** Wohnsitz(der) -- Fun info: Wohn- (dwelling) + Sitz (seat). Your officially registered address. The Anmeldung (registration) establishes your Wohnsitz. Without it, you legally do not live anywhere in Germany.
14. **teach:** Daten -- Fun info: Latin "data" (things given). Always plural in German. Personliche Daten = personal data. Name, Geburtsdatum, Adresse. Data protection (Datenschutz) is a near-sacred concept in Germany.
15. **teach:** rechtlich -- Fun info: Recht + -lich. Legal, juridical. "Rechtlich gesehen..." (legally speaking...) is how officials introduce legal requirements. Different from "recht" (right/correct) and "rechts" (to the right).
16. **teach:** endgültig -- Fun info: End- (final) + gultig (valid). Final, definitive. An endgultiger Bescheid (final decision) cannot be appealed through normal channels. The opposite of vorlaufig. The word you want to hear at the Behorde.
17. **teach:** verantwortlich -- Fun info: Ver- + Antwort (answer) + -lich. Literally "answerable." Responsible. "Wer ist dafur verantwortlich?" (Who is responsible for that?) The Behorde always wants to know who is verantwortlich.
18. **teach:** zuständig -- Fun info: Zu- (to) + Stand (state/position) + -ig. Competent, responsible (jurisdictionally). "Diese Behorde ist fur Visa zustandig." Each office is zustandig for specific matters. Wrong Behorde? They send you to the zustandige one.
19. **tip:** "Amtsdeutsch: The language of German bureaucracy" -- German official language uses long compound nouns (Aufenthaltsgenehmigung), passive constructions (wird bearbeitet), and formal vocabulary. It sounds different from everyday German. Key pattern: nouns are built from smaller parts. Aufenthalts-genehmigungs-antrag = residence-permit-application. Read right to left for meaning.
20. **mc:** Legal vocabulary -- q: "Welches Wort bedeutet 'deadline'?" opts: [Frist, Pflicht, Recht, Gebühr] ans: Frist. hint: "This word refers to a time limit for submitting something."
21. **mc:** Compound meaning -- q: "Was ist ein Wohnsitz?" opts: [Die offizielle Meldeadresse, Ein bequemer Stuhl, Der Arbeitsplatz, Das Heimatland] ans: Die offizielle Meldeadresse. hint: "Wohn- relates to living, Sitz relates to an official seat or registration."
22. **fb:** Official context -- s: "Die Ausländerbehörde ist für Visa {1}." a: ["zuständig"] opts: [zuständig, verantwortlich, endgültig, rechtlich] hint: "Which adjective describes jurisdictional responsibility of an office?"
23. **match:** Bureaucratic pairs -- pairs: [{trg:"Gebühr",src:"fee"},{trg:"Urkunde",src:"certificate"},{trg:"Gesetz",src:"law"},{trg:"Pflicht",src:"duty"},{trg:"Verwaltung",src:"administration"}]
24. **mc:** Combo -- q: "Verumius braucht eine Geburtsurkunde. Was ist das?" opts: [Ein offizielles Dokument uber die Geburt, Ein Formular fur die Schule, Eine Bestatigung der Adresse, Ein Foto fur den Pass] ans: Ein offizielles Dokument uber die Geburt. hint: "Geburt means birth. Urkunde means certificate."

## Lesson 3: "Das muss gemacht werden" (That Must Be Done)

**Type:** Grammar -- Passive with modals (#9)
**Mode:** Bubble -- learning mode, immigration office context
**Characters:** None (grammar instruction)
**Step count:** 22
**id:** deu_r19l3
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (14):** beantragen, genehmigen, ausstellen, verlängern, anerkennen, erhalten, enthalten, verpflichten, fällig, zusätzlich, untersagt, voraussichtlich, inklusive, einschließlich
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Passive with modals (#9). Modal + Partizip II + werden. All modals: muss/kann/soll/darf/will + PP + werden. RECYCLED: Passive Prasens (#7 from U14), modal verbs (#3 from U5)
**Communicative functions:** Expressing obligation (must be done), permission (can be done), prohibition (must not be done), expectation (should be done)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,482**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Das muss gemacht werden" -- Goals: Master passive voice with modal verbs. Express what must, can, should, and must not be done. Use bureaucratic German with confidence.
2. **teach:** beantragen -- Fun info: Be- + Antrag + -en. To apply for officially. "Einen Aufenthaltstitel beantragen" is what Verumius is doing right now. The be- prefix makes it transitive: you beantragen something directly.
3. **teach:** genehmigen -- Fun info: From "genehm" (agreeable). To approve, to grant permission. "Der Antrag wurde genehmigt" is the sentence every applicant wants to hear. Die Genehmigung = the approval.
4. **teach:** ausstellen -- Fun info: Aus- (out) + stellen (to place). To issue (a document). "Einen Pass ausstellen" = to issue a passport. The Behorde stellt Dokumente aus. Without ausstellen, no official paper exists.
5. **teach:** verlängern -- Fun info: Ver- + langer (longer) + -n. To extend, to renew. "Das Visum verlangern" = to extend the visa. Every permit has an expiry date. Verlangern before the Frist. Always.
6. **teach:** anerkennen -- Fun info: An- (at) + erkennen (to recognize). To officially recognize. "Einen Abschluss anerkennen" = to recognize a degree. Foreign qualifications must be anerkannt (recognized) in Germany before they count.
7. **teach:** erhalten -- Fun info: Er- + halten (to hold). To receive. Formal register. "Sie erhalten den Bescheid per Post." In official German, erhalten replaces bekommen (to get). Same meaning, higher register.
8. **teach:** enthalten -- Fun info: Ent- + halten (to hold). To contain. "Der Antrag enthalt alle Unterlagen." Not to be confused with erhalten (receive). Enthalten = what is inside. Erhalten = what you get.
9. **teach:** verpflichten -- Fun info: Ver- + Pflicht + -en. To obligate. "Sie sind verpflichtet, einen Integrationskurs zu besuchen." When the Behorde says verpflichtet, it is not a suggestion. It is the law.
10. **teach:** fällig -- Fun info: From "fallen" (to fall due). Due, payable. "Die Gebuhr ist sofort fallig." When something is fallig, the deadline has arrived. No more waiting.
11. **teach:** zusätzlich -- Fun info: Zu- (to) + Satz (set) + -lich. Additional. "Zusatzliche Dokumente sind erforderlich." The word you do not want to hear at the Behorde, because it means more paperwork.
12. **teach:** untersagt -- Fun info: Unter- + sagen (to say). Forbidden, prohibited. Stronger than "verboten." "Das Arbeiten ist ohne Genehmigung untersagt." Official German prefers untersagt in formal documents.
13. **teach:** voraussichtlich -- Fun info: Voraus (ahead) + sichtlich (visibly). Presumably, expected. "Die Bearbeitung dauert voraussichtlich sechs Wochen." Bureaucratic hedging: they expect it, but no guarantees.
14. **teach:** inklusive / einschließlich -- Fun info: Both mean "including." Inklusive is from Latin (casual). Einschliesslich is the German compound (formal). "Inklusive Bearbeitungsgebuhr" or "einschliesslich aller Unterlagen." Same meaning, different register.
15. **tip:** "Passive with modals: who does what to what?" -- You already know passive (wird + Partizip II). Now add a modal verb. Structure: Modal (position 2) + Partizip II + werden (end). "Das Formular muss ausgefullt werden." (The form must be filled out.) All modals work: muss (must), kann (can), soll (should), darf (may), will (wants to). The modal takes V2 position. Werden goes to the end. The Partizip II sits right before werden.

## Lesson 4: "Bevor die Frist abläuft" (Before the Deadline Expires)

**Type:** Grammar -- Subordinating conjunctions expanded (#18)
**Mode:** Bubble -- learning mode, document preparation context
**Characters:** None (grammar instruction)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r19l4
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (16):** obwohl, trotzdem, bevor, seitdem, bis, damit, falls, während, abhängen, abstimmen, umgehen, sichern, Zugang(der), Rufnummer(die), Zivilstand(der), Original(das)
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Subordinating conjunctions expanded (#18): obwohl, bevor, nachdem (review), wahrend, seitdem, bis, damit, falls. Trotzdem as adverbial contrast. RECYCLED: Nebensatz word order (verb-final from U9), als vs wenn (#19 from U18), nachdem + Plusquamperfekt (#20 from U16)
**Communicative functions:** Expressing conditions (falls), purpose (damit), temporal sequences (bevor/nachdem/seitdem/wahrend/bis), concession (obwohl/trotzdem)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,498**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Bevor die Frist abläuft" -- Goals: Master 8 new subordinating conjunctions. Express time, purpose, condition, and concession. Build complex German sentences for official contexts.
2. **teach:** obwohl -- Fun info: Ob (whether) + wohl (well). "Although." Introduces a concession. "Obwohl er nervos war, hatte er alle Dokumente." Verb goes to the END of the obwohl-clause. Always.
3. **teach:** trotzdem -- Fun info: Trotz (despite) + dem (that). "Nevertheless." NOT a conjunction but an adverb. "Er war nervos. Trotzdem hatte er alle Dokumente." Trotzdem takes V2 position in its clause. Contrast with obwohl: obwohl = verb-final, trotzdem = verb-second.
4. **teach:** bevor -- Fun info: Be- + vor (before). "Before." "Bevor die Frist ablauft, mussen alle Dokumente eingereicht werden." The bevor-clause describes what has NOT happened yet. Verb-final.
5. **teach:** seitdem -- Fun info: Seit (since) + dem (that). "Since (a point in time)." "Seitdem ich in Berlin wohne, lerne ich Deutsch." Marks the starting point of an ongoing situation. Verb-final.
6. **teach:** bis -- Fun info: Old High German "biz." "Until." "Warten Sie, bis Ihre Nummer aufgerufen wird." Marks the endpoint. Short, powerful, used constantly in bureaucratic instructions.
7. **teach:** damit -- Fun info: Da- (there) + mit (with). As a conjunction: "so that, in order that." "Bringen Sie alle Unterlagen mit, damit der Antrag bearbeitet werden kann." Different subjects allowed (unlike um...zu). Verb-final.
8. **teach:** falls -- Fun info: From "Fall" (case). "In case, if." "Falls Sie Fragen haben, rufen Sie uns an." More uncertain than "wenn." Falls = it might happen. Wenn = it probably will. Formal German prefers falls.
9. **teach:** während -- Fun info: From "wahren" (to last). "While, during." As a conjunction: "Wahrend der Beamte die Dokumente pruft, wartet Verumius." Temporal overlap. Also a preposition with genitive: wahrend des Gesprachs.
10. **teach:** abhängen -- Fun info: Ab- (from) + hangen (to hang). "To depend on." "Es hangt davon ab, ob alle Unterlagen vollstandig sind." Always used with "von": von etwas abhangen. The outcome is "hanging from" a condition.
11. **teach:** abstimmen -- Fun info: Ab- (off) + stimmen (to be correct/to vote). "To coordinate, to align." "Die Unterlagen mussen aufeinander abgestimmt sein." In bureaucracy, everything must be abgestimmt (aligned, coordinated).
12. **teach:** umgehen -- Fun info: Um- (around) + gehen (to go). Two meanings depending on stress. "UMgehen" (separable) = to handle/deal with. "umGEHen" (inseparable) = to circumvent. "Wie geht man mit der Verwaltung um?" = How do you deal with the administration?
13. **teach:** sichern -- Fun info: From "sicher" (safe/certain). "To secure, to safeguard." "Sichern Sie Ihre Daten." In bureaucratic context: make copies, store safely, have backups. Related to Sicherheit (security).
14. **teach:** Zugang(der) -- Fun info: Zu- (to) + Gang (way/passage). Access. "Zugang zum Arbeitsmarkt" = access to the job market. The Aufenthaltstitel determines your Zugang to many things in Germany.
15. **teach:** Rufnummer(die) -- Fun info: Ruf (call) + Nummer (number). Phone number. More formal than Telefonnummer. On official forms, you always see Rufnummer. Die -- feminine, because Nummer is feminine.
16. **teach:** Zivilstand(der) -- Fun info: Zivil (civil) + Stand (status). Marital status. Swiss/Austrian term; Germany uses Familienstand more often. Both appear on forms. Ledig, verheiratet, geschieden, verwitwet.
17. **teach:** Original(das) -- Fun info: From Latin "originalis." The original document, not a copy. "Bringen Sie bitte das Original mit." German offices almost always require Originale. Copies are nur in Ausnahmefallen (only exceptionally) accepted.
18. **tip:** "Subordinating conjunctions: verb to the end" -- All subordinating conjunctions push the conjugated verb to the END of their clause. Obwohl er nervos WAR. Bevor die Frist ABLAUFT. Wahrend der Beamte die Dokumente PRUFT. The main clause keeps normal V2 order. When the subordinate clause comes first, the main clause verb comes immediately after the comma: "Bevor die Frist ablauft, MUSS man den Antrag einreichen." deepDive: Trotzdem is NOT a subordinating conjunction. It is an adverb and keeps V2 word order: "Trotzdem HAT er alle Dokumente." Compare: "Obwohl er nervos war, hatte er alle Dokumente" vs. "Er war nervos. Trotzdem hatte er alle Dokumente." Same meaning, different structure.
