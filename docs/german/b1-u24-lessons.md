## Lesson 1: "Ein Jahr" (One Year)

**Type:** Story intro + reflection
**Mode:** Cinematic. Verumius's apartment, golden morning light, Fernsehturm visible through the window. A calendar on the wall with today circled. Coffee in hand. The city that was once foreign is now home.
**Characters:** Verumius (protagonist, reflective), Narrator
**Step count:** 24
**New vocabulary (16):** Abenteuer(das), Alltag(der), Beginn(der), Eile(die), Ereignis(das), Gewohnheit(die), Rückkehr(die), Sinn(der), Tatsache(die), Wahrheit(die), Zeitpunkt(der), Zustand(der), verbringen, vermissen, gewöhnen, alltäglich
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Plusquamperfekt (#1) in narrator's past-of-past sentences. RECYCLED: Präteritum narrative (from U15-U16), Perfekt (from A2), Nebensatz word order (weil/dass from A2), Konjunktiv II (from U22-U23)
**Communicative functions:** Reflect on past experiences (exposure), describe daily routines and habits (practiced), express feelings about change (exposure)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,191**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Ein Jahr." Goals: Reflect on Verumius's year in Germany. Learn vocabulary for experiences, habits, and truth. Hear the Plusquamperfekt for the first time in narration.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:warm-reflective. "Genau ein Jahr. Verumius steht am Fenster, Kaffee in der Hand, und schaut auf den Fernsehturm. Vor einem Jahr hatte er diesen Turm zum ersten Mal gesehen und nichts verstanden. Jetzt ist er Teil seiner Gewohnheit." / "Exactly one year. Verumius stands at the window, coffee in hand, and looks at the Fernsehturm. A year ago he had seen this tower for the first time and understood nothing. Now it is part of his routine." [Exposes Plusquamperfekt naturally: hatte gesehen]
3. **teach:** Abenteuer(das). Fun info: From Old French "aventure" (what comes to you). The same root as English "adventure." Das Abenteuer is neuter. "Ein Abenteuer erleben" (to experience an adventure). Verumius's whole year has been one.
4. **teach:** Alltag(der). Fun info: All (every) + Tag (day). Your everyday life. "Im Alltag" (in daily life). When the extraordinary becomes Alltag, you have truly arrived. Verumius's German Alltag now includes Brezeln and Fahrplane.
5. **teach:** Beginn(der). Fun info: From "beginnen" (to begin). "Am Beginn" or "zu Beginn" (at the beginning). "Von Beginn an" (from the start). The noun strips the verb down to its essence: the moment something starts.
6. **teach:** Eile(die). Fun info: "Hurry/haste." "In Eile sein" (to be in a hurry). "Eile mit Weile" (more haste, less speed) is a beloved German proverb. Die Eile is feminine. Verumius has learned that Berlin does not reward Eile.
7. **teach:** Ereignis(das). Fun info: From "sich ereignen" (to occur). An event, an occurrence. "Ein wichtiges Ereignis" (an important event). Stronger than "Veranstaltung" (organized event). An Ereignis happens to you.
8. **teach:** Gewohnheit(die). Fun info: From "gewohnt" (accustomed). A habit. "Aus Gewohnheit" (out of habit). "Die Macht der Gewohnheit" (the force of habit). Humans are Gewohnheitstiere (creatures of habit). Germans love this compound.
9. **teach:** Rückkehr(die). Fun info: Rück (back) + Kehr (turn). Return. "Die Rückkehr nach Hause" (the return home). But what if home is no longer where you came from? That is the question Verumius faces today.
10. **teach:** Sinn(der). Fun info: "Sense/meaning/purpose." "Das macht Sinn" (that makes sense). Also: the five Sinne (senses). "Sinnvoll" (meaningful), "sinnlos" (pointless). A tiny word carrying enormous philosophical weight.
11. **teach:** Tatsache(die). Fun info: Tat (deed/fact) + Sache (thing). A fact. "Die Tatsache, dass..." (the fact that...). Built from actions and things. German facts are solid: they are deed-things, proven by what happened.
12. **teach:** Wahrheit(die). Fun info: From "wahr" (true). "Die Wahrheit sagen" (to tell the truth). "In Wahrheit" (in truth/actually). The -heit suffix turns adjectives into abstract nouns. Wahr becomes Wahrheit. True becomes truth.
13. **teach:** Zeitpunkt(der). Fun info: Zeit (time) + Punkt (point). A point in time, a moment. "Der richtige Zeitpunkt" (the right moment). Germans are precise about time. Not just the hour, but the Zeitpunkt.
14. **teach:** Zustand(der). Fun info: Zu (to) + Stand (state/standing). A condition, a state. "In gutem Zustand" (in good condition). "Der Zustand der Wohnung" (the condition of the apartment). How things stand right now.
15. **teach:** verbringen. Fun info: "To spend (time)." Ver- intensifies "bringen" (to bring). You bring time into an activity. "Zeit verbringen" (to spend time). "Ich habe ein Jahr in Berlin verbracht" uses Perfekt. Irregular: verbrachte, verbracht.
16. **teach:** vermissen. Fun info: "To miss (someone/something)." "Ich vermisse dich" (I miss you). Regular verb. Different from "verpassen" (to miss a bus). Vermissen is emotional absence. Verpassen is practical failure.
17. **teach:** gewöhnen. Fun info: "To get used to." Reflexive: "sich an etwas gewöhnen" (to get used to something). "Ich habe mich daran gewöhnt" (I have gotten used to it). The root of Gewohnheit. Verumius has sich an Berlin gewöhnt.
18. **teach:** alltäglich. Fun info: "Everyday/ordinary." From Alltag + -lich. "Alltägliche Dinge" (everyday things). What was once extraordinary has become alltäglich. That is the meaning of calling a foreign city home.
19. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:contemplative. "Er hatte so viel gelernt. Nicht nur die Sprache, sondern auch die Gewohnheiten, die Regeln, die kleinen Wahrheiten des Alltags. Es war ein Abenteuer gewesen, und die Tatsache, dass er geblieben war, überraschte ihn selbst." / "He had learned so much. Not just the language, but also the habits, the rules, the small truths of daily life. It had been an adventure, and the fact that he had stayed surprised even himself." [Plusquamperfekt: hatte gelernt, war gewesen]
20. **tip:** "Plusquamperfekt: a first look" -- You just heard "hatte gelernt" and "war gewesen" in the story. This is the Plusquamperfekt (past perfect). It describes something that happened BEFORE another past event.\n\nFormula: hatte/war (Präteritum) + Partizip II (at the end).\n\n- Er hatte die Stadt gesehen. (He had seen the city.)\n- Sie war schon gegangen. (She had already gone.)\n\nWe will practice this grammar in Lesson 3 and 4.
21. **mc:** Vocabulary meaning. q: "Verumius reflects on his Jahr in Berlin. What does 'Alltag' mean?" opts: [everyday life, adventure, beginning, habit] ans: everyday life. hint: "This compound noun combines 'all' (every) with 'Tag' (day)."
22. **fb:** Context completion. s: "Verumius hat sich an das Leben in Berlin {1}." a: ["gewöhnt"] opts: [gewöhnt, vermisst, verbracht, begonnen] hint: "Which verb means to get accustomed to something? Reflexive with 'sich.'"
23. **mc:** Narrative comprehension. q: "The narrator says Verumius 'hatte so viel gelernt.' When did this learning happen?" opts: [Before the moment described in the story, Right now at the window, In the future, Yesterday] ans: Before the moment described in the story. hint: "The Plusquamperfekt describes events that happened before another past event."
24. **match:** Year-in-review vocabulary. pairs: [{trg:"Abenteuer",src:"adventure"},{trg:"Gewohnheit",src:"habit"},{trg:"Wahrheit",src:"truth"},{trg:"Zeitpunkt",src:"point in time"}]

## Lesson 2: "Rückblick" (Looking Back)

**Type:** Vocab teach
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Pure vocabulary: emotions, decisions, and reflection words
**Characters:** None (pure vocabulary)
**Step count:** 22
**New vocabulary (18):** Entscheidung(die), Chance(die), Gelegenheit(die), Möglichkeit(die), Überraschung(die), Überzeugung(die), Zweifel(der), Zufall(der), Wert(der), Wunder(das), Schritt(der), Fall(der), beschließen, entscheiden, realisieren, wundern, ähnlich, tatsächlich
**Grammar:** RECYCLED: Perfekt (examples use past events), Nebensatz (dass/weil in example sentences), adjective declension (A2). EXPOSED: Plusquamperfekt in examples
**Communicative functions:** Express decisions and doubts (taught), describe coincidences and surprises (taught), evaluate importance and worth (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,209**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Rückblick." Goals: Learn vocabulary for decisions, chances, and doubts. Master words for reflecting on important moments. Build the emotional vocabulary Verumius needs for his anniversary.
2. **teach:** Entscheidung(die). Fun info: From "entscheiden" (to decide). Ent- (un-/dis-) + scheiden (to separate). A decision separates one path from another. "Eine Entscheidung treffen" (to make a decision). The most important Entscheidung: nach Deutschland zu kommen.
3. **teach:** Chance(die). Fun info: From French "chance." Pronounced with a soft "sh" sound in German, not a hard "ch." "Eine Chance nutzen" (to seize a chance). "Zweite Chance" (second chance). Germans use the French pronunciation: "SHON-seh."
4. **teach:** Gelegenheit(die). Fun info: "Opportunity." From "gelegen" (convenient/situated). An opportunity that presents itself. "Bei Gelegenheit" (when the opportunity arises). More deliberate than Chance. A Gelegenheit is there if you look for it.
5. **teach:** Möglichkeit(die). Fun info: From "möglich" (possible). A possibility. "Es gibt viele Möglichkeiten" (there are many possibilities). -keit suffix from -lich adjectives. Möglich becomes Möglichkeit. Possible becomes possibility.
6. **teach:** Überraschung(die). Fun info: From "überraschen" (to surprise). Über (over) + raschen (to rush). Something that rushes over you. "Eine Überraschungsparty" (a surprise party). Verumius is planning one, in reverse: the surprise is for him.
7. **teach:** Überzeugung(die). Fun info: From "überzeugen" (to convince). A conviction, a belief. "Aus Überzeugung" (out of conviction). Stronger than Meinung (opinion). An Überzeugung is something you would defend.
8. **teach:** Zweifel(der). Fun info: From "zwei" (two). Doubt literally means "being in two minds." Same etymology as English "doubt" (from Latin "dubitare," also from "duo"/two). "Ohne Zweifel" (without doubt). "Im Zweifel" (when in doubt).
9. **teach:** Zufall(der). Fun info: Zu (to) + Fall (fall/case). A coincidence. Something that falls to you by chance. "Durch Zufall" (by coincidence). "Es war kein Zufall" (it was no coincidence). Was it Zufall that Verumius met Yilmaz on the wrong train?
10. **teach:** Wert(der). Fun info: "Value/worth." "Es ist viel wert" (it is worth a lot). "Wertvoll" (valuable), "wertlos" (worthless). Same root as English "worth." The Wert of a year in Berlin: not measurable in money.
11. **teach:** Wunder(das). Fun info: "Wonder/miracle." Cognate with English "wonder." "Kein Wunder" (no wonder). "Es grenzt an ein Wunder" (it borders on a miracle). "Sich wundern" (to be amazed). Das Wunder: neuter, like most Ge-less abstract nouns.
12. **teach:** Schritt(der). Fun info: "Step." From "schreiten" (to stride). "Schritt für Schritt" (step by step). "Der erste Schritt" (the first step). Verumius's first Schritt in Germany was at BER airport. Now he has made thousands.
13. **teach:** Fall(der). Fun info: "Case/fall." "Auf jeden Fall" (in any case). "Auf keinen Fall" (in no case/no way). "Im Fall, dass..." (in case that...). Also means a literal fall. Grammar cases (Nominativ, Akkusativ) are also Fälle.
14. **teach:** beschließen. Fun info: "To decide/resolve." Be- + schließen (to close). To close the matter. Stronger than entscheiden. A Beschluss is final. "Ich habe beschlossen zu bleiben" (I have decided to stay). Irregular: beschloss, beschlossen.
15. **teach:** entscheiden. Fun info: "To decide." Ent- + scheiden (to separate). To separate the options. Reflexive: "sich entscheiden" (to make up one's mind). "Ich kann mich nicht entscheiden" (I cannot decide). Irregular: entschied, entschieden.
16. **teach:** realisieren. Fun info: "To realize/to implement." Two meanings: "Ich habe realisiert, dass..." (I realized that...) and "einen Plan realisieren" (to implement a plan). The -ieren ending marks it as borrowed from French. Regular verb.
17. **teach:** wundern. Fun info: "To wonder/to be surprised." Reflexive: "sich wundern" (to be surprised). "Es wundert mich" (it surprises me). "Das wundert mich nicht" (that does not surprise me). From Wunder. Regular verb.
18. **teach:** ähnlich. Fun info: "Similar." "Ähnlich wie..." (similar to...). "Sie sehen sich ähnlich" (they look similar to each other). From Old High German "ana-līh" (having the same appearance). The Umlaut makes it unmistakably German.
19. **teach:** tatsächlich. Fun info: "Actually/indeed." From Tatsache (fact) + -lich. Factual, real. "Tatsächlich?" (Really?). Germans use this to express genuine surprise: "Er ist tatsächlich geblieben?" (He actually stayed?).
20. **mc:** Decision vocabulary. q: "Verumius has to choose between staying and leaving. Which two words both mean 'to decide'?" opts: [entscheiden and beschließen, wundern and realisieren, vermissen and verbringen, ähnlich and tatsächlich] ans: entscheiden and beschließen. hint: "Both verbs involve separating or closing options."
21. **fb:** Doubt and certainty. s: "Er hatte keine {1} mehr. Berlin war sein Zuhause." a: ["Zweifel"] opts: [Zweifel, Chance, Wunder, Schritt] hint: "Which noun means doubt? Its root is the number two."
22. **match:** Reflection pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Entscheidung",src:"decision"},{trg:"Möglichkeit",src:"possibility"},{trg:"Überraschung",src:"surprise"},{trg:"Zufall",src:"coincidence"}]

## Lesson 3: "Bevor Berlin" (Before Berlin)

**Type:** Story development + grammar (Plusquamperfekt)
**Mode:** Bubble. Verumius's apartment, morning. He is looking through old photos on his phone, scrolling back through the year. Each memory is a Plusquamperfekt sentence.
**Characters:** Verumius (protagonist, nostalgic), Narrator
**Step count:** 22
**New vocabulary (15):** Dauer(die), Fortsetzung(die), Mal(das), Vergleich(der), Art(die), hinterlassen, lohnen, erfüllen, vorkommen, einzig, besonder, gesamt, gewöhnlich, positiv, negativ
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Plusquamperfekt (#1) with hatte + Partizip II and war + Partizip II. PRACTICED: 4+ quiz steps on Plusquamperfekt. RECYCLED: Perfekt (contrast with Plusquamperfekt), Nebensatz word order (bevor/nachdem), Präteritum
**Communicative functions:** Narrate sequences of past events (taught), compare past and present (taught), express what had already happened (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,224**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Bevor Berlin." Goals: Master the Plusquamperfekt (past perfect). Learn to describe what had happened before something else. Compare past events using correct tense sequences.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:nostalgic. "Verumius scrollt durch alte Fotos. Vor einem Jahr hatte er noch kein Wort Deutsch gesprochen. Er hatte niemanden in Berlin gekannt. Und er hatte nicht gewusst, dass diese Stadt sein Leben verändern würde." / "Verumius scrolls through old photos. A year ago he had not spoken a single word of German. He had known nobody in Berlin. And he had not known that this city would change his life." [Three Plusquamperfekt examples]
3. **teach:** Dauer(die). Fun info: "Duration." From "dauern" (to last). "Auf Dauer" (in the long run). "Die Dauer des Aufenthalts" (the duration of the stay). How long something lasts. Verumius's stay has no set Dauer anymore.
4. **teach:** Fortsetzung(die). Fun info: From "fortsetzen" (to continue). Fort (onward) + setzen (to set). A continuation. "Fortsetzung folgt" (to be continued). Every unit of this course has been a Fortsetzung of Verumius's story.
5. **teach:** Mal(das). Fun info: "Time (as in instance)." "Das erste Mal" (the first time). "Zum ersten Mal" (for the first time). "Jedes Mal" (every time). Different from "Zeit" (duration). Mal counts occurrences.
6. **teach:** Vergleich(der). Fun info: From "vergleichen" (to compare). "Im Vergleich zu" (in comparison with). "Ein Vergleich hinkt" (a comparison limps = is flawed). Comparing his first day to today: kein Vergleich.
7. **teach:** Art(die). Fun info: "Type/kind/manner." "Auf diese Art" (in this way). "Eine Art von..." (a kind of...). Also means "species" in biology. "Auf seine Art" (in his own way). Die Art: a small word for a big concept.
8. **teach:** hinterlassen. Fun info: "To leave behind." Hinter (behind) + lassen (to leave). "Spuren hinterlassen" (to leave traces). "Eine Nachricht hinterlassen" (to leave a message). Irregular: hinterließ, hinterlassen. Every experience hinterlässt its mark.
9. **teach:** lohnen. Fun info: "To be worthwhile." Reflexive: "Es lohnt sich" (it is worth it). "Es hat sich gelohnt" (it was worth it). From "Lohn" (reward). When effort leads to reward. Verumius's year: Es hat sich gelohnt.
10. **teach:** erfüllen. Fun info: "To fulfill." Er- (completion) + füllen (to fill). To fill completely. "Einen Traum erfüllen" (to fulfill a dream). "Sich erfüllen" (to come true). When something fills you up entirely.
11. **teach:** vorkommen. Fun info: "To occur/to seem." Vor (before) + kommen (to come). "Das kommt mir bekannt vor" (that seems familiar to me). "Das kommt vor" (that happens). Separable. "Es kam ihm vor, als ob..." (It seemed to him as if...).
12. **teach:** einzig. Fun info: "Only/sole." From "ein" (one). "Der einzige Grund" (the only reason). "Einzigartig" (unique, literally one-of-a-kind). "Kein Einziger" (not a single one). The ultimate one.
13. **teach:** besonder. Fun info: "Special/particular." "Etwas Besonderes" (something special). "Besonders" (especially). "Im Besonderen" (in particular). No direct English cognate, but the concept is universal. Today is a besonderer Tag.
14. **teach:** gesamt. Fun info: "Entire/total." "Die gesamte Stadt" (the entire city). "Insgesamt" (altogether). "Gesamtkosten" (total costs). Stronger than "ganz" (whole). Gesamt means every single part included.
15. **teach:** gewöhnlich. Fun info: "Usually/ordinary." From Gewohnheit. "Gewöhnlich stehe ich um sieben auf" (I usually get up at seven). "Etwas ganz Gewöhnliches" (something quite ordinary). But today is anything but gewöhnlich.
16. **teach:** positiv. Fun info: International word. "Positiv denken" (to think positively). "Ein positives Ergebnis" (a positive result). Germans use it exactly like English. Verumius's Einstellung (attitude) toward Berlin: positiv.
17. **teach:** negativ. Fun info: "Negative." "Negativ bewerten" (to evaluate negatively). "Negative Erfahrungen" (negative experiences). Used in medicine too: "Der Test ist negativ" (the test is negative). International vocabulary, German pronunciation.
18. **tip:** "Plusquamperfekt: The past before the past" -- The Plusquamperfekt describes an event that happened BEFORE another past event. Think of it as "the deeper past."\n\nFormula: hatte/war (Präteritum of haben/sein) + Partizip II (at the end)\n\nWith hatte (most verbs):\n- Er hatte Deutsch gelernt. (He had learned German.)\n- Sie hatte das Buch gelesen. (She had read the book.)\n\nWith war (movement/change of state verbs, same as Perfekt):\n- Er war nach Berlin geflogen. (He had flown to Berlin.)\n- Sie war eingeschlafen. (She had fallen asleep.)\n\nCompare tenses:\n- Perfekt: Er hat Deutsch gelernt. (He learned German.) -- just past\n- Plusquamperfekt: Er hatte Deutsch gelernt. (He had learned German.) -- before another past event\n\nThe key signal words: bevor (before), nachdem (after), als (when), schon (already).
19. **mc:** Plusquamperfekt formation. q: "How do you say 'She had already left' in German?" opts: [Sie war schon gegangen, Sie hat schon gegangen, Sie ist schon gegangen, Sie hatte schon gegangen] ans: Sie war schon gegangen. hint: "'Gehen' uses sein in Perfekt. In Plusquamperfekt, sein becomes war."
20. **fb:** Plusquamperfekt with hatte. s: "Bevor er nach Berlin kam, {1} er kein Deutsch gesprochen." a: ["hatte"] opts: [hatte, hat, war, ist] hint: "'Sprechen' uses haben. The Plusquamperfekt form of haben is..."
21. **mc:** Tense contrast. q: "Which sentence describes something that happened BEFORE Verumius arrived in Berlin?" opts: [Er hatte noch nie Currywurst gegessen, Er hat gestern Currywurst gegessen, Er isst gerade Currywurst, Er wird Currywurst essen] ans: Er hatte noch nie Currywurst gegessen. hint: "The Plusquamperfekt shows the deeper past, before another past event."
22. **drag_fill:** Plusquamperfekt sentence. s: "Nachdem er das Formular {1} {2}, ging er zum Amt." blanks: {1:"ausgefüllt",2:"hatte"} pool: ["ausgefüllt","hatte","hat","war","ausfüllen"] hint: "In Nebensatz word order, the helper verb goes to the very end. Partizip II comes just before it."

## Lesson 4: "Zukunftsmusik" (Pipe Dreams)

**Type:** Grammar (Plusquamperfekt mastery + Futur I introduction)
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Grammar instruction with story-themed examples
**Characters:** None (grammar instruction)
**Step count:** 24
**New vocabulary (14):** Zukunft(die), Planung(die), Zweck(der), fortsetzen, vorhaben, vorschlagen, entschließen, zukünftig, weiter, optimistisch, vernünftig, sinnvoll, sinnlos, wohl
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Futur I (#2) with werden + Infinitiv. Formation, usage, and distinction from present tense for future. PRACTICED: Plusquamperfekt (#1) with 3+ quiz steps. PRACTICED: Futur I with 3+ quiz steps. REVIEWED: Adverbial conjunctions (#33) deshalb/trotzdem in examples
**Communicative functions:** Express plans and intentions (taught), make predictions (taught), talk about the future (taught), give reasons for decisions (practiced)
**Cumulative vocab after: 3,238**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Zukunftsmusik." Goals: Master Futur I (werden + Infinitiv). Solidify the Plusquamperfekt. Learn vocabulary for future plans and decisions. Distinguish between past-before-past and future.
2. **teach:** Zukunft(die). Fun info: Zu (to) + Kunft (coming). What is coming toward you. "In Zukunft" (in the future). "Zukunftsmusik" (literally future-music) means pipe dreams or castles in the air. But Verumius's Zukunft is very real.
3. **teach:** Planung(die). Fun info: From "planen" (to plan). -ung suffix creates the noun. "Stadtplanung" (city planning). "Die Planung steht" (the plan is set). Germans love a good Planung. Spontaneity makes them nervous.
4. **teach:** Zweck(der). Fun info: "Purpose." "Zu diesem Zweck" (for this purpose). "Zwecklos" (pointless). "Der Zweck heiligt die Mittel" (the end justifies the means). From Middle High German "zwec" (nail/target). The point you aim for.
5. **teach:** fortsetzen. Fun info: "To continue." Fort (onward) + setzen (to set). Separable: "Er setzt seine Reise fort" (He continues his journey). "Wird fortgesetzt" (to be continued). The Fortsetzung of the Abenteuer.
6. **teach:** vorhaben. Fun info: "To intend/plan." Vor (before) + haben (to have). "Was hast du heute vor?" (What are your plans today?). Separable. "Ich habe vor zu bleiben" (I intend to stay). Having something before you: a plan in mind.
7. **teach:** vorschlagen. Fun info: "To suggest." Vor (before) + schlagen (to hit/strike). To strike forward with an idea. "Ich schlage vor, dass..." (I suggest that...). Separable. Irregular: schlug vor, vorgeschlagen. KD would never vorschlagen anything informal.
8. **teach:** entschließen. Fun info: "To resolve/decide firmly." Ent- + schließen (to close/lock). To unlock a decision. Reflexive: "sich entschließen" (to make up one's mind). Stronger than entscheiden. "Ich habe mich entschlossen" = the decision is locked.
9. **teach:** zukünftig. Fun info: "Future (adjective)." From Zukunft + -ig. "Zukünftige Generationen" (future generations). "Mein zukünftiger Plan" (my future plan). The adjective form of what is coming.
10. **teach:** weiter. Fun info: "Further/continued." "Und so weiter" (and so on, abbreviated usw.). "Weitermachen" (to carry on). "Weiterhin" (furthermore/still). A word that always points forward. "Wie geht es weiter?" (What happens next?).
11. **teach:** optimistisch. Fun info: From Latin "optimus" (best). "Ich bin optimistisch" (I am optimistic). International word, German pronunciation with final "-isch." Verumius war am Beginn nicht optimistisch. Jetzt schon.
12. **teach:** vernünftig. Fun info: "Reasonable/sensible." From "Vernunft" (reason). "Eine vernünftige Entscheidung" (a sensible decision). "Sei vernünftig!" (Be reasonable!). Germans value Vernunft highly. It is the practical cousin of Weisheit (wisdom).

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Ich werde bleiben." Goals: Complete the emotional arc. Master the final vocabulary. Hear Verumius's decision. Get a glimpse of what B2 holds.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:quiet-triumphant. "Die Gäste sind gegangen. Die Lichterketten leuchten noch. Auf dem Tisch stehen die Geschenke: Hildis Teetasse, Lukas' Mixtape, Mias Wein, Yilmaz' Roman, und KDs Kaktus. Der kleine Bruder von Paragraph. 3. **teach:** Augenblick(der). Fun info: Auge (eye) + Blick (glance). A moment, an instant. The time it takes for one glance. "Einen Augenblick bitte" (one moment please). "Im Augenblick" (at the moment). This Augenblick, in this courtyard, is the one Verumius will remember forever.
4. **teach:** besetzen. Fun info: "To occupy/to fill (a position)." Be- + setzen (to set). "Der Platz ist besetzt" (the seat is taken). "Eine Stelle besetzen" (to fill a position). Verumius hat seinen Platz in Berlin besetzt. Nobody else can sit there now.
5. **teach:** abschreiben. Fun info: "To copy/to write off." Ab (off) + schreiben (to write). "Schreib das nicht ab!" (Don't write that off!). "Von jemandem abschreiben" (to copy from someone, like cheating). Separable. Verumius's old life: abgeschrieben. New chapter begins.
6. **teach:** verwechseln. Fun info: "To confuse/mix up." Ver- + wechseln (to change/exchange). "Verwechsle mich nicht mit einem Touristen" (Don't confuse me with a tourist). Regular verb. Verumius wird nicht mehr mit einem Anfänger verwechselt.
7. **teach:** vermuten. Fun info: "To suspect/assume." Ver- + Mut (courage, originally: mind/thought). "Ich vermute, dass..." (I suspect that...). "Vermutlich" (presumably). To form a thought about something uncertain. Regular verb.
8. **teach:** zweifeln. Fun info: "To doubt." From Zweifel (doubt). "An etwas zweifeln" (to doubt something). "Ich zweifle nicht daran" (I do not doubt it). Regular verb. Verumius hat aufgehört zu zweifeln. Berlin ist sein Zuhause.
9. **teach:** wert. Fun info: "Worth (adjective)." "Es ist es wert" (It is worth it). "Der Mühe wert" (worth the effort). Different from der Wert (noun). The adjective needs an object: etwas ist X wert. War dieses Jahr es wert? Ja.
10. **teach:** wertlos. Fun info: Wert + -los (without). "Worthless." "Wertloser Kram" (worthless junk). The -los suffix strips away value. But nothing from this year was wertlos. Every struggle had its Sinn.
11. **teach:** wertvoll. Fun info: Wert + voll (full). "Valuable." Full of worth. "Wertvolle Erinnerungen" (valuable memories). "Wertvoll" and "wertlos" are perfect antonyms. The same root, opposite suffixes.
12. **teach:** zufällig. Fun info: From Zufall (coincidence). "By chance/coincidental." "Zufällig" is also used to ask politely: "Weißt du zufällig...?" (Do you happen to know...?). "Es war nicht zufällig" (It was not by chance). Meeting Yilmaz: zufällig. Bleiben: nicht zufällig.
13. **teach:** zuverlässig. Fun info: "Reliable." Zu + Verlässig (dependable). "Ein zuverlässiger Freund" (a reliable friend). "Zuverlässig wie eine Schweizer Uhr" (reliable as a Swiss watch). Hildi: zuverlässig. KD: überraschend zuverlässig.
14. **teach:** los. Fun info: "Loose/off/going." "Los!" (Go!/Let's go!). "Was ist los?" (What's the matter?). "Es geht los" (It's starting). As a prefix: losfahren, loslassen, loswerden. The starting gun of German life. Also: "Das werde ich nicht los" (I cannot get rid of that).
15. **teach:** durcheinander. Fun info: Durch (through) + einander (each other). "Mixed up/confused." "Alles ist durcheinander" (Everything is mixed up). Also a noun: "ein Durcheinander" (a mess/chaos). Verumius's first months: ein einziges Durcheinander. Now: Ordnung.
16. **teach:** äußerlich. Fun info: From "äußer" (outer/external). "Externally/outwardly." "Äußerlich ruhig" (outwardly calm). The opposite of "innerlich" (inwardly). "Äußerliche Veränderungen" (external changes). Berlin has changed Verumius: nicht nur äußerlich, sondern auch innerlich.
17. **teach:** -weis / -weise. Fun info: Suffix meaning "in the manner of." Creates adverbs: "normalerweise" (normally), "möglicherweise" (possibly), "ausnahmsweise" (exceptionally). One of German's most productive suffixes. Once you see it, you see it everywhere. Recognizing -weise unlocks dozens of adverbs.
18. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:proud-quiet. "Vor einem Jahr hatte er nichts verstanden. Jetzt versteht er fast alles. Nicht nur die Sprache, sondern auch die Stadt, die Menschen, die Regeln, die kleinen Wunder des Alltags. Er hatte gezweifelt, ob er es schaffen würde. Er hatte es geschafft." / "A year ago he had understood nothing. Now he understands almost everything.
