## Lesson 1: "Das Bohrloch" (The Drill Hole)

**Type:** Story intro
**Mode:** Cinematic. Verumius's apartment, Sunday evening 20:05, warm lamplight, half-hung shelf, drill on table, clock prominent, note sliding under door
**Characters:** Verumius (protagonist), Narrator, Neighbor (unseen, heard through ceiling thumps)
**Step count:** 24
**New vocabulary (20):** Streit(der), Ärger(der), Konflikt(der), Laune(die), Stimmung(die), Nerv(der), Sorge(die), Vorwurf(der), Vorschrift(die), Verbot(das), Strafe(die), wütend, ärgerlich, ernst, still, schreien, schweigen, klagen, streiken, bloß
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: nachdem + Plusquamperfekt (#20) in narrator sentences. RECYCLED: Nebensatz word order (weil/dass from A2), Perfekt (from A2)
**Communicative functions:** Express annoyance (exposure), describe conflict (exposure), understand rules and regulations (exposure)
**Cumulative vocab after: 1,978**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Das Bohrloch". Goals: Learn conflict and emotion vocabulary. Discover German Ruhezeit (quiet hours). Witness Verumius's first neighborhood dispute.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:scene-setting. "Sonntagabend, 20:05 Uhr. Verumius hat eine Bohrmaschine in der Hand und ein Regal an der Wand. Alles ist ruhig. Fast." / "Sunday evening, 8:05 PM. Verumius has a drill in his hand and a shelf on the wall. Everything is quiet. Almost."
3. **teach:** Streit(der). Fun info: From Old High German "strit" (battle). The word covers everything from a lovers' quarrel to a legal dispute. "Einen Streit haben" (to have an argument) is heard in every German apartment building.
4. **teach:** Ärger(der). Fun info: Related to "arg" (bad/severe). Der Arger is trouble, annoyance, anger rolled into one. "Arger machen" means to cause trouble. The Umlaut dots make it hiss like frustration itself.
5. **teach:** Konflikt(der). Fun info: Borrowed from Latin "conflictus" (a striking together). Germans use it for serious disputes. A Streit can be small; a Konflikt is structural. Office politics, international relations, neighbor wars.
6. **teach:** Laune(die). Fun info: "Mood." "Gute Laune" (good mood), "schlechte Laune" (bad mood). From Latin "luna" (moon). The idea that moods change like moon phases. "Launisch" means moody.
7. **teach:** Stimmung(die). Fun info: "Atmosphere/mood." From "Stimme" (voice). The mood that a group or room creates together. "Die Stimmung ist gut" means the vibe is right. Concert reviews always discuss Stimmung.
8. **teach:** Nerv(der). Fun info: "Nerve." "Du gehst mir auf die Nerven" (You are getting on my nerves) is THE German complaint phrase. Borrowed from Latin "nervus." Plural: Nerven. Every neighbor dispute involves Nerven.
9. **teach:** Sorge(die). Fun info: "Worry/concern." "Keine Sorge!" (No worries!) is comforting. "Sich Sorgen machen" (to worry) uses the reflexive. Die Sorge is feminine, like most abstract nouns ending in -e.
10. **teach:** Vorwurf(der). Fun info: "Accusation/reproach." Vor (before) + Wurf (throw). You throw something in front of someone. "Jemandem Vorwurfe machen" (to make accusations). The classic weapon in any German Streit.
11. **teach:** Vorschrift(die). Fun info: "Regulation/rule." Vor (before) + Schrift (writing). Something written down beforehand. Germans are famous for Vorschriften. The Hausordnung (house rules) is a book of Vorschriften.
12. **teach:** Verbot(das). Fun info: "Prohibition/ban." From "verbieten" (to forbid). A Verbot is absolute: no exceptions. "Rauchverbot" (smoking ban), "Parkverbot" (parking ban). German compounds with Verbot are everywhere.
13. **teach:** Strafe(die). Fun info: "Punishment/penalty." "Zur Strafe" (as punishment). Geldstrafe = fine. Haftstrafe = prison sentence. The Ruhezeit violation Verumius just committed? In theory, that is a Strafe-worthy offense.
14. **teach:** wutend. Fun info: "Furious/angry." From "Wut" (rage). Stronger than argerlich. "Wutend werden" (to become furious). The neighbor above is probably wutend right now.
15. **teach:** argerlich. Fun info: "Annoying/irritating." From Arger. Can describe the person who is annoyed OR the thing that causes annoyance. "Das ist argerlich" (That is annoying). Dual meaning, context decides.
16. **teach:** ernst. Fun info: "Serious." "Im Ernst?" (Seriously?). Also a common first name (Ernst). "Die Lage ist ernst" (The situation is serious) is how German news anchors begin bad news.
17. **teach:** still. Fun info: "Quiet/silent." "Sei still!" (Be quiet!) is what every German child hears. Also: "stille Nacht" (Silent Night), the Christmas carol written in Austria. Still water = Stilles Wasser (non-carbonated).
18. **teach:** schreien. Fun info: "To scream/shout." Irregular: schrie, geschrien. From the same root as English "shriek." "Schrei nicht so!" (Do not shout like that!) is neighbor-dispute standard vocabulary.
19. **teach:** schweigen. Fun info: "To be silent/stay quiet." Irregular: schwieg, geschwiegen. The opposite of schreien. "Schweigen ist Gold" (Silence is golden). A verb that means NOT making sound. Very German: a verb for doing nothing.
20. **teach:** klagen. Fun info: "To complain/to sue." Same word for both. "Uber etwas klagen" (complain about) vs "gegen jemanden klagen" (sue someone). In Germany, complaining and suing share linguistic DNA.
21. **teach:** streiken. Fun info: "To go on strike." From English "strike." Germans borrowed it during the Industrial Revolution. Deutsche Bahn strikes are legendary. "Die Nachbarn streiken" could mean they are on a noise protest.
22. **teach:** blos. Fun info: "Just/merely/only." "Was soll ich blos tun?" (What on earth should I do?). As a Modalpartikel, it adds desperation or emphasis. Verumius is thinking this right now.
23. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:tense. "Nachdem Verumius die Bohrmaschine abgestellt hatte, klopfte es an der Tur." / "After Verumius had turned off the drill, there was a knock at the door." [Exposes nachdem + Plusquamperfekt naturally]
24. **mc:** Emotion vocabulary. q: "Verumius's neighbor is angry about the noise. Which word describes someone who is furious?" opts: [wutend, still, ernst, blos] ans: wutend. hint: "This word comes from Wut, meaning rage."

## Lesson 2: "Die Hausordnung" (The House Rules)

**Type:** Vocab teach
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Pure vocabulary in conflict/complaint/law domain
**Characters:** None (pure vocabulary)
**Step count:** 24
**New vocabulary (20):** Schuld(die), Schuldig, schuld, Kritik(die), Respekt(der), Geduld(die), Kompromiss(der), Verstandnis(das), Stimme(die), Gedanke(der), kritisieren, beleidigen, unterbrechen, widersprechen, verlangen, behaupten, frech, peinlich, merkwurdig, seltsam
**Grammar:** RECYCLED: Nebensatz with weil/dass (A2), adjective declension (A2). EXPOSED: Indirect questions (#21) in example sentences
**Communicative functions:** Express opinions (taught), agree/disagree (taught), make complaints (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 1,998**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Die Hausordnung." Goals: Learn vocabulary for arguments, complaints, and conflict resolution. Master words for blame, criticism, and compromise.
2. **teach:** Schuld(die). Fun info: "Guilt/fault/debt." Three meanings in one word. "Wer hat Schuld?" (Whose fault is it?). "Schulden" (debts) is the plural. "Entschuldigung" literally means "un-guilting." You already know that word.
3. **teach:** schuldig. Fun info: "Guilty." "Er ist schuldig" (He is guilty). Used in courtrooms and arguments. The opposite: unschuldig (innocent). The -ig ending turns the noun Schuld into an adjective.
4. **teach:** schuld. Fun info: "At fault" (predicative only). "Er ist schuld" (He is to blame). Unlike "schuldig," this form is only used with "sein." No endings, no declension. A stripped-down blame word.
5. **teach:** Kritik(die). Fun info: "Criticism." From Greek "kritike" (the art of judging). "Kritik uben" (to exercise criticism) is the formal phrase. Germans value "konstruktive Kritik" (constructive criticism) in the workplace.
6. **teach:** Respekt(der). Fun info: "Respect." Same in English, French, and a dozen other languages. "Respekt haben vor" (to have respect for). "Respekt!" on its own is slang for "impressive!"
7. **teach:** Geduld(die). Fun info: "Patience." From "dulden" (to endure). "Geduld ist eine Tugend" (Patience is a virtue). The opposite: Ungeduld (impatience). German bureaucracy requires enormous Geduld.
8. **teach:** Kompromiss(der). Fun info: "Compromise." From Latin "compromissum." In German apartment disputes, the Kompromiss is king. "Einen Kompromiss finden" (to find a compromise) resolves most Streit situations.
9. **teach:** Verstandnis(das). Fun info: "Understanding/comprehension." From "verstehen" (to understand). "Ich habe Verstandnis dafur" (I understand/sympathize). The -nis ending creates neuter abstract nouns: Ergebnis, Erlebnis, Verstandnis.
10. **teach:** Stimme(die). Fun info: "Voice." Also means "vote" (die Stimme abgeben = to cast a vote). From "stimmen" (to be correct/to tune). Your voice, your vote, your tuning fork. All one word.
11. **teach:** Gedanke(der). Fun info: "Thought." From "denken" (to think). "Gute Gedanken!" (Good thoughts!). Weak masculine noun: der Gedanke, den Gedanken, dem Gedanken. One of those N-Deklination words from U15.
12. **teach:** kritisieren. Fun info: "To criticize." From Kritik. The -ieren ending marks verbs borrowed from French or Latin. Always regular, always stressed on the -ier-. "Alles kritisieren" is a German stereotype.
13. **teach:** beleidigen. Fun info: "To insult/offend." "Beleidigung" (insult) is actually a crime in German law (Section 185 StGB). You can be fined for insulting someone. The German legal system takes words seriously.
14. **teach:** unterbrechen. Fun info: "To interrupt." Unter (under) + brechen (break). You break into someone's speech from below. Inseparable prefix: "Er unterbricht mich standig" (He constantly interrupts me).
15. **teach:** widersprechen. Fun info: "To contradict." Wider (against) + sprechen (speak). To speak against. Inseparable: "Ich widerspreche dir" (I contradict you). Takes dative. A polite argument verb.
16. **teach:** verlangen. Fun info: "To demand/require." "Das ist zu viel verlangt" (That is asking too much). Stronger than "bitten" (to ask). When your neighbor verlangen something, the Streit is escalating.
17. **teach:** behaupten. Fun info: "To claim/assert." "Er behauptet, dass..." (He claims that...). From "Haupt" (head). To hold your head up, to maintain a position. Essential for indirect speech and reported claims.
18. **teach:** frech. Fun info: "Cheeky/rude." "Sei nicht so frech!" (Do not be so rude!). Originally meant "brave/bold" in Old High German. The meaning shifted from courage to impudence over centuries.
19. **teach:** peinlich. Fun info: "Embarrassing." From "Pein" (pain/torment). Something peinlich causes social pain. "Das ist mir peinlich" (That embarrasses me). Drilling on a Sunday? Peinlich.
20. **teach:** merkwurdig. Fun info: "Strange/odd." Literally "worthy of noticing" (merken + wurdig). Originally positive: remarkable. Now mostly means weird. The shift from "noteworthy" to "odd" happened over centuries.
21. **teach:** seltsam. Fun info: "Strange/peculiar." From "selten" (rarely). Something that happens seldom is seltsam. Slightly softer than merkwurdig. "Ein seltsamer Nachbar" (A peculiar neighbor).
22. **mc:** Blame vocabulary. q: "The neighbor says it is Verumius's fault. Which sentence fits?" opts: [Er ist schuld., Er hat Geduld., Er hat Respekt., Er ist still.] ans: Er ist schuld. hint: "The predicative form that means 'at fault' uses sein."
23. **fb:** Complaint verb. s: "Der Nachbar {1}, dass Verumius zu laut war." a: ["behauptet"] opts: [behauptet, kritisiert, beleidigt, unterbricht] hint: "Which verb means to claim or assert something?"
24. **match:** Conflict pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Kritik",src:"criticism"},{trg:"Kompromiss",src:"compromise"},{trg:"Vorwurf",src:"accusation"},{trg:"Geduld",src:"patience"}]

## Lesson 3: "Die Zettel an der Wand" (The Notes on the Wall)

**Type:** Story development + grammar introduction
**Mode:** Bubble. Apartment stairwell, multiple passive-aggressive notes on notice board, ornate Altbau tiling
**Characters:** Verumius (confused), Hildi (authoritative, posting counter-note), Neighbor (door crack visible)
**Step count:** 24
**New vocabulary (20):** Geheimnis(das), Gewissen(das), Herausforderung(die), Kraft(die), Mut(der), Herz(das), Gefuhl(das), beruhigen, entspannen, einigen, entgegenkommen, uberzeugen, uberreden, loben, reichen, ehrlich, fair, tolerant, treu, dankbar
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: nachdem + Plusquamperfekt (#20). Tip card: tense sequence rule, verb-final in nachdem-clause. 3+ quiz steps.
**Communicative functions:** Mediate conflicts (taught), express feelings (taught), describe sequences of events (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,018**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Die Zettel an der Wand." Goals: Learn mediation and resolution vocabulary. Master nachdem + Plusquamperfekt for time sequences. Read the stairwell note war.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:humorous. "Nachdem Verumius den ersten Zettel gelesen hatte, fand er am nachsten Morgen drei neue Zettel an der Wand." / "After Verumius had read the first note, he found three new notes on the wall the next morning."
3. **teach:** Geheimnis(das). Fun info: "Secret." From "geheim" (secret, adjective). Das Geheimnis. The -nis ending creates neuter nouns. "Ein offenes Geheimnis" (an open secret) is one everyone knows but nobody says.
4. **teach:** Gewissen(das). Fun info: "Conscience." From "wissen" (to know). Your Gewissen is what you know inside. "Ein gutes Gewissen" (a good conscience). "Ein schlechtes Gewissen haben" (to have a guilty conscience). Verumius has one right now.
5. **teach:** Herausforderung(die). Fun info: "Challenge." Heraus (out) + Forderung (demand). A demand that pulls you out of comfort. 16 letters. Living in Germany as a foreigner is eine grose Herausforderung.
6. **teach:** Kraft(die). Fun info: "Strength/power/force." "Mit voller Kraft" (with full force). Also in compound: "Kraftwerk" (power plant, and the famous electronic band). Die Kraft is feminine, like most abstract -ft nouns.
7. **teach:** Mut(der). Fun info: "Courage." "Mut machen" (to encourage). Der Mut. "Mutig" (courageous). Verumius needs Mut to knock on the neighbor's door and apologize.
8. **teach:** Herz(das). Fun info: "Heart." Same root as English "heart," Latin "cor," Greek "kardia." "Von Herzen" (from the heart). "Herzlich" (heartfelt) is already in your vocabulary from U1. Das Herz is neuter.
9. **teach:** Gefuhl(das). Fun info: "Feeling/emotion." From "fuhlen" (to feel). Das Gefuhl, plural: Gefuhle. "Ein gutes Gefuhl haben" (to have a good feeling). The Ge- prefix + -l ending creates neuter collective nouns.
10. **teach:** beruhigen. Fun info: "To calm (someone) down." Be- + ruhig (quiet) + -en. "Beruhige dich!" (Calm down!). Reflexive: "sich beruhigen." The be- prefix makes it transitive: you calm SOMEONE.
11. **teach:** entspannen. Fun info: "To relax." Ent- (un-) + spannen (to tense). To un-tense. "Entspann dich!" (Relax!). Reflexive. "Entspannung" (relaxation) is a German wellness keyword.
12. **teach:** einigen. Fun info: "To agree/to unite." "Sich einigen" (to reach agreement). From "ein" (one). To become one mind. "Wir haben uns geeinigt" (We reached an agreement). Essential conflict resolution verb.
13. **teach:** entgegenkommen. Fun info: "To meet halfway/to accommodate." Entgegen (toward) + kommen (come). Separable: "Er kommt mir entgegen." Literally: someone comes toward you. Figuratively: compromise.
14. **teach:** uberzeugen. Fun info: "To convince." Uber (over) + zeugen (to witness/testify). To bring someone over to your testimony. "Ich bin uberzeugt" (I am convinced). Inseparable prefix.
15. **teach:** uberreden. Fun info: "To persuade/talk into." Uber + reden (talk). To talk someone over. Slightly manipulative compared to uberzeugen. "Er hat mich uberredet" (He talked me into it).
16. **teach:** loben. Fun info: "To praise." "Lob" (praise) is the noun. "Nicht geschimpft ist genug gelobt" (Not scolded is enough praised) is a Swabian saying. Germans praise sparingly; when they loben, they mean it.
17. **teach:** reichen. Fun info: "To suffice/to hand." Two meanings: "Das reicht!" (That is enough!) and "Kannst du mir das Salz reichen?" (Can you pass me the salt?). One verb, two everyday uses.
18. **teach:** ehrlich. Fun info: "Honest." From "Ehre" (honor). "Ehrlich gesagt..." (Honestly speaking...) begins many German confessions. "Ehrlich wahrt am langsten" (Honesty lasts longest) is the German proverb for "honesty is the best policy."
19. **teach:** fair. Fun info: Borrowed from English. Pronounced "fehr" in German. "Das ist nicht fair!" (That is not fair!). Used constantly in arguments. Germans adopted this word because "gerecht" felt too formal.
20. **teach:** tolerant. Fun info: Same spelling as English, from Latin "tolerare" (to endure). "Tolerant sein" (to be tolerant). Berlin prides itself on being tolerant. The apartment building is testing that claim.
21. **teach:** treu. Fun info: "Loyal/faithful." "Treu wie Gold" (faithful as gold). Same root as English "true." A treu friend, a treu dog, a treu neighbor who does not drill on Sundays.
22. **teach:** dankbar. Fun info: "Grateful." Dank (thanks) + -bar (able). "Ich bin dankbar" (I am grateful). The -bar suffix works like English "-able": dankbar, machbar (doable), essbar (edible).
23. **tip:** "nachdem + Plusquamperfekt: sequencing the past." nachdem = "after." The nachdem-clause uses Plusquamperfekt (hatte/war + Partizip II) because its event happened FIRST. The main clause uses Perfekt or Prateritum. Example: "Nachdem er den Zettel gelesen hatte, ging er zum Nachbarn." (After he had read the note, he went to the neighbor.) Verb goes to the END in the nachdem-clause. Think of nachdem as a time arrow: earlier event (Plusquamperfekt) then later event (Prateritum/Perfekt).
24. **fb:** nachdem clause. s: "Nachdem Verumius die Bohrmaschine abgestellt {1}, klopfte es an der Tur." a: ["hatte"] opts: [hatte, hat, war, ist] hint: "The nachdem-clause needs Plusquamperfekt. Which auxiliary + past participle?"

## Lesson 4: "Indirekte Fragen" (Indirect Questions)

**Type:** Grammar
**Mode:** Bubble. Learning mode, no scene
**Characters:** None (grammar instruction)
**Step count:** 24
**New vocabulary (20):** Diskussion(die), Beziehung(die), Freundschaft(die), Ehe(die), Ehepaar(das), Scheidung(die), Trennung(die), Schreck(der), Schrecken(der), Enttauschung(die), leiden, enttauschen, erschrecken, trennen, scheiden, furchten, hassen, langweilen, verzeihen, verzichten
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: Indirect questions (#21). Tip card: W-word + verb-final, ob for yes/no indirect. Polite forms: Konnten Sie mir sagen...? 3+ quiz steps. PRACTICED: nachdem + PQP (#20) recycled in quiz steps.
**Communicative functions:** Ask polite questions (taught), report what others said/asked (taught), express disappointment (taught)
**Cumulative vocab after: 2,038**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Indirekte Fragen." Goals: Master indirect questions with W-words and ob. Learn relationship and emotion vocabulary. Combine time sequences with reported speech.
2. **teach:** Diskussion(die). Fun info: "Discussion." From Latin "discussio" (examination). "Eine Diskussion fuhren" (to lead a discussion). More structured than a Streit. Germans value "sachliche Diskussion" (objective discussion).
3. **teach:** Beziehung(die). Fun info: "Relationship." From "beziehen" (to relate to). Covers romantic, professional, and international relationships. "In einer Beziehung sein" (to be in a relationship). Very versatile.
4. **teach:** Freundschaft(die). Fun info: "Friendship." Freund (friend) + -schaft (collective suffix, like English "-ship"). Germans distinguish sharply between Freunde (friends) and Bekannte (acquaintances). Freundschaft is earned, not given.
5. **teach:** Ehe(die). Fun info: "Marriage." One of the shortest German nouns. From Old High German "ewa" (law/eternity). "Die Ehe schliesen" (to enter marriage). "Ehe" also means "before" as a conjunction. Coincidence? Debatable.
6. **teach:** Ehepaar(das). Fun info: "Married couple." Ehe + Paar (pair). Das Ehepaar. The neighbor and his wife are ein Ehepaar. The compound tells you everything: a pair bonded by Ehe.
7. **teach:** Scheidung(die). Fun info: "Divorce." From "scheiden" (to separate). "Die Scheidung einreichen" (to file for divorce). Germany has a mandatory one-year separation period before divorce.
8. **teach:** Trennung(die). Fun info: "Separation." From "trennen" (to separate). Broader than Scheidung: any split. "Die Trennung von Kirche und Staat" (separation of church and state). Also: breaking up.
9. **teach:** Schreck(der). Fun info: "Fright/shock." "Einen Schreck bekommen" (to get a fright). Short, sharp, immediate. Der Schreck is the moment of shock. Related to "erschrecken."
10. **teach:** Schrecken(der). Fun info: "Horror/terror." Longer and deeper than Schreck. "Der Schrecken des Krieges" (the horror of war). Can also mean fright: "Zu meinem Schrecken..." (To my horror...).
11. **teach:** Enttauschung(die). Fun info: "Disappointment." Ent- (un-) + Tauschung (deception). To be un-deceived, to have illusions removed. German frames disappointment as losing a false belief. Philosophical.
12. **teach:** leiden. Fun info: "To suffer." Irregular: litt, gelitten. "Ich kann ihn nicht leiden" (I cannot stand him) is the common idiom. Also: "Leid tun" (to be sorry). "Es tut mir leid."
13. **teach:** enttauschen. Fun info: "To disappoint." From Enttauschung. "Du enttauschst mich" (You disappoint me). Inseparable prefix: ent- stays attached. Regular conjugation despite irregular-looking stem.
14. **teach:** erschrecken. Fun info: "To frighten / to be frightened." Two verbs in one. Transitive (frighten someone): regular. Intransitive (be frightened): irregular (erschrak, erschrocken). "Erschreck mich nicht!" (Do not scare me!).
15. **teach:** trennen. Fun info: "To separate." "Sich trennen" (to break up). Regular verb. "Mulltrennng" (waste separation) was in U8. Same verb, domestic and romantic contexts.
16. **teach:** scheiden. Fun info: "To divorce / to separate (formally)." Irregular: schied, geschieden. "Sich scheiden lassen" (to get divorced). The "lassen" construction adds formality. Legal and permanent.
17. **teach:** furchten. Fun info: "To fear." "Sich furchten vor" (to be afraid of). From "Furcht" (fear). "Furchtbar" (terrible, literally "fear-able"). Verumius furchtet sich vor dem nachsten Zettel.
18. **teach:** hassen. Fun info: "To hate." Strong word. "Ich hasse Larm" (I hate noise). Regular conjugation. Same root as English "hate." Germans use it seriously. "Hass" (hatred) is also a legal concept in Hassrede (hate speech).
19. **teach:** langweilen. Fun info: "To bore." "Sich langweilen" (to be bored). From "Langeweile" (boredom, literally "long-while"). "Der Film langweilt mich" (The film bores me). Reflexive for being bored, transitive for boring others.
20. **teach:** verzeihen. Fun info: "To forgive." Irregular: verzieh, verziehen. "Verzeihung!" (Forgiveness! = I am sorry). More formal than "Entschuldigung." "Konnen Sie mir verzeihen?" (Can you forgive me?). The peace-offering verb.
21. **teach:** verzichten. Fun info: "To give up / to do without." "Auf etwas verzichten" (to forgo something). "Ich verzichte auf mein Recht" (I waive my right). Takes auf + accusative. A conscious decision to let go.
22. **tip:** "Indirect questions: polite German." Direct: "Wo ist das Buro?" (Where is the office?). Indirect: "Konnen Sie mir sagen, wo das Buro ist?" The verb moves to the END after the W-word. For yes/no questions, use "ob": "Wissen Sie, ob er zu Hause ist?" (Do you know whether he is home?). Polite starters: "Konnen/Konnten Sie mir sagen, ...?", "Ich mochte wissen, ...", "Wissen Sie, ...?"
23. **mc:** Indirect question structure. q: "Which sentence correctly asks 'Can you tell me where the office is?'" opts: [Konnen Sie mir sagen, wo das Buro ist?, Konnen Sie mir sagen, wo ist das Buro?, Wo das Buro ist, konnen Sie mir sagen?, Sagen Sie mir, wo ist das Buro?] ans: Konnen Sie mir sagen, wo das Buro ist?. hint: "In indirect questions, the verb goes to the end of the clause."

15. **teach:** unheimlich. Fun info: "Uncanny/incredibly." Un- + heimlich (homely). What is not homely is creepy. Freud wrote about "das Unheimliche." In casual German: "unheimlich gut" (incredibly good). Same word: creepy AND intensifier.
16. **teach:** verliebt. Fun info: "In love." Past participle of "sich verlieben" (to fall in love). "Verliebt sein" (to be in love). "Frisch verliebt" (freshly in love). After the jam peace offering, Verumius and the neighbor are not verliebt, but perhaps they are friends.

19. **teach:** beschranken. Fun info: "To limit/restrict." "Sich beschranken auf" (to limit oneself to). "Beschrankt" can mean "limited" or "narrow-minded." "Bahnubergang beschrankt" (railroad crossing with barrier).
20. **teach:** entlassen. Fun info: "To release/dismiss/fire." Ent- + lassen (let). "Aus dem Krankenhaus entlassen" (discharged from hospital). "Entlassen werden" (to be fired). Context determines whether it is freedom or unemployment.
21. **teach:** bestrafen. Fun info: "To punish." Be- + Strafe + -en. "Jemanden bestrafen" (to punish someone). The be- prefix makes Strafe into a transitive verb. The Hausordnung exists to bestrafen violators. In theory.

Five powerful German affixes: miss- (wrong: Missverstandnis = misunderstanding), schwieger- (in-law: Schwiegermutter = mother-in-law), un- (not: unglaublich = unbelievable), vorbei- (past: vorbeigehen = to walk past), weg- (away: weggehen = to go away). These prefixes and combining forms multiply your vocabulary. Every word you know can be modified by these.
