**Grammar constructs:** Konj II past, Nominalisierung, Academic register

- Research: die Hypothese, die Methode, die Quelle, der Beleg, die Statistik, die Umfrage, die Stichprobe, der Zusammenhang, die Variable, das Ergebnis [V1 partial], die Studie [V1 partial]
- Academic writing: die Gliederung [V1], das Literaturverzeichnis, die Fussnote, das Zitat, zitieren, paraphrasieren, belegen [V1], widerlegen [V1], eroertern [V1], zusammenfassen [V1], argumentieren

### Key Findings

1. **V1 salvage covers ~200 of ~1,750 B2 words (11%)** -- Much less than B1 salvage because V1 B2 was phrase/grammar-heavy rather than vocabulary-heavy.

2. **Largest gaps:** U26 (Medienkritik), U30 (Amtsdeutsch), U27 (Der Vortrag) need the most new vocabulary because V1 had no dedicated media, legal, or presentation vocabulary units.

3. **Strongest salvage:** U29 (Die Debatte) benefits most from V1 U28's discourse connectors. U28 (Sprichwoerter) gets nearly complete proverb/idiom coverage from V1 U29.

4. **Total B2 word estimate: ~1,750-2,000** -- This is within the cross-level-allocation target of ~1,700-2,700, placing cumulative total at ~5,050-5,300 words (matches polyglot target of 5,000-6,000).

5. **No official word list constraint:** Unlike A1-B1 where Goethe word lists exist, B2 vocabulary must be derived from topic domains, exam preparation materials, and textbook analysis. Each unit's word list should be validated against B2 exam sample papers.
