## 4. Cross-Construct Dependency Chain

Each arrow indicates "must be taught before."

```
LEVEL 1 (Foundation — U1):
  Personal pronouns nom (#11) ──→ Present tense regular (#1)
  Present tense regular (#1) ──→ Present tense irregular (#2, needs conjugation concept)
  SVO word order (#16) ──→ W-question order (#17), Yes/No order (#18), V2 rule (#19)
  Formal/informal (#23) ──→ All verb conjugation (du vs Sie forms)

LEVEL 2 (Case system foundation — U2):
  Noun genders (#13) ──→ Definite articles (#8)
  Definite articles (#8) ──→ Indefinite articles (#9, same slot different forms)
  Nominative case (#6) ──→ Accusative case (#7, requires nom contrast)

LEVEL 3 (Building on cases — U3-U4):
  Nominative (#6) + Indefinite articles (#9) ──→ Possessive articles (#12, follow ein-pattern)
  Nominative (#6) + Indefinite articles (#9) ──→ Negative kein (#10, follow ein-pattern)
  Definite articles (#8) ──→ Demonstrativartikel (#27, follow der-pattern)
  Present tense regular (#1) ──→ Separable verbs (#4, adds prefix movement)
  Present tense regular (#1) ──→ Imperative (#5, derives from present stem)
  Accusative (#7) ──→ Prepositions + accusative (#20, case selection)
  Nominative (#6) ──→ Predicative adjectives (#24, requires sein which is nom-context)

LEVEL 4 (Advanced A1 — U5-U6):
  Present tense (#1) + V2 rule (#19) ──→ Modal verbs (#3, infinitive at end disrupts V2)
  Accusative (#7) ──→ Prepositions + dative basic (#21, case contrast essential)
  Present tense (#1) ──→ Simple past sein/haben (#22, first past-tense concept)
  Noun genders (#13) + Plurals (#14) ──→ Numbers + ordinals (#25, applied to dates/quantities)
  Prepositions + acc (#20) + dat (#21) ──→ Time expressions (#26, am/im/um patterns)

DEPENDENCY CHAIN INTO A2:
  Accusative (#7) + Dative basic (#21) ──→ Dative case full (A2 #5)
  Present tense (#1-2) + Simple past (#22) ──→ Perfekt (A2 #1-3)
  Separable verbs (#4) ──→ Separable in Nebensatz (A2 #11, prefix stays)
  V2 rule (#19) ──→ Verb-final in Nebensatz (A2 #11, V2 contrast)
  Modal verbs (#3) ──→ Prateritum modals (A2 #4)
  Nom + Acc cases (#6-7) ──→ Adjective declension nom/acc (A2 #14-15)
  Predicative adj (#24) ──→ Attributive adj contrast (A2 #14, the "why endings?" moment)
  Demonstrativartikel (#27) ──→ Verweisorter expanded (B2 #29)
  Kein (#10) + nicht (#15) ──→ Negation scope in Nebensatze (A2 #8-11)
```

## 7. Load Balancing Assessment

| Unit | New Constructs | Heaviness | Notes |
|------|---------------|-----------|-------|
| U1 | 9 (but most are simple: pronouns, word order, greetings) | MODERATE | High count but low complexity. Word order and pronouns are foundational and interlock. |
| U2 | 5 (article system + gender + predicative adj) | MODERATE | The der/die/das system is the first real grammar challenge. 5 constructs but they form one coherent block. |
| U3 | 4 (separable verbs, possessives, plurals, numbers) | MODERATE | Balanced. Separable verbs are the hardest new concept. |
| U4 | 6 (accusative, negation, demonstratives, imperative, acc prepositions) | HEAVY | Most grammar-dense unit. Accusative case is the major hurdle. Consider whether imperative could shift to U5. |
| U5 | 2 (modal verbs, dative prepositions basic) | LIGHT | Intentionally light to give modals room. Modal verb paradigm is complex (6 verbs x 6 persons). |
| U6 | 3 (past tense sein/haben, time expressions, ordinals) | MODERATE | Review-heavy unit. Past tense is the A1 capstone concept. |

U4 is the heaviest unit. This is intentional: the accusative case is the gateway to German grammar, and grouping it with negation (which requires acc knowledge for kein) and demonstratives (which follow the same declension pattern as definite articles) creates a coherent "case system" block. The imperative is lightweight and serves as relief within the unit.

If U4 proves too heavy during build, the imperative (#5) can shift to U5 without breaking any dependency chains.
