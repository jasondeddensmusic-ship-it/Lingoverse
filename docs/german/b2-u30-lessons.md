## Lesson 1: "Der Brief" (The Letter)

**Type:** Story intro
**Mode:** Cinematic. Verumius's apartment. A Finanzamt letter on the table. A single sentence that is 47 words long with 3 subordinate clauses and 2 participial constructions. Verumius stares at it like hieroglyphics.
**Characters:** Verumius (confused, bewildered)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r30l1
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (20):** Amt(das), Behoerde(die), Bescheid(der), Formular(das), Antrag(der), Frist(die)[V1], Genehmigung(die), Verwaltung(die), Vorschrift(die), Verordnung(die), Pflicht(die), Recht(das), Sachbearbeiter(der), Sachbearbeiterin(die), Buerger(der), Buergerin(die), amtlich, buerokratisch, gesetzlich, schriftlich
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Zustandspassiv #3 in the letter ("Das Verfahren ist eingeleitet", "Der Bescheid ist beigefuegt"). Subjektlose Passivsatze #5 in the letter ("Es wird darauf hingewiesen, dass...").
**Communicative functions:** Understanding official German documents, identifying bureaucratic vocabulary, navigating the German Amt system, recognizing formal written register
**Cumulative vocab after: ~4,168**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Der Brief." Goals: Enter the world of German bureaucracy. Learn core vocabulary for official documents. Understand why Amtsdeutsch is its own language.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:ominous. Verumius's apartment. Evening. A letter on the kitchen table. The envelope says "Finanzamt Berlin-Mitte." The paper is official, the font is small, and the sentence is... one. One single sentence. Forty-seven words long.
3. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:confused. Staring at the letter, mouth slightly open. "Das... ist ein Satz? Ein einziger Satz?"
4. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:comic. The sentence contains three subordinate clauses, two participial constructions, a genitive chain, and exactly zero comforting words. Somewhere in the middle, it says "Finanzamt." Somewhere near the end: "Frist." Those are the only words Verumius recognizes.
5. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:overwhelmed. "Ich habe eine Podiumsdiskussion auf Deutsch ueberlebt. Ich habe vor einem Publikum gesprochen. Aber diesen Satz? Diesen Satz verstehe ich nicht."
6. **teach:** Amt(das) -- Fun info: From Old High German "ambaht" (service). Das Amt. An office, an authority. "Finanzamt" (tax office), "Buergeramt" (citizens' office), "Ordnungsamt" (public order office). Germany has an Amt for everything. The -amt suffix is your clue: official government business.
7. **teach:** Behoerde(die) -- Fun info: From "behoeren" (to belong to). Die Behoerde. An authority, a government agency. "Die zustaendige Behoerde" (the responsible authority). More formal than Amt. A Behoerde is the institution. An Amt is the office where you go.
8. **teach:** Bescheid(der) -- Fun info: From "bescheiden" (to inform/decide). Der Bescheid. An official notice, a decision. "Steuerbescheid" (tax assessment notice). A Bescheid is legally binding. When a Behoerde sends you a Bescheid, it is an official act with legal force.
9. **teach:** Formular(das) -- Fun info: From Latin "formularium." Das Formular. A form. Germans fill out Formulare for everything: tax, residence, dog ownership, marriage, death. "Das Formular ausfuellen" (to fill out the form) is the national pastime of German bureaucracy.
10. **teach:** Antrag(der) -- Fun info: "An" (at/on) + "Trag" (carrying). Der Antrag. An application, a request. "Einen Antrag stellen" (to file an application). Everything in German bureaucracy begins with an Antrag. No Antrag, no action. The gateway document.
11. **teach:** Frist(die) -- Fun info: From Old High German "frist" (time period). Die Frist. A deadline, a time limit. "Die Frist einhalten" (to meet the deadline). Missing a Frist in German bureaucracy can cost money or rights. Take every Frist seriously.
12. **teach:** Genehmigung(die) -- Fun info: From "genehmigen" (to approve). Die Genehmigung. An approval, a permit. "Baugenehmigung" (building permit), "Aufenthaltsgenehmigung" (residence permit). Without Genehmigung, you cannot build, stay, or park. Germany runs on Genehmigungen.
13. **teach:** Verwaltung(die) -- Fun info: From "verwalten" (to manage/administer). Die Verwaltung. Administration. "Die oeffentliche Verwaltung" (public administration). The machinery that makes government work. Over 5 million people work in German Verwaltung.
14. **teach:** Vorschrift(die) -- Fun info: "Vor" (before/pre) + "Schrift" (writing). Die Vorschrift. A regulation, a rule. "Sicherheitsvorschriften" (safety regulations). Literally: what is written before (prescribed). Germany famously has Vorschriften for everything.
15. **teach:** Verordnung(die) -- Fun info: From "verordnen" (to decree). Die Verordnung. A decree, an ordinance. "Rechtsverordnung" (legal ordinance). More binding than a Vorschrift, less than a Gesetz. The middle layer of German law.
16. **teach:** Pflicht(die) -- Fun info: From Old High German "pfliht" (care/duty). Die Pflicht. Duty, obligation. "Pflichtversicherung" (compulsory insurance). "Pflicht" is deeply German: Kant's "Pflicht" is the foundation of moral philosophy. Duty before desire.
17. **teach:** Recht(das) -- Fun info: From Old High German "reht" (straight/right). Das Recht. Right, law. "Rechte und Pflichten" (rights and duties). Always paired in German legal thinking. You have Rechte, but they come with Pflichten.
18. **teach:** Sachbearbeiter(der) -- Fun info: "Sache" (matter) + "Bearbeiter" (processor). Der Sachbearbeiter. A case worker, a clerk. The person at the Behoerde who handles YOUR file. "Ihr zustaendiger Sachbearbeiter" (your assigned case worker). The human behind the bureaucracy.
19. **teach:** Sachbearbeiterin(die) -- Fun info: Sachbearbeiter + "-in." Die Sachbearbeiterin. The female form. Standard in all government correspondence. Official letters often use: "Ihr Sachbearbeiter / Ihre Sachbearbeiterin: Frau Mueller."
20. **teach:** Buerger(der) -- Fun info: From "Burg" (castle/town). Der Buerger. A citizen. Originally: someone who lives in a Burg (fortified town). "Buergerrechte" (civil rights). The fundamental identity in German democracy: you are a Buerger with rights.
21. **teach:** Buergerin(die) -- Fun info: Buerger + "-in." Die Buergerin. A female citizen. "Buergerinnen und Buerger" (citizens, m/f) is the standard opening in political speeches. Always both forms, always in that order.
22. **teach:** amtlich -- Fun info: "Amt" + "-lich." Official. "Amtliche Dokumente" (official documents). "Amtlich beglaubigt" (officially certified). The adjective that turns any piece of paper into a legally binding document.
23. **teach:** buerokratisch -- Fun info: From French "bureaucratique." Bureaucratic. "Der buerokratische Aufwand" (the bureaucratic burden). Germans both love and hate their Buerokratie. It works, but it is heavy. "Buerokratieabbau" (reducing bureaucracy) is every government's promise.
24. **teach:** gesetzlich -- Fun info: "Gesetz" (law) + "-lich." Legal, statutory. "Gesetzlich vorgeschrieben" (required by law). "Die gesetzliche Krankenversicherung" (statutory health insurance). When something is gesetzlich, there is no negotiating.
25. **teach:** schriftlich -- Fun info: "Schrift" (writing) + "-lich." In writing, written. "Bitte bestaetigen Sie schriftlich" (Please confirm in writing). In German bureaucracy, if it is not schriftlich, it did not happen. Oral agreements mean nothing to a Behoerde.
26. **mc:** Core vocabulary. q: "Verumius received a Bescheid from the Finanzamt. What is a Bescheid?" opts: [an official notice with legal force, a friendly letter, a form to fill out, an invitation] ans: an official notice with legal force. hint: "When a Behoerde sends you a Bescheid, it is a legally binding decision, not just information."
27. **fb:** Bureaucratic vocabulary. s: "Verumius muss den {1} bis zum 15. Juni einreichen." a: ["Antrag"] opts: [Antrag, Brief, Bescheid, Formular] hint: "The document you file to REQUEST something from a government office. The gateway to bureaucracy."
28. **mc:** Context. q: "Why is Verumius confused by the letter?" opts: [The single sentence is 47 words long with multiple subordinate clauses, He cannot read German at all, The letter is in English, The handwriting is illegible] ans: The single sentence is 47 words long with multiple subordinate clauses. hint: "He survived a public debate. But Amtsdeutsch has its own grammar: one sentence that never ends."

## Lesson 2: "Formulare und Fristen" (Forms and Deadlines)

**Type:** Vocab teach
**Mode:** Bubble. No scene. Pure vocabulary.
**Characters:** None (pure vocabulary)
**Step count:** 24
**id:** deu_r30l2
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (20):** Buergeramt(das), Finanzamt(das), Bauamt(das), Ordnungsamt(das), Rathaus(das), Meldebescheinigung(die), Aufenthaltserlaubnis(die), Auslaenderbehoerde(die), Steuererklarung(die), Bussgeld(das), Ordnungswidrigkeit(die), Mahnung(die), Einspruch(der), Widerspruch(der), Beschwerde(die)[V1], Bewilligung(die), befristet, unbefristet, fristgerecht, gueltig
**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Zustandspassiv in example sentences ("Die Meldebescheinigung ist ausgestellt", "Der Antrag ist genehmigt"). Genitive prepositions in bureaucratic phrases.
**Communicative functions:** Navigating German offices and agencies, understanding deadlines and permits, filing complaints and objections, recognizing time-bound legal language
**Cumulative vocab after: ~4,188**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Formulare und Fristen." Goals: Learn the names of German government offices. Understand permits, deadlines, and objection procedures. Build your bureaucratic survival kit.
2. **teach:** Buergeramt(das) -- Fun info: "Buerger" (citizen) + "Amt" (office). Das Buergeramt. The citizens' office. Your first stop after arriving in Germany: "Anmeldung" (registration). Every person in Germany must register their address. No registration, no life in Germany.
3. **teach:** Finanzamt(das) -- Fun info: "Finanz" (finance) + "Amt." Das Finanzamt. The tax office. Every German city has one. They send Bescheide, collect taxes, and terrify residents with 47-word sentences. "Ich muss zum Finanzamt" is a sentence nobody says with joy.
4. **teach:** Bauamt(das) -- Fun info: "Bau" (building/construction) + "Amt." Das Bauamt. The building office. Want to build a shed in your garden? Baugenehmigung from the Bauamt. Germany regulates construction at every level. The Bauamt decides what you can and cannot build.
5. **teach:** Ordnungsamt(das) -- Fun info: "Ordnung" (order) + "Amt." Das Ordnungsamt. The public order office. Noise complaints, illegal parking, unregistered dogs. The Ordnungsamt handles everything that disturbs public order. They can issue Bussgelder (fines).
6. **teach:** Rathaus(das) -- Fun info: "Rat" (council) + "Haus" (house). Das Rathaus. City hall. Where the mayor (Buergermeister) works and the city council meets. Every German city, no matter how small, has a Rathaus. Often the most beautiful building in town.
7. **teach:** Meldebescheinigung(die) -- Fun info: "Meldung" (registration) + "Bescheinigung" (certificate). Die Meldebescheinigung. A registration certificate. Proof that you registered your address. You need it for: bank accounts, phone contracts, work permits. The first German document.
8. **teach:** Aufenthaltserlaubnis(die) -- Fun info: "Aufenthalt" (stay/residence) + "Erlaubnis" (permission). Die Aufenthaltserlaubnis. A residence permit. For non-EU citizens, this document is life in Germany. Without it, you cannot work, rent, or stay. Applied for at the Auslaenderbehoerde.
9. **teach:** Auslaenderbehoerde(die) -- Fun info: "Auslaender" (foreigner) + "Behoerde" (authority). Die Auslaenderbehoerde. The foreigners' registration office. Now often called "Aufenthaltsbehoerde" (residence authority). Where non-EU citizens apply for and renew their residence permits.
10. **teach:** Steuererklarung(die) -- Fun info: "Steuer" (tax) + "Erklarung" (declaration). Die Steuererklarung. A tax return. "Die Steuererklarung abgeben" (to file a tax return). Germans who are required to file have until July 31 (or extended with a Steuerberater).
11. **teach:** Bussgeld(das) -- Fun info: "Busse" (penance/fine) + "Geld" (money). Das Bussgeld. A fine. "Ein Bussgeld zahlen" (to pay a fine). For traffic violations, late registrations, missing deadlines. The Ordnungsamt and Finanzamt both issue Bussgelder.
12. **teach:** Ordnungswidrigkeit(die) -- Fun info: "Ordnung" (order) + "widrigkeit" (contravention). Die Ordnungswidrigkeit. A regulatory offense (less than criminal). Parking wrong, skipping registration, making noise after 10pm. Not criminal, but you pay. A uniquely German legal category.
13. **teach:** Mahnung(die) -- Fun info: From "mahnen" (to remind/urge). Die Mahnung. A reminder, a dunning notice. "Erste Mahnung, zweite Mahnung, letzte Mahnung." Three chances. After the third, legal action. The escalation system of German payment culture.
14. **teach:** Einspruch(der) -- Fun info: "Ein" (in) + "Spruch" (statement). Der Einspruch. An objection, an appeal. "Einspruch erheben" (to raise an objection). Legal right: if you disagree with a Bescheid, you can Einspruch einlegen. But respect the Frist.
15. **teach:** Widerspruch(der) -- Fun info: "Wider" (against) + "Spruch" (statement). Der Widerspruch. A formal objection/contradiction. "Widerspruch einlegen" (to file an objection). Slightly broader than Einspruch. Used in administrative law. Your right to say: "I disagree officially."
16. **teach:** Beschwerde(die) -- Fun info: From "beschweren" (to burden/complain). Die Beschwerde. A complaint. "Eine Beschwerde einreichen" (to file a complaint). Less formal than Widerspruch. You can Beschwerde einreichen about service, but Widerspruch einlegen against a decision.
17. **teach:** Bewilligung(die) -- Fun info: From "bewilligen" (to grant/approve). Die Bewilligung. An approval, a grant. "Die Bewilligung erteilen" (to grant approval). Synonym for Genehmigung but often used for financial approvals (budget Bewilligung).
18. **teach:** befristet -- Fun info: From "befristen" (to set a deadline). Limited in time. "Ein befristeter Vertrag" (a fixed-term contract). "Befristete Aufenthaltserlaubnis" (temporary residence permit). The clock is always ticking.
19. **teach:** unbefristet -- Fun info: "Un-" + "befristet." Unlimited, permanent. "Ein unbefristeter Arbeitsvertrag" (a permanent employment contract). The holy grail of German work life. Security, stability, bank loans. Unbefristet means: no end date.
20. **teach:** fristgerecht -- Fun info: "Frist" (deadline) + "gerecht" (just/proper). On time, within the deadline. "Den Antrag fristgerecht einreichen" (to file the application on time). In German bureaucracy, fristgerecht is not a suggestion. It is survival.
21. **teach:** gueltig -- Fun info: From "gelten" (to be valid). Valid. "Der Ausweis ist noch gueltig" (The ID is still valid). "Bis wann ist das gueltig?" (Until when is this valid?). Everything in Germany has a Gueltigkeit (validity period).
22. **tip:** "The German Amt system." Germany has a three-tier government: Bund (federal), Land (state), Kommune (local). Each level has its own Aemter. Your Buergeramt is local. The Finanzamt is state-level. The Auslaenderbehoerde can be either. Rule of thumb: if it involves your address, go local. If it involves money, go state. If it involves foreigners, check your city. Always bring: Personalausweis or Reisepass, Meldebescheinigung, and patience.
23. **mc:** Office identification. q: "Verumius needs to register his new address. Which office does he visit?" opts: [Buergeramt, Finanzamt, Bauamt, Ordnungsamt] ans: Buergeramt. hint: "The citizens' office handles Anmeldung (registration). Every new address must be registered within 14 days."
24. **match:** Bureaucratic pairs. pairs: [{trg:"Meldebescheinigung",src:"registration certificate"},{trg:"Aufenthaltserlaubnis",src:"residence permit"},{trg:"Steuererklarung",src:"tax return"},{trg:"Bussgeld",src:"fine"}]
25. **fb:** Legal action. s: "Wenn Sie mit dem Bescheid nicht einverstanden sind, koennen Sie {1} einlegen." a: ["Widerspruch"] opts: [Widerspruch, Formular, Genehmigung, Mahnung] hint: "The formal objection you file when you disagree with an official decision."
26. **mc:** Time-bound vocabulary. q: "'Ein befristeter Vertrag laeuft am 31. Dezember aus.' What does befristet mean?" opts: [limited in time / fixed-term, unlimited, renewed, cancelled] ans: limited in time / fixed-term. hint: "Frist means deadline. Befristet means: a deadline has been set. There is an end date."

## Lesson 3: "Fuer Elise auf Endlosschleife" (Fuer Elise on Repeat)

**Type:** Story development + grammar intro
**Mode:** Bubble. Verumius's apartment, phone on speaker. Fuer Elise playing on loop. He has been on hold for 45 minutes. Doing stretches while waiting.
**Characters:** Verumius (resigned patience, stretching), Finanzamt automated system
**Step count:** 26
**id:** deu_r30l3
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (20):** Bearbeitungsgebuehr(die), Aktenzeichen(das), Bescheinigung(die), Eingangsbestaetigung(die), Erteilung(die), Zustellung(die), Verwaltungsakt(der), Sachverhalt(der), Rechtslage(die), Verfahren(das), Betrag(der), Laufzeit(die), Paragraph(der), Dienstweg(der), es handelt sich um, es ist davon auszugehen, es bedarf, es gilt, es sei darauf hingewiesen, gemaess
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: #3 Zustandspassiv (sein + Partizip II). Verb table contrasting Vorgangspassiv (wird gemacht) with Zustandspassiv (ist gemacht). Tip card on state vs. process passive. EXPOSED: #5 Subjektlose Passivsatze in hold-message language.
**Communicative functions:** Understanding automated bureaucratic language, reading official documents, distinguishing process from result in passive constructions, formal telephone procedures
**Cumulative vocab after: ~4,208**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Fuer Elise auf Endlosschleife." Goals: Survive German bureaucratic hold music. Master the Zustandspassiv (state passive). Decode the impersonal constructions of Amtsdeutsch.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:comic. Verumius's apartment. The phone is on speaker. Beethoven's Fuer Elise plays. Again. And again. The call timer shows 00:45:12. Verumius has moved from sitting to standing to stretching to yoga.
3. **story:** speaker:automated, mood:mechanical. "Bitte haben Sie noch einen Moment Geduld. Ihr Anruf ist uns wichtig. Sie werden mit dem naechsten freien Sachbearbeiter verbunden."
4. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:resigned. "Mein Anruf ist euch wichtig. Seit 45 Minuten."
5. **story:** speaker:automated, mood:mechanical. "Bitte beachten Sie: Das Aktenzeichen ist in allen Schreiben anzugeben. Die Frist ist am 15. Juni abgelaufen. Der Bescheid ist Ihnen zugestellt."
6. **teach:** Bearbeitungsgebuehr(die) -- Fun info: "Bearbeitung" (processing) + "Gebuehr" (fee). Die Bearbeitungsgebuehr. A processing fee. You pay the Behoerde for the privilege of them processing your Antrag. "Bearbeitungsgebuehr: 25 Euro." The price of bureaucracy.
7. **teach:** Aktenzeichen(das) -- Fun info: "Akte" (file) + "Zeichen" (sign/mark). Das Aktenzeichen. A file reference number. "Bitte geben Sie Ihr Aktenzeichen an." Every official letter has one. Lose it, and the Behoerde cannot find your case. Your bureaucratic identity.
8. **teach:** Bescheinigung(die) -- Fun info: From "bescheinigen" (to certify). Die Bescheinigung. A certificate, an attestation. "Arbeitgeberbescheinigung" (employer's certificate). Official paper that confirms something. More specific than Formular, less binding than Bescheid.
9. **teach:** Eingangsbestaetigung(die) -- Fun info: "Eingang" (receipt/entry) + "Bestaetigung" (confirmation). Die Eingangsbestaetigung. A confirmation of receipt. "Wir bestaetigen den Eingang Ihres Antrags." The bureaucracy telling you: we received your paper. Now wait.
10. **teach:** Erteilung(die) -- Fun info: From "erteilen" (to grant/issue). Die Erteilung. Issuance, granting. "Die Erteilung der Genehmigung" (the issuance of the permit). The formal noun for when the Behoerde says yes. Erteilung = you got it.
11. **teach:** Zustellung(die) -- Fun info: From "zustellen" (to deliver). Die Zustellung. Delivery (of official documents). "Die Zustellung des Bescheids" (the delivery of the notice). Legal term: the moment the document officially reaches you. Fristen start from Zustellung.
12. **teach:** Verwaltungsakt(der) -- Fun info: "Verwaltung" + "Akt" (act). Der Verwaltungsakt. An administrative act. Legal term for any official decision by a Behoerde. "Jeder Verwaltungsakt muss begruendet sein" (Every administrative act must be justified). The building block of German administrative law.
13. **teach:** Sachverhalt(der) -- Fun info: "Sache" (matter) + "Verhalt" (state of affairs). Der Sachverhalt. The facts of the matter, the circumstances. "Den Sachverhalt pruefen" (to examine the circumstances). Legal vocabulary: before deciding, establish the Sachverhalt.
14. **teach:** Rechtslage(die) -- Fun info: "Recht" (law) + "Lage" (situation). Die Rechtslage. The legal situation. "Die aktuelle Rechtslage" (the current legal situation). What the law says RIGHT NOW about your case. Can change if laws change.
15. **teach:** Verfahren(das) -- Fun info: From "verfahren" (to proceed). Das Verfahren. A procedure, a process, a legal proceeding. "Ein gerichtliches Verfahren" (a court proceeding). Also: "Verwaltungsverfahren" (administrative procedure). The formal path from Antrag to Bescheid.
16. **teach:** Betrag(der) -- Fun info: From "betragen" (to amount to). Der Betrag. An amount (of money). "Der zu zahlende Betrag" (the amount to be paid). Official letters always specify the Betrag: how much you owe, to the cent.
17. **teach:** Laufzeit(die) -- Fun info: "Lauf" (run) + "Zeit" (time). Die Laufzeit. Duration, term. "Die Laufzeit des Vertrags" (the duration of the contract). How long something runs. "Laufzeit: 12 Monate." The clock starts now.
18. **teach:** Paragraph(der) -- Fun info: From Greek "paragraphos" (written beside). Der Paragraph. A section of law. "Gemaess Paragraph 3 des Einkommensteuergesetzes..." The symbol: special section sign. Paragraphen are numbered, cited, and argued over.
19. **teach:** Dienstweg(der) -- Fun info: "Dienst" (service) + "Weg" (way/path). Der Dienstweg. Official channels. "Auf dem Dienstweg" (through official channels). The proper bureaucratic path. Skipping the Dienstweg is frowned upon. Follow the road.
20. **teach:** es handelt sich um -- Fun info: "It concerns" / "This is about." Impersonal construction. "Es handelt sich um eine Steuernachzahlung." The official way to introduce what a letter is about. Impersonal, distanced, institutional.
21. **teach:** es ist davon auszugehen -- Fun info: "It is to be assumed that." "Es ist davon auszugehen, dass der Bescheid korrekt ist." Bureaucratic certainty phrased as assumption. Translation: this is true until you prove otherwise.
22. **teach:** es bedarf -- Fun info: "It requires." + genitive. "Es bedarf einer schriftlichen Bestaetigung." The most formal way to say "you need to." Es bedarf + genitive is pure Amtsdeutsch. Beautiful, impersonal, non-negotiable.
23. **teach:** es gilt -- Fun info: "It applies" / "the following applies." "Es gilt die Frist zu beachten." Impersonal. What follows is law. "Es gilt" announces a rule. No subject needed. The rule exists independently of people.
24. **teach:** es sei darauf hingewiesen -- Fun info: "Attention is drawn to the fact that." Konjunktiv I passive. "Es sei darauf hingewiesen, dass die Frist am 15. ablaeuft." The politest possible threat. We are merely pointing out that your deadline is approaching.
25. **teach:** gemaess -- Fun info: From "Mass" (measure). In accordance with. + dative: "Gemaess Paragraph 5..." The preposition of legal conformity. What the law says, you do. Can precede or follow its noun.

27. **tip:** "Zustandspassiv: State vs. process." German has TWO passives. Vorgangspassiv (werden + Partizip II): the ACTION is happening. 'Der Antrag wird bearbeitet' (is being processed). Zustandspassiv (sein + Partizip II): the RESULT exists. 'Der Antrag ist bearbeitet' (has been processed, it is done).
