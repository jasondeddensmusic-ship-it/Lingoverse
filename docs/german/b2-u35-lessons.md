## Lesson 1: "Pruefungstag" (Exam Day)

**Type:** Story intro
**Mode:** Cinematic. Goethe-Institut Berlin, early morning. Verumius at the entrance. The building is familiar from B1 exam. Higher stakes.
**Characters:** Verumius (nervous, determined)
**Step count:** 24

**Grammar:** EXPOSED: Modalsaetze in Verumius's internal monologue ("Indem ich ruhig bleibe, schaffe ich das"). Indefinitpronomen in exam instructions ("Jede Aufgabe...").
**Communicative functions:** Discussing exam structure, expressing nervousness and determination, time management, understanding test instructions
**Cumulative vocab after: 4,909**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Pruefungstag." Goals: Enter the Goethe B2 exam. Learn exam vocabulary. Understand the four exam sections. Manage exam-day nerves.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:tense. Early morning. Berlin. The Goethe-Institut on Neue Schoenhauser Strasse. Verumius stands at the entrance, hands in his jacket pockets. He has been here before, for the B1 exam. Same door. Same steps. But today the certificate says B2.
3. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:nervous. "Pruefungstag. Wieder. Aber diesmal ist alles schwerer. Leseverstehen, Hoerverstehen, Schreiben, Sprechen. Vier Teile. Drei Stunden."
4. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:calm. He takes a deep breath. He thinks of Dr. Yilmaz: "Du schaffst das. Du hast hart gearbeitet." He thinks of Hildi correcting his Dativ for two years. He thinks of every Doner ordered, every form filled out, every misunderstanding survived.
5. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:determined. "Indem ich ruhig bleibe und systematisch arbeite, schaffe ich das. Ich bin vorbereitet."
6. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:quiet. He pushes through the glass doors. The exam room waits.
7. **teach:** Pruefungsteil(der) -- Fun info: "Pruefung" (exam) + "Teil" (part). An exam section. The Goethe B2 has four Pruefungsteile: Lesen, Hoeren, Schreiben, Sprechen. Der Pruefungsteil is masculine because Teil is masculine. Each Teil has its own time limit.
8. **teach:** Hoerverstehen(das) -- Fun info: "Hoeren" (to hear) + "Verstehen" (understanding). Listening comprehension. Das Hoerverstehen tests whether you can understand spoken German at natural speed. Radio interviews, announcements, conversations. Neuter because Verstehen is neuter.
9. **teach:** Leseverstehen(das) -- Fun info: "Lesen" (to read) + "Verstehen." Reading comprehension. Das Leseverstehen requires understanding newspaper articles, formal letters, and academic texts at B2 level. The hardest section for many candidates.
10. **teach:** Schreibaufgabe(die) -- Fun info: "Schreiben" (to write) + "Aufgabe" (task). A writing task. Die Schreibaufgabe at B2 level asks you to write a structured essay with introduction, arguments, and conclusion. 250+ words in 75 minutes.
11. **teach:** Sprachbaustein(der) -- Fun info: "Sprache" (language) + "Baustein" (building block). A language building block. The Sprachbausteine section tests grammar and vocabulary in context: fill-in-the-blank with connectors, prepositions, and verb forms. Der Sprachbaustein is masculine because Baustein is masculine.
12. **teach:** Textproduktion(die) -- Fun info: "Text" + "Produktion" (production). Text production, written output. Die Textproduktion. The formal term for "writing an essay." At B2 level, Textproduktion means argumentation, not just description.
13. **teach:** Pruefungsvorbereitung(die) -- Fun info: "Pruefung" + "Vorbereitung" (preparation). Exam preparation. Die Pruefungsvorbereitung. Every Goethe-Institut offers Pruefungsvorbereitung courses. The compound is long but each part is familiar.
14. **teach:** Pruefungsangst(die) -- Fun info: "Pruefung" + "Angst" (fear/anxiety). Exam anxiety. Die Pruefungsangst. A real psychological condition recognized in Germany. Even native speakers experience Pruefungsangst. "Angst" entered English directly from German.
15. **teach:** Bearbeitungszeit(die) -- Fun info: "Bearbeitung" (processing) + "Zeit" (time). Processing time, time allowed. Die Bearbeitungszeit. Every exam section has its Bearbeitungszeit printed at the top. Managing it is half the battle.
16. **teach:** bestehen -- Fun info: "Be-" + "stehen" (to stand). To pass (an exam), to endure. "Die Pruefung bestehen." Literally "to stand through." You stand firm against the test. Strong verb: bestehen/bestand/bestanden. "Bestanden!" is the best word on an exam certificate.
17. **teach:** durchlesen -- Fun info: "Durch" (through) + "lesen" (to read). To read through completely. "Lies die Aufgabe zuerst ganz durch." Separable. The exam strategy: durchlesen before answering. Read ALL of it first.
18. **teach:** markieren -- Fun info: From French "marquer." To mark, to highlight. "Schluesselwoerter markieren" (mark key words). The essential exam skill. Mark first, answer second. Every Pruefungsvorbereitung teaches this.
19. **teach:** korrigieren -- Fun info: From Latin "corrigere" (to correct). To correct, to proofread. "Den Text korrigieren" before submission. Korrekturlesen (proofreading) is the final exam step. Five minutes at the end saves stupid mistakes.
20. **teach:** strukturieren -- Fun info: From Latin "structura." To structure. "Den Aufsatz strukturieren" (to structure the essay). Einleitung, Hauptteil, Schluss. Introduction, body, conclusion. German examiners reward clear Struktur.
21. **teach:** fortgeschritten -- Fun info: "Fort" (forward/onward) + "geschritten" (stepped). Advanced. "Fortgeschrittene Deutschkenntnisse." B2 is the fortgeschritten level. You have stepped forward from the basics. Past participle used as adjective.
22. **teach:** pruefungsrelevant -- Fun info: "Pruefung" + "relevant." Exam-relevant. "Ist das pruefungsrelevant?" is the universal student question. If it is on the exam, it is pruefungsrelevant. If not, why study it?
23. **teach:** rechtzeitig -- Fun info: "Recht" (right/proper) + "zeitig" (timely). On time, punctually. "Rechtzeitig ankommen" is crucial on exam day. Not "puenktlich" (punctual), but "in proper time" with a margin. Germans arrive rechtzeitig, not just puenktlich.
24. **mc:** Exam structure. q: "The Goethe B2 exam has four Pruefungsteile. Which is NOT one of them?" opts: [Uebersetzen (translating), Lesen (reading), Hoeren (listening), Schreiben (writing)] ans: Uebersetzen (translating). hint: "The four parts are reading, listening, writing, and speaking. Translation is not tested."
25. **fb:** Exam strategy. s: "Lies die Aufgabe zuerst komplett {1}." a: ["durch"] opts: [durch, vor, ab, an] hint: "The separable prefix of 'durchlesen.' Read all the way THROUGH before answering."

## Lesson 2: "Wortfeld Pruefung" (Exam Word Field)

**Communicative functions:** Understanding exam formats, following written instructions, discussing assessment criteria
**Cumulative vocab after: 4,926**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Wortfeld Pruefung." Goals: Master the vocabulary of exams and assessments. Understand different task formats. Know what examiners look for.
2. **teach:** Aufgabenstellung(die) -- Fun info: "Aufgabe" (task) + "Stellung" (positioning). The task prompt, the question as formulated. Die Aufgabenstellung. Reading the Aufgabenstellung carefully is the first rule of exams. Misreading it costs more points than wrong answers.
3. **teach:** Bewertung(die) -- Fun info: "Bewerten" (to evaluate) + "-ung." An assessment, a grade, an evaluation. Die Bewertung. At B2 level, Bewertung considers content, structure, grammar, and vocabulary range. Four criteria, one grade.
4. **teach:** Kriterium(das) -- Fun info: From Greek "kriterion" (standard of judgment). A criterion. Das Kriterium, plural: Kriterien. "Nach welchen Kriterien wird bewertet?" (By which criteria is it graded?). Same word across European languages.
5. **teach:** Massstab(der) -- Fun info: "Mass" (measure) + "Stab" (rod/staff). A standard, a yardstick, a scale. "Einen hohen Massstab anlegen" (to apply a high standard). Literally a measuring rod. Der Massstab also means the scale on a map.
6. **teach:** Musterantwort(die) -- Fun info: "Muster" (sample/pattern) + "Antwort" (answer). A model answer. Die Musterantwort shows what a perfect response looks like. Goethe-Institut publishes Musterantworten for every exam level.
7. **teach:** Musterloesung(die) -- Fun info: "Muster" + "Loesung" (solution). A model solution. Similar to Musterantwort but for problem-solving tasks. Die Musterloesung. "Muster" means "pattern" here: the pattern others should follow.
8. **teach:** Loesungsblatt(das) -- Fun info: "Loesung" (solution) + "Blatt" (sheet). An answer sheet. Das Loesungsblatt. You write your final answers on the Loesungsblatt, not the question paper. Transfer carefully. Neuter because Blatt is neuter.
9. **teach:** Lueckentext(der) -- Fun info: "Luecke" (gap) + "Text." A gap-fill text, a cloze test. Der Lueckentext. You fill in the Luecken (gaps). The Sprachbausteine section is essentially a Lueckentext.
10. **teach:** Mindmap(die) -- Fun info: English borrowing. A visual diagram for brainstorming. Die Mindmap. German took the English word and made it feminine (possibly by analogy with "die Karte/Landkarte"). Used in Pruefungsvorbereitung to organize essay ideas.
11. **teach:** Aufgabentyp(der) -- Fun info: "Aufgabe" + "Typ" (type). A task type. Der Aufgabentyp. Multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, matching, free writing: each is an Aufgabentyp. Knowing the Aufgabentyp tells you what strategy to use.
12. **teach:** Auswahlaufgabe(die) -- Fun info: "Auswahl" (selection/choice) + "Aufgabe." A multiple-choice task. Die Auswahlaufgabe. You choose from given options. Tip: eliminate wrong answers first, then choose among the remaining.
13. **teach:** Grammatikuebung(die) -- Fun info: "Grammatik" + "Uebung" (exercise). A grammar exercise. Die Grammatikuebung. The Sprachbausteine section is a Grammatikuebung in disguise: testing grammar through context, not rules.
14. **teach:** Uebungsaufgabe(die) -- Fun info: "Uebung" (exercise) + "Aufgabe." A practice task. Die Uebungsaufgabe. Doing many Uebungsaufgaben before the exam is the key to success. Practice makes the patterns automatic.
15. **teach:** ausfuellen -- Fun info: "Aus" (out/completely) + "fuellen" (to fill). To fill out, to complete (a form). "Das Formular ausfuellen." Separable. On the exam: "Fuellen Sie das Loesungsblatt aus." The most common instruction.
16. **teach:** ankreuzen -- Fun info: "An" (on) + "kreuzen" (to cross). To mark with an X, to tick. "Kreuzen Sie die richtige Antwort an." Separable. The physical act of marking your answer on paper. Even digital exams use "ankreuzen" metaphorically.
17. **teach:** sichten -- Fun info: From "Sicht" (sight/view). To review, to sort through, to scan. "Material sichten" (to review material). More formal than "ansehen." Implies systematic examination of documents or texts.
18. **teach:** sortieren -- Fun info: From French "sortir" (to sort out). To sort, to organize. "Die Ideen sortieren" (to sort the ideas). Before writing the essay: brainstorm, then sortieren. Order from chaos.
19. **mc:** Exam format. q: "A Lueckentext is..." opts: [a gap-fill text where you complete missing words, a text you must translate, a text you must read aloud, a text you must summarize] ans: a gap-fill text where you complete missing words. hint: "Luecke means gap. **Type:** Story development + grammar intro
**Mode:** Bubble. Exam room. Verumius writing the Schreiben section. Internal monologue interleaved with grammar.
**Characters:** Verumius (concentrated, writing)
**Step count:** 26

**New vocabulary (17):** Argumentation(die), Einleitung(die), Hauptteil(der), Schluss(der), Fazit(das), Textsorte(die), Satzbau(der), Ausdruck(der), Zusammenfassung(die), nacherzaehlen, paraphrasieren, zusammenfassen, ueberarbeiten, umformulieren, gliedern, herausarbeiten, schriftlich
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: #15 Modalsaetze (indem, dadurch dass, ohne dass, anstatt dass). Tip card on manner clauses. EXPOSED: #30 Indefinitpronomen in exam texts.
**Communicative functions:** Structuring arguments, writing formal essays, paraphrasing, expressing manner and means
**Cumulative vocab after: 4,943**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Indem du schreibst." Goals: Master Modalsaetze (manner clauses). Structure a B2-level essay. Learn the language of argumentation.
2. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:focused. The Schreiben section. 75 minutes. The prompt: "Sollen Schulen mehr digitale Medien im Unterricht einsetzen? Nehmen Sie Stellung." Verumius reads it twice. He breathes. He has practiced this.
3. **story:** speaker:verumius, mood:thinking. "Einleitung, Hauptteil, Schluss. Indem ich klar strukturiere, bekomme ich gute Punkte. Dadurch dass ich Beispiele gebe, wird mein Text ueberzeugend."
4. **story:** speaker:narrator, mood:calm. He begins. The pen moves. Two years of German pour onto the page. Compound sentences, Konjunktiv II, connectors. The essay takes shape.
5. **teach:** Argumentation(die) -- Fun info: From Latin "argumentatio." Argumentation, making a case. Die Argumentation. At B2 level, you must build a logical Argumentation: claim, evidence, counterargument, conclusion. Not just opinions, but structured reasoning.
6. **teach:** Einleitung(die) -- Fun info: "Ein" (in/into) + "Leitung" (leading). An introduction. Die Einleitung leads the reader INTO the topic. A good Einleitung states the topic, gives context, and hints at your position. Two to three sentences.
7. **teach:** Hauptteil(der) -- Fun info: "Haupt" (main) + "Teil" (part). The main body. Der Hauptteil contains your arguments, examples, and counterarguments. The longest section. "Im Hauptteil entwickle ich meine Argumentation."
8. **teach:** Schluss(der) -- Fun info: From "schliessen" (to close). A conclusion, an ending. Der Schluss. "Zum Schluss" (in conclusion) is how you signal the end. A good Schluss summarizes and takes a final position. Not just a repeat of the Einleitung.
9. **teach:** Fazit(das) -- Fun info: From Latin "facit" (it makes/amounts to). A conclusion, a summary judgment. Das Fazit. More formal than Schluss. "Mein Fazit ist..." (My conclusion is...). The bottom line of your argument.
10. **teach:** Textsorte(die) -- Fun info: "Text" + "Sorte" (type/kind). A text type, a genre. Die Textsorte. Essay, letter, report, email: each is a Textsorte with its own conventions. The exam tells you which Textsorte to write.
11. **teach:** Satzbau(der) -- Fun info: "Satz" (sentence) + "Bau" (construction). Sentence structure. Der Satzbau. German Satzbau with its verb-second and verb-final rules is what makes the language unique. Good Satzbau = good grade.
12. **teach:** Ausdruck(der) -- Fun info: "Aus" (out) + "Druck" (pressing). Expression, phrasing. Der Ausdruck. "Einen guten Ausdruck haben" (to express yourself well). Examiners grade Ausdruck: your choice of words and phrases. Precision matters.
13. **teach:** Zusammenfassung(die) -- Fun info: "Zusammen" (together) + "Fassung" (version/grasp). A summary. Die Zusammenfassung brings everything together into a compressed version. "Eine Zusammenfassung schreiben" is a core B2 skill.
14. **teach:** nacherzaehlen -- Fun info: "Nach" (after/re-) + "erzaehlen" (to tell). To retell, to narrate after hearing. "Den Text nacherzaehlen." Separable. You hear or read something, then nacherzaehlen it in your own words.
15. **teach:** paraphrasieren -- Fun info: From Greek "paraphrasis" (restatement). To paraphrase. "Den Satz paraphrasieren." Same word internationally. The B2 skill of saying the same thing differently. Essential for avoiding plagiarism.
16. **teach:** zusammenfassen -- Fun info: "Zusammen" + "fassen" (to grasp). To summarize. "Den Text zusammenfassen." Separable. Literally: to grasp things together into one. The verb form of Zusammenfassung.
17. **teach:** ueberarbeiten -- Fun info: "Ueber" (over) + "arbeiten" (to work). To revise, to rework. "Den Text ueberarbeiten." Not separable (inseparable ueber-). To go OVER your work again and improve it. First draft, then ueberarbeiten.
18. **teach:** umformulieren -- Fun info: "Um" (re-/around) + "formulieren" (to formulate). To rephrase, to reformulate. "Den Satz umformulieren." Separable. When your sentence sounds clunky: umformulieren. Change the form while keeping the meaning.
19. **teach:** gliedern -- Fun info: From "Glied" (limb/member/section). To structure, to divide into sections. "Den Text in Abschnitte gliedern." Gliederung (outline) comes from this. Like a body divided into its limbs, a text divided into its parts.
20. **teach:** herausarbeiten -- Fun info: "Heraus" (out) + "arbeiten" (to work). To work out, to highlight, to bring to light. "Die Hauptaussage herausarbeiten" (to work out the main message). Separable. You dig the meaning OUT through effort.
21. **teach:** schriftlich -- Fun info: "Schrift" (writing/script) + "-lich." Written, in writing. "Schriftliche Pruefung" (written exam). The opposite of "muendlich" (oral). "Bitte schriftlich bestaetigen" (please confirm in writing).
22. **tip:** "Modalsaetze: How to say HOW something is done." Modalsaetze answer the question: "Auf welche Weise?" (In what way?). Key connectors: "indem" (by doing X). "Dadurch dass" (through the fact that). "ohne dass" (without doing). "anstatt dass" (instead of doing). Examples: "Indem ich ueberarbeite, verbessere ich den Text." (By revising, I improve the text.) "Er besteht die Pruefung, ohne dass er nervoes wird." (He passes the exam without getting nervous.) "Anstatt dass er aufgibt, arbeitet er weiter." (Instead of giving up, he keeps working.) Position: the verb goes to the END of the dass-clause. "Indem" is the most common and most versatile.

## Lesson 4: "Saemtliche Aufgaben" (All the Tasks)

**Type:** Grammar deep dive
**Mode:** Bubble. Grammar instruction with exam context.
**Characters:** None (grammar instruction)
**Step count:** 26
**id:** deu_r35l4
**board:** true
**New vocabulary (17):** saemtliche, jegliche, irgendein, irgendwelche, manch ein, jedweder, allerhand, allerlei, Hauptaussage(die), Schluesselwort(das), Absatz(der), Ueberschrift(die), Globalverstehen(das), Detailverstehen(das), erkennen, erschliessen, einordnen
**Grammar:** TAUGHT: #30 Indefinitpronomen expanded (saemtliche, jegliche, irgendein, jedweder, etc.). Tip card on formal indefinite pronouns. PRACTICED: #15 Modalsaetze (3+ quiz steps recycled).
**Communicative functions:** Understanding formal written registers, identifying main ideas, classifying information, working with reading comprehension
**Cumulative vocab after: 4,960**

### Step sequence:
1. **intro:** "Saemtliche Aufgaben." Goals: Master expanded Indefinitpronomen for formal texts. Understand reading comprehension strategies. Build academic vocabulary.
2. **teach:** saemtliche -- Fun info: From "Samt" (together with). All, every single one (emphatic). "Saemtliche Aufgaben muessen bearbeitet werden." More emphatic than "alle." Used in formal and legal language. Declines like a regular adjective.
3. **teach:** jegliche -- Fun info: From "jeglich" (any/every). Any whatsoever, all manner of. "Jegliche Hilfsmittel sind verboten." Stronger than "jede." The exam instruction that bans everything. Formal, slightly old-fashioned.
4. **teach:** irgendein -- Fun info: "Irgend" (some-) + "ein" (a/an). Some (unspecified), any. "Hast du irgendeine Idee?" The vaguest possible article. It could be any one. Declines like "ein": irgendeinen, irgendeiner, irgendeinem.
