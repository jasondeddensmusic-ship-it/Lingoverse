## Methodology Notes

**What counts as "taught":**
- A word is taught if it appears in a `teach` card's `trg` field in an earlier step of the same lesson, OR in a prior lesson within the same unit, OR in a prior unit (A1/A2 units 1-12, loaded from `existing-v2-vocab.txt`).
- `tip`, `verb_table`, `story`, and `intro` cards do NOT count as teaching.
- Component words of multi-word teach phrases (e.g., "die Bewerbung" teaches "Bewerbung") are also considered taught.

**What is excluded from violation checking:**
- English-language MC answers (translation questions — "What does X mean?").
- Grammar metalinguistic terms (Präteritum, Konjunktiv, Genitiv, etc.).
- Ellipsis constructions (`um...zu`, `ohne...zu`, `Worauf...darauf`).
- Questions ending in `?` and declarative sentences ending in `.`.
- 2- and 3-word comprehension sentences starting with pronouns, articles, or prepositions.
- Compound verb tense examples (`hatte gesehen`, `wird bleiben`, etc.).
- Da-compounds and wo-compounds (`daran`, `darüber`, `worauf` = grammar forms of taught prepositions).
- Article-stripped nouns (`des Mannes` → `Mann` is taught).
- Noun genitive/plural forms (`Kollegen` → `Kollege` is taught).
- Adjective declension forms (`guter` → `gut` is taught).
- Partizip I adjective forms (`fehlenden` → `fehlen` is taught).
- Partizip II of taught verbs (`geplant` → `planen` is taught).
- Present-tense conjugations including umlaut forms (`lässt` → `lassen` is taught).
- Präteritum of taught verbs (`arbeitete` → `arbeiten` is taught).
- Modal conjugations (`muss`, `kann`, `sollte` → base modal is taught).
- Konjunktiv II forms (`würde`, `wäre`, `hätte` → base auxiliaries).
- Compound nouns where both component parts are taught.
- Separable verb forms of taught base verbs (`aufmachen` → `machen` is taught).
- `zu`-infinitive constructions (`mitzuteilen` → `teilen` is taught).
- Parenthetical dialect notes (`Möhre (North)` = comprehension, not vocab).

**Grammar Form notes** are NOT violations but require review: these are inflected forms of taught words tested in grammar exercises. PP52 strictly requires the exact form to have its own teach card, but many grammar exercises test morphological forms where the base was taught.

**Cumulative taught set** grows as units are processed: U13 → U14 → ... → U24. A word taught in U13 is available for U14-U24.
